## 1. <u>Introduction and Nature of the Service</u>

### 1.1 <u>What WorldCup26 is</u>

WorldCup26 is a <u>fantasy football (association football) game of skill</u> built around the FIFA World Cup 2026 tournament. A participant uses knowledge, judgement, and analysis to <u>select three (3) national teams</u> for a single contest. Those teams then <u>accumulate points based on the real, official, real-time performance of those teams</u> in the tournament, measured against a fixed and publicly-published scoring system. A participant's standing on the public leaderboard is the sum of the points earned by their three selected teams.

WorldCup26 is, in plain terms, a <u>fantasy-sports competition</u>: the participant assembles a small "squad" of teams and competes against other participants to achieve the highest skill-based score over the course of the tournament.

### 1.2 <u>Subscription model — one subscription, one entry</u>

WorldCup26 operates on a <u>subscription / single-entry model</u>. A participant pays a <u>fixed, one-time subscription</u> (referred to in the application as an "entry ticket") which entitles them to <u>one (1) single entry</u> into the contest. 

- There is <u>**one** entry per subscription</u>.
- There are <u>**no** repeat stakes, no rolling wagers, and no "double-or-nothing" mechanics</u>.
- The subscription price is <u>fixed and disclosed in advance</u> and is identical for every participant.
- Once an entry is created and locked, the participant simply <u>follows the tournament</u>; there is nothing further to stake, bet, or risk.

### 1.3 <u>What WorldCup26 is NOT</u>

For the avoidance of doubt, and to assist payment and banking partners in correctly classifying the business, WorldCup26 is expressly <u>**not**</u> the following:

- It is <u>**not** gambling</u> and not a game of chance.
- It is <u>**not** a sportsbook</u> and does not offer fixed-odds or any odds-based betting.
- It is <u>**not** a betting exchange</u> and does not match wagers between users.
- It does <u>**not** accept multiple bets, accumulators, parlays, or in-play wagers</u>.
- It does <u>**not** offer casino, slots, lottery, bingo, or any chance-based product</u>.
- The Operator does <u>**not** act as a bookmaker</u>, does not "lay" outcomes, and holds <u>no position against the participant</u>.

### 1.4 <u>Why WorldCup26 is a game of skill</u>

WorldCup26 is a <u>predominantly skill-based competition</u> for the following reasons:

1. <u>Fixed, published rules.</u> The scoring formula, team coefficients, stage coefficients, prize structure, and paid places are <u>all defined and published before the tournament begins</u> and are <u>not changed once play starts</u>. Every participant competes under identical, known rules.
2. <u>Outcome determined by knowledge and judgement.</u> Success depends on the participant's analysis of team strength, form, draw difficulty, and tournament progression — not on a random draw, spin, or generator.
3. <u>No wager against the house.</u> The participant does not bet against the Operator or any counterparty. The subscription is an <u>entry fee into a skill competition</u>, not a stake on an outcome.
4. <u>Common pool, ranked result.</u> All participants are ranked on a single leaderboard; prizes are awarded to the top-ranked participants by skill, in the manner of a tournament or competition.

### 1.5 <u>Real-time data basis</u>

Player scores are calculated <u>exclusively from official, real-time tournament data</u> — match results, goals, clean sheets, and stage progression — as published for the FIFA World Cup 2026. Points are applied <u>automatically after each relevant match</u> using the fixed scoring rules in Section 3. The Operator does not set, influence, or trade on these results; it merely <u>ingests the official real-time data and applies the published formula</u>.

## 3. <u>Game Rules</u>

### 3.1 <u>Eligibility</u>

To take part you must:

- be <u>at least 18 years of age</u>;
- sign in with <u>your own genuine Google account</u>;
- participate only from a jurisdiction where doing so is <u>lawful for you</u>; and
- operate <u>only one account</u> — operating multiple accounts to gain an advantage is prohibited.

### 3.2 <u>Creating an entry — selecting three teams</u>

Each valid Entry consists of <u>exactly three (3) national teams</u> chosen by the participant. An Entry can be finalised (locked) <u>only when exactly three teams are selected</u>. The participant's total score is:

```text
user_total_points =
    selected_team_1_points
  + selected_team_2_points
  + selected_team_3_points
```

### 3.3 <u>Team lock / cut-off</u>

A team remains <u>selectable until shortly before its first World Cup 2026 match begins</u>, after which it locks and can no longer be added to a new Entry. Late participants may still join after the tournament has started, provided <u>every team they select is still open</u>. A team becomes unavailable for new Entries once its <u>second group-stage match starts</u> — so a late entrant may pick a team that has played only its first group match, but may not pick a team whose second group match has already begun. This rule exists to keep the competition <u>fair and consistent for all participants</u>.

### 3.4 <u>Scoring formula</u>

Each team's points for a match are calculated as:

```text
final_points = base_points × team_coefficient × stage_coefficient
```

where:

```text
base_points = result_base_points + goal_bonus_points + clean_sheet_bonus_points
```

### 3.5 <u>Base points</u>

| Result | Base Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Qualification in 90 minutes | 5 |
| Qualification after extra time | 4 |
| Qualification after penalties | 3 |
| Loses after extra time | 1 |
| Loses after penalties | 1.5 |
| Loses in 90 minutes | 0 |
| Group-stage win | 5 |
| Group-stage draw | 2 |
| Group-stage loss | 0 |
| Goal scored bonus | +0.5 per goal |
| Clean sheet in 90 minutes | +1 |

A <u>clean sheet</u> means the team concedes no goals in the first 90 minutes of play.

### 3.6 <u>Team coefficients</u>

Each team carries a <u>fixed coefficient</u>, set before the tournament and never changed once play begins. The strongest favourites carry a coefficient of <u>1.00</u>; the biggest underdogs carry up to <u>3.00</u>. Selecting an underdog therefore carries a higher reward multiplier — a <u>skill-and-judgement trade-off</u> for the participant.

| Team (example) | Coefficient |
| --- | ---: |
| Spain | 1.00 |
| France | 1.00 |
| England | 1.12 |
| Brazil | 1.19 |
| Japan | 1.86 |
| Ghana | 2.47 |
| Australia | 2.73 |
| Curaçao | 3.00 |
| Panama | 3.00 |

### 3.7 <u>Stage coefficients</u>

| Stage | Coefficient |
| --- | ---: |
| Group Stage | 1.00 |
| Round of 32 | 1.20 |
| Round of 16 | 1.35 |
| Quarter-final | 1.50 |
| Semi-final | 1.75 |
| Third-place Match | 1.25 |
| Final | 2.00 |

### 3.8 <u>Worked examples</u>

<u>Example 1 — Curaçao wins a group match 2–0:</u>

```text
base_points  = 5 + (2 × 0.5) + 1 = 7
final_points = 7 × 3.00 × 1.00 = 21
```

<u>Example 2 — Spain wins the Final 2–0:</u>

```text
base_points  = 5 + (2 × 0.5) + 1 = 7
final_points = 7 × 1.00 × 2.00 = 14
```

### 3.9 <u>Leaderboard and ranking</u>

The public Leaderboard ranks every Entry by <u>total accumulated points</u>, shown in Pick 1 / Pick 2 / Pick 3 order with each team's point contribution. Ranking determines prize eligibility under Section 4.6.

### 3.10 <u>Data sources</u>

Scoring is derived from the <u>official FIFA World Cup 2026 schedule and results</u> and the published 104-match fixture list. Team reward coefficients are derived from public pre-tournament market baselines and are <u>fixed for the whole event</u> so the game remains fair after kick-off.
