## Skills and Strengths

### Versatility
Havertz is one of the most positionally flexible attacking players in world football. He can operate as a centre-forward, a number 10, a false 9, or even drop into deeper midfield positions. This versatility has made him invaluable for both club and country, allowing managers to deploy him wherever he is needed most.

### Composure in Big Moments
Havertz has a remarkable habit of delivering in the biggest matches. His goal in the 2021 Champions League final for Chelsea against Manchester City is the defining moment of his career so far. He has also scored in a European Championship, a World Cup, and multiple FA Cup and League Cup finals. The bigger the stage, the more Havertz seems to thrive.

### Technical Quality
At 1.93 m, Havertz possesses an unusually soft first touch and elegant ball control for a player of his size. He can receive the ball under pressure, turn, and play forward in one fluid motion. His left foot is cultured, capable of precise passes, delicate chips, and powerful strikes.

### Intelligent Movement
Havertz's movement off the ball is among the best in the Premier League. He times his runs into the box perfectly, often arriving unmarked to finish chances. His understanding of space and timing allows him to create chances for himself and others without needing explosive pace.

### Aerial Ability
Standing at 6'4", Havertz is a significant aerial threat. He scores headers from crosses and is effective at winning flick-ons and knock-downs. This makes him an alternative option to Woltemade when Nagelsmann wants a more technical approach up front.

## Funny and Interesting Facts

### 1. The Champions League Final Goal That Changed Everything
On May 29, 2021, Kai Havertz scored the only goal in the Champions League final against Manchester City -- the biggest goal of his life. What most people don't know is that he almost didn't start the match. Thomas Tuchel had been considering other options but ultimately went with his gut. Havertz later admitted that he barely remembered the goal itself: "I just remember the ball hitting the net and then everything went blank. The next thing I knew, my teammates were on top of me." The goal was particularly sweet because Havertz had endured a difficult first season at Chelsea, with fans and media questioning whether the 71-million-pound signing was worth the investment. One goal in the Champions League final silenced every critic. He keeps the match-worn shirt from that game framed at home.

### 2. The Dog Who Went Viral
Havertz is a devoted dog owner, and his dog, a Golden Retriever named Baloo, has become something of a celebrity in his own right. Baloo has his own Instagram fan account (run by fans, not Havertz) with tens of thousands of followers. After Havertz scored the Champions League final goal, a photo of Baloo wearing a Chelsea scarf went viral. When Havertz transferred to Arsenal, fans photoshopped Baloo into an Arsenal shirt. Havertz has joked that Baloo gets more attention than he does when they walk through London. The dog has become such a beloved figure that Arsenal's social media team once featured Baloo in a promotional video, to the delight of fans worldwide.

### Bonus Facts

- Havertz was the youngest player to reach 50 Bundesliga appearances, a record later broken by Florian Wirtz.
- He made his Bayer Leverkusen debut at age 17, becoming the club's youngest ever debutant in the Bundesliga.
- His 43 Premier League goals for Arsenal have made him one of the highest-scoring German players in Premier League history.
