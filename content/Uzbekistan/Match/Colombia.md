# Uzbekistan vs Colombia — Group K, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

First-ever meeting between Uzbekistan and Colombia in official competition.

---

## The Storyline: Central Asia vs South America

Uzbekistan's World Cup journey concludes against one of South America's most gifted teams. Colombia's attacking flair — Luis Diaz, James Rodriguez, Jhon Duran — represents the kind of football that Uzbekistan has only seen on television.

But the White Wolves have surprised everyone by qualifying. They didn't come to the World Cup just to participate — they came to compete. Against Colombia, Uzbekistan will need to be at their organized, disciplined best.

For Colombia, this match is about securing their path to the knockout rounds. Lorenzo will demand a professional, efficient performance. But if Uzbekistan fights with the spirit that got them here, it won't be easy.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Eldor Shomurodov vs Davinson Sanchez
Shomurodov's Serie A experience against Sanchez's defensive quality. Both know Italian football — this will be a tactical battle.

### Luis Diaz vs Farrukh Sayfiev
Diaz's explosive pace against Sayfiev's defensive discipline. If Diaz gets running, there's no catching him.

### James Rodriguez vs Husniddin Aliqulov
James' vision and passing range against Aliqulov's midfield work rate. Stopping James is nearly impossible — slowing him down is Uzbekistan's only option.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Uzbekistan (4-2-3-1):** Maximum defensive discipline. Deep block, narrow lines, and counter-attacks through Shomurodov and Fayzullaev. Set pieces are the best hope for a historic result.

**Colombia (4-3-3):** Express themselves in attack. James Rodriguez orchestrates, Luis Diaz and Jhon Arias provide width, and Jhon Duran offers power in the box. Colombia will dominate possession and territory.

---

## X-Factor

**James Rodriguez's World Cup magic.** The man who lit up the 2014 tournament has a special relationship with the World Cup. Even at 34, he can produce a moment of genius that changes everything.
