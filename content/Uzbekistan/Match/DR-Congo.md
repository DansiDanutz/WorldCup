# Uzbekistan vs DR Congo — Group K, Matchday 2

**Date:** June 17, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

First-ever meeting between Uzbekistan and DR Congo in official competition.

---

## The Storyline: The Battle of the Trailblazers

Both teams are writing history. Uzbekistan as the first Central Asian team to reach a World Cup. DR Congo as a team returning after 52 years. This match is between two sides that have overcome extraordinary obstacles just to be here.

On paper, this is the most evenly-matched contest in Group K. Neither team has the technical superiority of Portugal or Colombia, but both have genuine quality and the motivation of a lifetime. The winner takes a massive step toward the knockout rounds.

Eldor Shomurodov vs Cedric Bakambu — two strikers with European experience — could be the head-to-head that decides the match. Both know where the goal is. Both will be desperate to write their names into World Cup history.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Eldor Shomurodov vs Chancel Mbemba
Shomurodov's movement against Mbemba's defensive leadership. A matchup between two players with European top-flight experience.

### Yoane Wissa vs Farrukh Sayfiev
Wissa's pace against Sayfiev's positioning. If Wissa gets in behind, Uzbekistan's defensive structure could collapse.

### Jaloliddin Masharipov vs Cedric Bakambu
Masharipov's creativity against DR Congo's compact midfield. Uzbekistan's playmaker must find a way through the physical barrier.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Uzbekistan (4-2-3-1):** Organized, disciplined, and compact. They will look to control midfield through Masharipov and release Shomurodov with precise passes. Set pieces are a weapon.

**DR Congo (4-3-3):** Physical and direct. The Leopards will try to overpower Uzbekistan's midfield and use their pace on the flanks. Set pieces could be decisive with their aerial strength.

---

## X-Factor

**The first goal.** In a match between two evenly-matched teams, scoring first is enormous. The team that concedes must open up, creating space for counters. The first 30 minutes could be cagey; after that, all bets are off.
