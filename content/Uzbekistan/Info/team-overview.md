# Uzbekistan National Football Team — White Wolves

## Overview

The Uzbekistan national football team, known as the **White Wolves** (Oq Bo'rilar), is Central Asia's most successful football nation. After decades of near-misses, Uzbekistan finally qualified for their first FIFA World Cup in 2026.

## World Cup Record
| Year | Result | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 2026 | Qualified | First ever World Cup appearance |

- **Appearances:** 1 (2026)
- **AFC Asian Cup:** Fourth place (2011)
- **Central Asian Cup:** Champions (multiple)

## Playing Style

Uzbekistan plays an organized, technically sound brand of football influenced by both Russian/Soviet tactical traditions and modern European coaching. They combine physical presence with improving technical quality.

### Key Tactical Elements
- **Defensive Organization:** Disciplined defensive structure
- **Counter-Attacking:** Rapid transitions using pace on the wings
- **Technical Quality:** Players developed through improved domestic and European pathways
- **Set Pieces:** Strong aerial presence at both ends

## Notable Achievements
- First Central Asian team to qualify for a FIFA World Cup
- Consistently competitive in Asian qualifying
- Growing number of players in European leagues

## Famous Players
- **Server Djeparov** — Two-time Asian Footballer of the Year
- **Maksim Shatskikh** — Prolific striker, Dynamo Kyiv legend
- **Ignatiy Nesterov** — Long-serving goalkeeper
- **Eldor Shomurodov** — Current star, Serie A striker
