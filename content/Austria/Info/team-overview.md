## Team History

### Das Wunderteam's Legacy

Austria's national football team carries one of the richest traditions in European football. The original **Wunderteam** (Wonder Team) of the 1930s, coached by Hugo Meisl, was among the most influential sides in football history, pioneering a fluid passing style that predated Total Football. Though that golden generation was broken apart by the Anschluss in 1938, Austria's football identity has endured through the decades.

### Modern Renaissance

Under Ralf Rangnick, appointed in 2022, Austria has undergone a tactical transformation. Rangnick implemented his trademark gegenpressing system, turning Austria into one of Europe's most intense pressing teams. A deep run at Euro 2024, where they reached the quarter-finals, confirmed Austria's return to relevance. The team's spine — Alaba, Sabitzer, Laimer, Baumgartner — plays at elite European clubs and brings Champions League pedigree.

### Playing Style

Under Ralf Rangnick, Austria play an aggressive 4-2-3-1 / 4-4-2 pressing system. The team presses high and relentlessly, looking to win the ball in dangerous areas. Quick vertical passing follows turnovers. The wide players — Baumgartner and Arnautovic — provide creativity and finishing, while the midfield duo of Sabitzer and Laimer combines technical quality with tireless running. Alaba, when fit, orchestrates from the back with his passing range.
