# Thumbnail & Growth Research — what gets more views

**Practice (series rule):** before changing the thumbnail template, refresh this
file. Two inputs: (1) our **own YouTube Studio** — CTR + impressions per video,
and which thumbnails already win for us; (2) the **fastest-growing football /
faceless-narration channels** — copy what they do. Last refreshed: **Jun 2026**.

## What our own data already tells us (YouTube Studio)

- **App-style thumbnails win for us.** The "Pick 3 + climb" and "phone +
  leaderboard" thumbnails are our most-viewed — the value proposition (climb a
  leaderboard, free) out-pulls a plain match face. → baked into every episode
  thumbnail via the kit (`_thumbnail-kit/`).
- **Star power + a curiosity/stakes hook wins** (Ep6 Messi/Argentina breakout,
  979 views, zero ad spend, per `SERIES_PLAYBOOK.md`). Generic, no-face,
  no-hook thumbnails earned ~zero CTR.
- **Action:** keep tracking CTR per video in Studio; promote the winning
  variant; retire formats under ~4% CTR.

## What the research says drives CTR (2025–26)

1. **Faces with strong emotion.** Faces beat object-only by ~25–30%; a clear
   emotion (shock/joy/anger/fear) adds up to ~30% by opening a *curiosity gap*.
   Face well-lit, in focus, ≥25–30% of the frame, readable at 168×94 px.
2. **High contrast / bold color.** Yellow & orange accents and a subject 30%
   brighter or darker than the background lift CTR ~20–30%. 2–3 bold colors max.
3. **≤3 words of text**, bold condensed sans, upper portion (avoid the bottom
   timestamp). 1–3 high-impact words beat sentences.
4. **Packaging = title + thumbnail as ONE unit (the "tension triangle").** The
   thumbnail raises a question; the **title gives the answer / open loop** — they
   must *complement*, never repeat. Curiosity gap = bigger the gap, higher the click.
5. **A/B everything.** Thumbnail swaps move CTR more than title swaps. Use
   YouTube's native **Test & Compare** (up to 3 thumbnails/video); test extremes
   (eye-contact vs looking-at-value-prop; mystery vs clarity) — different
   audiences reward different motivators.
6. **Consistency + cadence.** ~2 uploads/week; packaging + retention +
   consistency are what actually break a channel out. Target **CTR > 6%**.
7. **Mobile-first.** Most impressions are tiny — verify legibility at ~120 px.

## How our current template scores, and what to copy next

Our kit thumbnail already does: star face (left), gold ≤4-word hook (top-left,
high contrast), the leaderboard-climb phone (our proven value prop), brand
colors. Gaps to close, copied from the above:

- [ ] **Push facial emotion.** Our Pixar renders read intense/neutral; favor the
  most *expressive* render per player, or generate shock/joy variants. (Biggest
  expected lift.)
- [ ] **Tighten hooks to ≤3 words** where possible ("BRAZIL'S NIGHTMARE?" → keep;
  "EVERY STADIUM. NO COUNTRY." → shorten for the thumbnail).
- [ ] **A/B variants per episode.** Extend the kit to emit 2–3 variants
  (A: face + eye contact, no phone; B: face + leaderboard phone; C: bigger number
  / score tease) and run them through Test & Compare. Promote the winner.
- [ ] **Package title↔thumbnail as a pair.** Thumbnail asks ("THEY BEAT SPAIN");
  title answers the open loop — don't duplicate the words. Keep titles in
  `UPLOAD_PACKS.md` aligned to each thumbnail hook.
- [ ] **Verify each thumbnail at 120 px** before upload (kit could output a
  contact sheet at mobile scale).

## Copy-list: tactics from fast-growing channels

- Packaging-first workflow: design the **title + thumbnail before/with** the
  video, not after.
- One dominant subject, one emotion, one idea per thumbnail — no clutter.
- Curiosity gap on the thumbnail; payoff in the title; deliver in the first 30s
  (retention) so the algorithm keeps serving impressions.
- Ship Shorts around each long-form (we do) to feed subscribers → suggested.
- Re-test and swap thumbnails on older videos that have impressions but low CTR.
