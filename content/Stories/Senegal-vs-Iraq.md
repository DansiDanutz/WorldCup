# Senegal vs Iraq — The Lion Meets the Lions of Mesopotamia
*Group I · Matchday 2 · June 17, 2026 · Stanford Stadium, California*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** two proud anthems of survival → Senegalese sabar vs Iraqi maqam → silence on the 55th-minute header → crossbar clang, a held breath → both themes rising into one on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Lions of Teranga — *"Sé-né-gal!"*, sabar drums, *"Lions de la Te-ran-ga!"*; Lions of Mesopotamia — *"I-raq! I-raq!"*, *"U-sood Al-Ra-fi-dain!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Two teams called the Lions, two nations that have played through hardship most footballers cannot imagine. If football can be a form of resistance — which kind of lion's roar carries furthest tonight?*

### 1 · COLD OPEN — A Crossroads of Civilizations (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: two proud, weathered anthems overlapping.]**
**[ANIMATION: West African savanna and the Tigris-Euphrates plain dissolving into one floodlit pitch; two lions, one golden, one ancient.]**

Stanford Stadium becomes a crossroads of civilizations. Senegal, the Lions of Teranga, carry the rhythm and fire of West Africa. Iraq, the Lions of Mesopotamia, bring the resilience of a nation that has played through war, invasion, and hardship most footballers cannot imagine. *(beat)* Two teams, two continents, two stories of football as resistance.

**[SUPPORTERS — Senegalese sabar: "Sé-né-gal! Sé-né-gal!"]**

### 2 · THE BIG BROTHER (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: sabar drums, warm and steady.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mané, dignified, surrounded by younger teammates; the hospital he built rising behind him.]**

Sadio Mané — the man from Bambali who built a hospital for his village — stands with the quiet dignity that made him one of football's most beloved figures. 55 goals, his nation's all-time top scorer, but it is his spirit that defines him. *"Sadio is our big brother,"* says young Lamine Camara. *"When he's in the team, we feel anything is possible."* Beside him, Nicolas Jackson, who grew up watching Mané, now plays alongside him — generational continuity made flesh.

### 3 · THE ANCHOR (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: Iraqi maqam strings, proud and resolute.]**
**[ANIMATION: Zidane Iqbal calm under pressure; Aymen Hussein and Ali Al-Hamadi flanking him.]**

Iraq's Zidane Iqbal — the boy named after a legend — faces another test of his extraordinary journey. His teammates Aymen Hussein, Ali Al-Hamadi, and Amir Al-Ammari — who calls Iqbal's calm *"our anchor"* — represent a new generation of Iraqis raised in Europe who chose the land of their parents. *(beat)* They did not come to make up the numbers. They came because football, for Iraq, has always been more than a game.

**[SUPPORTERS — Iraqi end: "U-sood Al-Ra-fi-dain!"]**

### 4 · THE 55TH MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: sabar and maqam lock — then silence on the leap.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mané beating two on the left; the cross; giant Koulibaly rising from a corner; the header; the Iraqi keeper flying; the crossbar.]**

55th minute. Mané receives on the left — 34 now, but the explosive pace remains. He beats one, then another, body feints as devastating as his Liverpool days. The cross comes in, and Kalidou Koulibaly, who has pushed up for the corner, meets it with his head. The Iraqi keeper flies. **The ball crashes against the crossbar.** **[SUPPORTERS: a collective gasp.]** Koulibaly holds his head. Mané pats his captain's back. *"Next time,"* he says. *"There's always a next time."*

### 5 · COMMON GROUND (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: sabar and maqam begin to share a single rhythm.]**
**[ANIMATION: players from both sides clasping hands mid-battle, two kinds of survival recognising each other.]**

The Lions of Teranga and the Lions of Mesopotamia share more than a nickname. *(beat)* They share a belief that football can transcend the circumstances of birth, war, and history — and on this afternoon in California, two nations that have suffered more than most find common ground on a patch of grass.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the two anthems rise into one, then hold.]**
**[ANIMATION: the golden lion and the ancient lion standing together as the lights dim.]**

Two prides, two roars, one pitch. *(beat)* **And the deeper question carries on the California wind: if both nations turned suffering into a team, and both made football a form of resistance — then whose roar is the louder, the one forged in a village with rolled-up socks, or the one forged under the weight of war? Or do both lions, simply by being here, already win?** The crossbar kept the answer. The pride roars on.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Sé-né-gal!" … "I-raq! I-raq!"]**
