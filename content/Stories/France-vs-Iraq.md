# France vs Iraq — The Impossible Meeting
*Group I · Matchday 2 · June 17, 2026 · Stanford Stadium, California*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a soft, fated music-box melody → French grandeur vs Iraqi maqam strings → tension to the 38th-minute pass → a shot just wide, a held breath → the music-box returning, knowing, on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Les Bleus — *"Allez les Bleus!"*, *"La Marseillaise"*; Lions of Mesopotamia — *"I-raq! I-raq!"*, *"U-sood Al-Ra-fi-dain!"* (Lions of the Two Rivers), Arabic chant
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *A boy his father named after Zidane now leads Iraq against Zidane's own nation. Football keeps writing a story too perfect to be fiction — so what happens when the name finally meets its origin?*

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Unthinkable a Generation Ago (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a soft, fated music-box melody.]**
**[ANIMATION: a war-shadowed Iraqi street dissolves into a sunlit Californian pitch; a passport reading "Zidane Iqbal" turns in the light.]**

Stanford Stadium hosts a meeting that would have been unthinkable a generation ago. France — 2018 champions, 2022 finalists — face Iraq, a nation whose footballing story was written in the shadow of war, sanctions, and hardship most cannot imagine. *(beat)* Yet here they are, on the same grass, united by nothing more complicated than a ball and a dream. And one impossible coincidence.

**[SUPPORTERS — Iraqi end: "U-sood Al-Ra-fi-dain!"]**

### 2 · THE PRINCE AND THE MAGICIAN (0:55–1:40)
**[MUSIC: French grandeur, light and confident.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mbappé adjusting the armband; Michael Olise drifting between lines, making defenders dizzy.]**

Kylian Mbappé adjusts his armband with the ease of a man whose 12 World Cup goals already place him among the immortals. Beside him, Michael Olise — "The Magician," born in London, who chose France over England, Algeria and Nigeria — spins defences into knots. William Saliba, who calls Mbappé *"our leader… because of the standard he sets every day,"* marshals the back.

### 3 · THE BOY NAMED ZIDANE (1:40–2:35)
**[MUSIC: Iraqi maqam strings, proud and aching.]**
**[ANIMATION: a young Zidane Iqbal in a Manchester academy; his father pointing at a TV showing the real Zidane.]**

Iraq's story is the tournament's most extraordinary. Zidane Iqbal — 23, born in Manchester to an Iraqi-Pakistani family, a Manchester United academy product who trained beside Ronaldo, now at FC Utrecht. His father named him after the French-Algerian legend. *(beat)* And tonight the son faces the nation of his namesake. *"My dad set the bar pretty high,"* Iqbal once laughed. *"I'm just trying to be the best Zidane Iqbal I can be."* Beside him, Aymen Hussein's power and Ali Al-Hamadi's English-honed edge. *The irony is almost too perfect.*

**[SUPPORTERS — full Iraqi roar: "I-raq! I-raq!"]**

### 4 · THE 38TH MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: tension gathers — then silence as the shot flies.]**
**[ANIMATION: Iqbal under pressure from two Frenchmen; the calm pass; Iraq's shape holding; Hussein turning and shooting just wide.]**

38th minute. Iqbal receives in midfield with Mbappé bearing down on him. He does what he has done since he was a boy at Manchester United — stays calm, plays the simple pass, keeps Iraq's shape intact. The ball finds Aymen Hussein, who turns and shoots — *just wide.* **[SUPPORTERS: a held breath, then a roar.]** France exhale. *Iraq believe.*

### 5 · POETRY TOO PERFECT (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the music-box melody returns, almost smiling.]**
**[ANIMATION: the name "Zidane" on an Iraqi shirt, framed against the French crest.]**

The beautiful game writes stories no scriptwriter would dare invent. *(beat)* A boy named Zidane, leading Iraq against France. Sometimes football's poetry is too perfect to be fiction — and tonight it is being written in real time, in front of a disbelieving world.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the fated music-box holds its final, knowing note.]**
**[ANIMATION: the two Zidanes — the legend in ghostly sepia, the boy in living colour — facing each other across the pitch.]**

A name given in tribute, now standing across from the very thing it honoured. *(beat)* **And the question is too poetic to ignore: when a father names his son after a hero, and that son grows up to lead a war-scarred nation against the hero's own country — is it coincidence that put them on this pitch tonight, or was this match written the day the boy was named?** The music-box knows. It just won't say.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Allez les Bleus!" … "I-raq! I-raq!"]**
