# Haiti vs Scotland — The Battle of the Underdogs
*Group C · Matchday 3 · June 22, 2026*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** two anthems played softly, overlapping (rara horn / bagpipe) → anxious ticking under the stakes → eruption on each goal → frantic strings through the final assault → both anthems swelling together on the bittersweet question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Les Grenadiers — *"A-yi-ti! A-yi-ti!"*, rara horns and tanbou; Tartan Army — *"O Flower of Scotland…"*, *"Yes Sir, I Can Boogie!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Two nations who have spent their whole histories as football's nearly-men, fighting for one last door. If just being here was already a victory — why does it hurt so much to lose?*

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Brutal Mathematics (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: rara horn and bagpipe play their anthems softly, overlapping.]**
**[ANIMATION: the Group C table glows; Brazil and Morocco locked above; Haiti and Scotland circling the final third-place door.]**

Matchday 3, and the maths are merciless. Brazil and Morocco have all but taken the top two. Haiti and Scotland fight for third — and one of the eight best third-placed teams advances. For Haiti, a win means history: a first knockout stage for the first Caribbean qualifiers. For Scotland, a win could end a curse that has held since 1954 — eight World Cups, never once past the group. *(beat)* Two nations of nearly-men, one last door.

**[SUPPORTERS — rara horns and pipes overlap: "A-yi-ti!" and "Flower of Scotland".]**

### 2 · TWO MUSEUMS OF HEARTBREAK (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: a wistful, shared minor theme.]**
**[ANIMATION: a gallery of ghosts — Scotland's lost goal to Peru in '78; Haiti's 52-year exile, earthquakes, an empty federation office.]**

Scotland's agony is famous — unbeaten and out in '74, the Peru collapse in '78, Costa Rica in '90. Haiti's is quieter and just as deep — a 52-year gap, a country battered by disaster and instability, a federation that barely exists. Two museums of heartbreak, meeting at last.

### 3 · THE TWO WHO CARRY IT (1:45–2:30)
**[MUSIC: a determined pulse.]**
**[ANIMATION: Nazon, jaw set, beside Étienne Jr.; opposite them, McTominay and McGinn shoulder to shoulder.]**

Duckens Nazon carries Haiti — the supermarket striker who knows this might be his only World Cup. Beside him, Derrick Étienne Jr. pulls the strings. Scotland answer with McTominay, playing with the fury of a man one win from making history, and McGinn driving alongside Robertson. *Everything either nation is, gathered into ninety minutes.*

### 4 · SCOTLAND STRIKES (2:30–3:20)
**[MUSIC: silence on the cross — then a Tartan eruption.]**
**[ANIMATION: Robertson's cross; McTominay arriving at the near post; the header buried.]**

17th minute. Robertson crosses from the left; McTominay attacks the near post and heads it home — powerful, precise. **[SUPPORTERS: the Tartan Army erupts so hard the stand trembles — "Yes Sir, I Can Boogie!"]** Twenty-eight years of pain, released in a single moment. *But Haiti does not know how to fold.*

### 5 · THE EQUALISER AND THE CHAOS (3:20–4:25)
**[MUSIC: a triumphant horn — then frantic, end-to-end strings.]**
**[ANIMATION: Nazon holding off Hendry, the deflected loop over Gordon; then a storm of chances at both ends.]**

63rd minute. A long ball; Nazon holds off Jack Hendry, turns, shoots — a deflection loops it over Gordon. **[SUPPORTERS: rara horns scream; eleven million dance through one goal.]** The final twenty minutes are bedlam, because a draw eliminates both. McTominay from 25 — saved. Nazon clean through — Gordon blocks. 89th minute: Shankland turns and shoots — off the post — the rebound to McGinn, sliced wide. *Inches from heaven, both of them.*

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: both anthems rise together, bittersweet.]**
**[ANIMATION: 1–1 final; the Haitian players collapse, then rise, then dance; the Tartan Army sings on in the rain.]**

1–1. Both eliminated. And still the Tartan Army sings; still the Haitians dance on the grass. *(beat)* For both nations, simply being here was already a victory — **so why does it ache like this? Maybe because the nearly-men know a secret the giants never learn: that showing up to fight, and almost winning, is its own kind of glory — and its own kind of curse.** Some battles are won just by daring to play them. Tonight, two underdogs won that one, and lost everything else.

**[SUPPORTERS — undimmed, overlapping into the night: "A-yi-ti!" … "O Flower of Scotland…"]**
