# Morocco vs Haiti — The Lion and the Grenadier

*Group C, Matchday 2 — June 17, 2026*

Achraf Hakimi was born in Madrid. His parents are Moroccan. He could have played for Spain — the country of his birth, the country that developed him through Real Madrid's academy. Instead, he chose Morocco, the country of his heart. When he covered his eyes after that penalty against Spain in the 2022 World Cup quarterfinal, he was telling the world: I chose. And I chose correctly.

Haiti's players understand choice. Derrick Etienne Jr. chose Haiti over the United States. Bryan Alceus chose Haiti over France. Every player in the Haitian squad has faced the same decision — represent the nation of their birth or the nation of their blood. They chose Haiti, a country that has given them heartbreak and pride in equal measure.

When Morocco and Haiti meet in Group C, it is a match between two diaspora teams, two nations whose best players were developed in European academies but chose to represent their ancestral homelands. The Atlas Lions, fresh from a historic semifinal run, face Les Grenadiers, returning to the World Cup after a 52-year absence.

Hakimi vs Haiti's left wing is a mismatch on paper. The best right-back in the world against players from the Belgian second division and French lower leagues. But football isn't played on paper. In the 22nd minute, Duckens Nazon — the supermarket worker turned international striker — receives the ball with his back to goal, turns past Sofyan Amrabat, and fires a shot that forces Yassine Bounou into a full-stretch save. The Haitian fans, small in number but immense in voice, believe.

Morocco's quality eventually tells. In the 38th minute, Hakimi overlaps on the right, receives Ziyech's pass, and delivers a cross that Youssef En-Nesyri heads home with the authority of a man who has scored in Champions League finals. Morocco leads. In the 56th minute, Bilal El-Khannouss, the young Moroccan playmaker, adds a second with a curling shot from the edge of the box.

But Haiti scores. In the 81st minute, from a corner, Ricardo Ade — the Haitian defender — rises highest and powers a header past Bounou. The goal is celebrated like a victory. Because for Haiti, at the World Cup, scoring against a semifinalist is a victory. Morocco wins 2-1, but Haiti takes home something that cannot be measured in points.
