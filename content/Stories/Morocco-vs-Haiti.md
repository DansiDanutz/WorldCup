# Morocco vs Haiti — The Lion and the Grenadier
*Group C · Matchday 2 · June 17, 2026*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** two themes from two diasporas (oud / rara horn) → both circling the idea of "choice" → tension on the chances → swelling pride on each goal → the two themes meeting as equals on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Atlas Lions — *"Di-ma Magh-rib!"*, darbuka; Les Grenadiers — *"A-yi-ti! A-yi-ti!"*, rara horns and tanbou
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Two teams built by sons who could have worn other flags — and chose the homeland instead. What does that choice cost a man, and what does it give him back when the anthem plays?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — He Chose (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a single oud line, decisive.]**
**[ANIMATION: Hakimi covering his eyes after the 2022 penalty vs Spain — then opening them, looking straight to camera.]**

Achraf Hakimi was born in Madrid. He was developed by Real Madrid. He could have worn Spain. Instead he chose Morocco — the country of his heart. When he covered his eyes after that penalty against Spain in 2022, he was telling the world: *I chose. And I chose correctly.* (beat) Tonight he faces a team made entirely of men who made the same choice.

**[SUPPORTERS — darbuka and voices: "Di-ma Magh-rib!"]**

### 2 · THE GRENADIERS WHO CHOSE (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: a lone rara horn answers.]**
**[ANIMATION: a row of Haitian players, each with two faded flags behind them — one fades, the Haitian one brightens.]**

Haiti understand choice. Derrick Étienne Jr. chose Haiti over the United States. Bryan Alcéus chose Haiti over France. Every man in this squad faced the same fork — the nation of their birth, or the nation of their blood — and chose Les Grenadiers, returning from a 52-year exile. *Two diaspora teams, one quiet, defiant decision repeated over and over.*

### 3 · MISMATCH ON PAPER (1:45–2:30)
**[MUSIC: tense, anticipatory.]**
**[ANIMATION: Hakimi, world-class, lining up opposite players from the Belgian second tier and French lower leagues.]**

On paper it is a mismatch — the best right-back alive against journeymen from modest leagues. *But football is not played on paper.* 22nd minute: Duckens Nazon, the supermarket striker, takes it back-to-goal, spins past Amrabat, and forces Yassine Bounou into a full-stretch save. **[SUPPORTERS: rara horns blaze — "A-yi-ti!"]** Small in number, immense in voice, the Haitians believe.

### 4 · QUALITY TELLS (2:30–3:25)
**[MUSIC: the oud swells, assured.]**
**[ANIMATION: Hakimi overlapping; En-Nesyri rising; El-Khannouss curling one home.]**

Morocco's class eventually speaks. 38th minute: Hakimi overlaps, takes Ziyech's pass, and clips a cross that En-Nesyri heads home with the authority of a Champions League finalist. 56th: young Bilal El-Khannouss bends a second from the edge. **[SUPPORTERS: "Di-ma Magh-rib!" rolls round the bowl.]** Two-nil. The lion is in control.

### 5 · THE GRENADIER'S REPLY (3:25–4:20)
**[MUSIC: silence on the corner — then a single triumphant horn.]**
**[ANIMATION: Ricardo Adé climbing above the pack at a corner, the header flashing past Bounou.]**

81st minute. A corner. Ricardo Adé rises highest and powers a header past Bounou. **[SUPPORTERS: the Haitian end erupts as if they had won the cup.]** Because for Haiti, at a World Cup, scoring against a semifinalist *is* a kind of winning.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:20–5:00)
**[MUSIC: oud and rara horn settle into one shared chord.]**
**[ANIMATION: players from both sides embracing; two flags — chosen, not inherited — rippling side by side.]**

Morocco win 2–1. But Haiti carry off something no scoreboard records. *(beat)* **Every man on this pitch could have worn an easier shirt, a richer flag, a safer future — and every one of them chose the harder road home. So what is it worth, that choice?** Tonight it cost Haiti the points and gave them their pride; it gave Morocco the win and a reminder of why they play. The lion and the grenadier, bound by the same defiant yes.

**[SUPPORTERS — overlapping, fading: "Di-ma Magh-rib!" … "A-yi-ti!"]**
