# USA vs Australia — The Pacific Showdown
*Group D · Matchday 2 · June 17, 2026 · Rose Bowl, Pasadena*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** ocean swell and gulls → bright Californian theme vs a stoic Australian motif → near-silence on the 84th-minute save → a held breath → the tide pulling back on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** USA — *"U-S-A! U-S-A!"*, *"I believe that we will win!"*; Socceroos — *"Aussie! Aussie! Aussie! Oi! Oi! Oi!"*, *"Soc-ce-roos!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Two nations on opposite shores of the largest ocean on earth, who have never met and not blinked. Across all that distance — does talent decide, or is it simply who refuses to be intimidated?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Largest Ocean (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: an ocean swell; distant gulls; a low drone beneath.]**
**[ANIMATION: a satellite view of the Pacific; two pins — Los Angeles and Sydney — pull toward the Rose Bowl.]**

Two nations separated by the world's largest ocean, united by a rivalry that has burned across every code — cricket, rugby, and now football's grandest stage. The Rose Bowl gleams under the California sun, the same turf where the USA women lifted the World Cup in 1999. *(beat)* Today the men carry that legacy — and fifteen million Australians carry their hopes across the Pacific. *Distance, it turns out, is just water.*

**[SUPPORTERS — red-white-blue tide: "U-S-A! U-S-A!"]**

### 2 · THE CAPTAIN, POSSESSED (0:55–1:40)
**[MUSIC: a bright, restless Californian theme.]**
**[ANIMATION: Pulisic prowling the touchline, every touch sparking.]**

Christian Pulisic prowls the line like a man possessed. For half a decade he has been the beating heart of American football, and this afternoon — with the knockout rounds in reach — his every touch carries electricity.

### 3 · THE SIX-LEAGUE WALL (1:40–2:30)
**[MUSIC: a stoic, weathered Australian motif.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mathew Ryan in his gloves; a globe spins behind him, lighting up the six countries he's played in.]**

Across the grass stands Mathew Ryan — "Maty" — the six-league goalkeeper who has played in more countries than most people visit on holiday. 104 caps. He stood firm in Qatar as Australia pushed Argentina to the brink. He will not be cowed by a partisan crowd. Behind him, Harry Souttar surveys the field like a castle guard; Jackson Irvine patrols the centre as if every blade of grass were personal. *Australia have never beaten the USA at a World Cup. This is a different Socceroos side — and it remembers how close it came.*

**[SUPPORTERS — green-and-gold corner: "Aussie! Aussie! Aussie! Oi! Oi! Oi!"]**

### 4 · THE 84TH MINUTE (2:30–3:40)
**[MUSIC: tension crackles like static; then total silence on the strike.]**
**[ANIMATION: 0–0; Tyler Adams wins it in the circle; Reyna spins; Pulisic lining up the shot; Ryan already moving.]**

84th minute. 0–0. Tension crackling like static. Tyler Adams wins the ball in the centre circle and launches the counter. Reyna receives, spins, slides Pulisic into space. The captain takes one touch, lines it up — but Ryan is already there. A 34-year-old body thrown into the path of destiny. **The save is magnificent.** **[SUPPORTERS: a gasp, then "Soc-ce-roos!"]** The Pacific Ocean feels a little smaller.

### 5 · THE THING THAT TRAVELS (3:40–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the two themes hold each other in balance.]**
**[ANIMATION: Ryan rising from the save, roaring; the American bench, hands on heads.]**

One save can cross an ocean. It can shrink the distance between two shores to the width of a goalmouth. *(beat)* Two nations, one ocean, one shared truth taking shape under the California lights — that on this stage, courage travels further than any talent.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the tide pulls back; the drone fades to gulls.]**
**[ANIMATION: the satellite Pacific again — the two pins still glowing, the water between them suddenly small.]**

In the World Cup, distance means nothing and courage means everything. *(beat)* **And so the ocean asks its quiet question: when two peoples this far apart finally meet and neither will blink, what is left to decide it — a moment of magic, or a man who simply refuses to be beaten?** Tonight, the answer wore gloves. The tide goes out. The question stays.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "U-S-A!" … "Aussie! Aussie! Aussie!"]**
