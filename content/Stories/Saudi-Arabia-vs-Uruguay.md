# Saudi Arabia vs Uruguay — The Falconer and the Panther

*Group H, Matchday 1 — June 12, 2026*

MetLife Stadium in New Jersey hosts a match that carries the scent of the impossible. Saudi Arabia, the team that shocked Argentina in 2022, face Uruguay, the team that nearly reached the 2010 World Cup final. Two nations from different footballing continents, united by a shared belief that the beautiful game belongs to anyone brave enough to claim it.

Salem Al-Dawsari steps onto the pitch with the calm of an avid falconer — patient, precise, deadly when the moment arrives. His goal against Argentina in 2022 was not a fluke; it was the culmination of a lifetime of work, a left-footed strike that announced Saudi Arabian football to the world. Now 34, he is Al-Hilal's most decorated player, his jersey the best-selling in Saudi Pro League history. Beside him, Yasser Al-Shahrani, the defender who sacrificed his body in that Argentina match, returns for one final World Cup campaign.

Uruguay's response is Darwin Nunez, "El Pantera," the Liverpool legend turned Al-Hilal teammate of Saudi Arabia's stars. Nunez knows these defenders — he plays with them, trains with them, shares meals with them. But tonight, friendship is suspended. His teammate Federico Valverde, "El Halcancito," provides the midfield engine that makes everything possible. Ronald Araujo, the Barcelona colossus, anchors a defense that has conceded fewer goals than almost any South American team in qualifying.

In the 44th minute, Al-Dawsari picks up the ball on the left wing. He is 35 yards from goal. No one expects what comes next — except perhaps Nunez, who has seen this in training. Al-Dawsari strikes the ball with his left foot, a rising, swirling shot that seems to hang in the New Jersey air for an eternity. The Uruguayan goalkeeper, Dominik Livakovic, backpedals, stretches, gets his fingertips to it — but not enough. The ball crashes into the top corner. MetLife Stadium erupts. Saudi Arabia's bench empties. Al-Dawsari slides to his knees, arms spread wide, a falcon in full flight.

Some moments are written in the stars. Al-Dawsari, the falconer, has struck again on the World Cup stage. Uruguay, the two-time champions, have been warned: the desert warriors do not fear the Charruas.
