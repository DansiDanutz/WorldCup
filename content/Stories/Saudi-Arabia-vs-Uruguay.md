# Saudi Arabia vs Uruguay — The Falconer and the Panther
*Group H · Matchday 1 · June 12, 2026 · MetLife Stadium, New Jersey*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a desert-falcon theme over a low panther-prowl bass → Arabic strings vs garra-charrúa drums → silence on the 44th-minute strike → an eruption → the falcon theme soaring on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Green Falcons — *"Sau-di! Sau-di!"*, *"Al-Akh-dar!"*, Arabic drums; La Celeste — *"¡U-ru-guay!"*, candombe, *"¡Va-mos Uru-guay no-má!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *The falconer who once felled Argentina against the panther who plays alongside him every week. They know each other's every move — so is a moment of genius something you can prepare for, or is it always written in the stars?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Scent of the Impossible (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a lone falcon-cry motif over a prowling bass.]**
**[ANIMATION: a falcon circling above MetLife; below, a panther pacing the touchline.]**

MetLife Stadium hosts a match that carries the scent of the impossible. Saudi Arabia — who shocked Argentina in 2022 — face Uruguay, who came within a whisker of the 2010 final. *(beat)* Two nations from different footballing worlds, bound by one belief: that the beautiful game belongs to anyone brave enough to claim it. And one of them has a man who has claimed the impossible before.

**[SUPPORTERS — Green Falcons: "Al-Akh-dar! Al-Akh-dar!"]**

### 2 · THE FALCONER (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: Arabic strings, patient, coiled.]**
**[ANIMATION: Al-Dawsari calm as a falconer waiting for the moment; Yasser Al-Shahrani beside him, the 2022 sacrifice etched in his face.]**

Salem Al-Dawsari steps out with the calm of a falconer — patient, precise, deadly when the moment comes. His goal against Argentina in 2022 was no fluke; it was a lifetime of work in one left-footed strike. Now 34, Al-Hilal's most decorated player, his jersey the best-selling in Saudi history. Beside him, Yasser Al-Shahrani — who sacrificed his body in that Argentina match — returns for one final campaign.

### 3 · THE PANTHER WHO KNOWS HIS PREY (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: garra-charrúa drums, restless and powerful.]**
**[ANIMATION: Darwin Núñez prowling; flashbacks of him training and laughing with the very Saudis he now faces.]**

Uruguay's answer is Darwin Núñez — "El Pantera" — now an Al-Hilal teammate of Saudi Arabia's stars. *He knows these defenders. He trains with them, eats with them. But tonight, friendship is suspended.* Federico Valverde drives the engine; Ronald Araújo anchors a defence that conceded almost nothing in qualifying. *(beat)* Núñez, more than anyone, knows what Al-Dawsari can do — because he has seen it in training.

**[SUPPORTERS — La Celeste: "¡U-ru-guay! ¡U-ru-guay!"]**

### 4 · THE 44TH MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: everything drops to silence — then the falcon strikes.]**
**[ANIMATION: Al-Dawsari on the left, 35 yards out; nobody expecting it — except Núñez, who has seen it before; the rising, swirling shot hanging in the air; Rochet backpedalling, fingertips, not enough; the top corner.]**

44th minute. Al-Dawsari collects on the left, 35 yards from goal. No one expects what comes next — except, perhaps, Núñez. Al-Dawsari strikes it with his left foot, a rising, swirling shot that seems to hang in the New Jersey air for an eternity. Sergio Rochet backpedals, stretches, gets his fingertips to it — *but not enough.* **The ball crashes into the top corner.** **[SUPPORTERS: MetLife erupts; the Saudi bench empties.]** Al-Dawsari slides to his knees, arms wide — a falcon in full flight.

### 5 · WRITTEN IN THE STARS (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the falcon theme soars, then a flicker of doubt underneath.]**
**[ANIMATION: Núñez, hands on head — he knew it was coming and still could not stop it.]**

Some moments are written in the stars. *(beat)* The falconer has struck again on the World Cup stage. And the cruelest detail of all: the one man who knew exactly what was coming — his own club teammate — could only watch it fly.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the falcon-cry holds one final, ringing note.]**
**[ANIMATION: the ball still nestled in the top corner; a falcon's silhouette crossing the floodlights.]**

Uruguay, two-time champions, have been warned: the desert warriors do not fear the charrúas. *(beat)* **And the deepest question circles MetLife like a hunting bird: if even the man who trains beside Al-Dawsari every day, who has seen that strike a hundred times, still could not stop it — then is a moment of true genius something anyone can ever prepare for, or is it simply written in the stars before the match begins?** Tonight, the stars wore green. The panther can only wonder what they have planned next.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Sau-di! Sau-di!" … "¡U-ru-guay!"]**
