# Spain vs Cape Verde — The Empire and the Island
*Group H · Matchday 1 · June 11, 2026 · Lumen Field, Seattle*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a vast imperial theme over a lone Atlantic morna guitar → tiki-taka pulse vs island rhythm → silence on the 34th-minute tackle → a teenager's smile, a held breath → both themes meeting as equals on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** La Roja — *"¡A por e-llos, o-é!"*, *"¡Es-pa-ña!"*; Blue Sharks — *"Ca-bo Ver-de!"*, morna guitar and drums
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *An empire of 47 million against an archipelago of half a million playing its first-ever World Cup match. The Blue Sharks crossed an ocean to get here — but did they come to be a footnote, or to write their own chapter?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Embers and Volcanoes (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a grand imperial theme; beneath it, a single morna guitar.]**
**[ANIMATION: Spain's red shirts glowing like embers; then ten volcanic islands rising from the Atlantic, tiny and defiant.]**

Lumen Field erupts as Spain step out, their red glowing like embers of a fire that has burned since 2010. Across from them: Cape Verde — the Blue Sharks — 590,000 souls scattered across ten volcanic islands. *(beat)* This is their first World Cup match in history. Their captain, Ryan Mendes, 35, has carried this team for over a decade. *They did not cross an ocean to admire the empire.*

**[SUPPORTERS — La Roja: "¡A por e-llos, o-é!"]**

### 2 · THE BOY MESSI ONCE BATHED (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: a bright, mesmerising tiki-taka motif.]**
**[ANIMATION: Lamine Yamal bouncing on his toes; a faint old photo flickers — an infant Yamal, bathed by a young Messi for a charity shoot.]**

Lamine Yamal — 18, the greatest teenage talent since Messi — bounces during the anthem. Two years ago he did homework between Champions League matches; as a baby, his photo was used in a UNICEF campaign featuring Messi himself. Beside him, Rodri — the 2024 Ballon d'Or winner, 74 games unbeaten — stands with quiet authority. *The empire's machine, with a boy genius at its tip.*

### 3 · THE MOST ROMANTIC STORY IN THE TOURNAMENT (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: the morna guitar swells, proud and warm.]**
**[ANIMATION: a map — Cape Verde, 350 miles off West Africa — its sons scattered across Europe's top leagues, all flying home.]**

Cape Verde's story is the tournament's most romantic. Islands 350 miles off West Africa, producing players who star across Europe. Ryan Mendes plays in the UAE but his heart beats for Praia. Garry Rodrigues brings the trickery; Logan Costa, the rising defender, the future. *A generation that refuses to be grateful merely to be here.*

**[SUPPORTERS — island corner: "Ca-bo Ver-de! Ca-bo Ver-de!"]**

### 4 · THE 34TH MINUTE (2:35–3:45)
**[MUSIC: tiki-taka and island rhythm collide — then silence on the tackle.]**
**[ANIMATION: Yamal's first touch freezing three defenders; the acceleration; old captain Mendes reading it, sliding in clean.]**

34th minute. Yamal takes it on the right, his first touch so hypnotic that three Cape Verdean defenders simply freeze. He accelerates — electric, terrifying — leaving one stranded. But Ryan Mendes, the old captain, reads the teenager's mind and slides in with a perfectly timed tackle. **[SUPPORTERS: the Seattle crowd gasps.]** Yamal smiles. *He has met his first World Cup resistance — and it came from an island half the world has never heard of.*

### 5 · SCALE AND SPIRIT (3:45–4:25)
**[MUSIC: imperial theme and morna find a shared key.]**
**[ANIMATION: 47 million and half a million, side by side on the same patch of grass, equal under the lights.]**

An empire of 47 million against an archipelago of half a million. *(beat)* But the Blue Sharks did not cross the Atlantic to be anyone's footnote. They came to write their own chapter in the greatest story in sport — and tonight, a single clean tackle reminded the empire that spirit does not check the size of a nation first.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the morna guitar holds one final, hopeful note.]**
**[ANIMATION: the volcanic islands glowing as brightly as the empire's embers across the dark Atlantic.]**

The empire and the island, on the same stage at last. *(beat)* **And the romantic question rises with the Atlantic wind: when half a million people refuse to kneel before forty-seven million — is it the size of the empire that writes history, or the size of the heart that dares to challenge it?** Tonight the Sharks bared their teeth. The chapter is only just beginning.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "¡Es-pa-ña!" … "Ca-bo Ver-de!"]**
