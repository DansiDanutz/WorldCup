# USA vs Turkey — Ghosts of Empires

*Group D, Matchday 3 — June 22, 2026*

SoFi Stadium rises from the Los Angeles earth like a spaceship that decided to stay — a $5 billion cathedral of American ambition. Tonight, it hosts a collision of two nations whose sporting histories could not be more different, yet whose paths always seem to cross at the most dramatic moments. The USA have met Turkey ten times, and every encounter has produced something worth remembering.

Christian Pulisic vs Arda Guler. The American captain against "El Magico." Two left-footed artists who operate on opposite flanks, both wearing the weight of their nation's footballing dreams. Pulisic, the 27-year-old who conquered Chelsea and AC Milan, carries a country's expectations with quiet dignity. Guler, the 21-year-old Turkish Messi, has already won a Champions League Breakthrough Player award at Real Madrid — a talent so luminous that Jude Bellingham himself stops in training to watch. Behind Guler lurks Hakan Calhanoglu, the Inter Milan architect whose passing range and set-piece delivery could unlock any defense from 30 yards. And Kenan Yildiz, the Juventus prodigy who chose Turkey over Germany, brings the fearless creativity of a player who has never learned what pressure means.

For Turkey, this is the ghost of 2002 made flesh. That magical World Cup in South Korea and Japan — third place, Hakan Sukur's 11-second goal, a nation transported by impossible dreams. For 24 years, Turkish fans have waited for a team to match that alchemy. This generation might be better. But the weight of that history is a burden as much as a blessing. For the USA, this is the match that could define their tournament — a win guarantees advancement, a loss could mean the end of the road on home soil.

Picture the 91st minute. The score is 2-2. Corner kick to Turkey. Calhanoglu stands over the ball, that elegant left foot poised like a scorpion's tail. He whips it in — a thunderbolt of a delivery — and Yildiz rises, connects, but Tyler Adams, the American warrior, throws his head into the path of the shot. The ball deflects wide. The crowd erupts. In the chaos of the penalty area, two nations hold their breath.

The beautiful game writes its own poetry, and in Los Angeles, the ink is still wet.
