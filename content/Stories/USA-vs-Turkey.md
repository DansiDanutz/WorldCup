# USA vs Turkey — Ghosts of Empires
*Group D · Matchday 3 · June 22, 2026 · SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a spaceship-hum over SoFi → two duelling left-footed themes (American anthem / Anatolian strings) → the ghost of 2002 swelling → frantic tension at the 91st-minute corner → a single suspended chord on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** USA — *"U-S-A! U-S-A!"*, *"I believe!"*; Ay-Yıldız — *"Tür-ki-ye! Tür-ki-ye!"*, *"Şam-pi-yon Tür-ki-ye!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Turkey have a ghost — third place in 2002 — that 24 years of teams have failed to match. Is a glorious past a map to follow, or a haunting you can never outrun?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Spaceship That Stayed (0:00–0:50)
**[MUSIC: a low, sci-fi hum under a single struck piano note.]**
**[ANIMATION: SoFi Stadium glows from above like a landed spacecraft; a faint "2002" flickers across its translucent roof.]**

SoFi Stadium rises from the Los Angeles earth like a spaceship that decided to stay — five billion dollars of American ambition. Tonight it hosts two nations whose histories could not be more different, yet whose paths keep crossing at the most dramatic moments. Ten meetings. Every one of them left something worth remembering. *(beat)* And one of them is carrying a ghost.

**[SUPPORTERS — Turkish end: "Tür-ki-ye! Tür-ki-ye!"]**

### 2 · TWO LEFT FEET, TWO NATIONS (0:50–1:45)
**[MUSIC: an American anthem-theme and an Anatolian string-line, mirrored.]**
**[ANIMATION: Pulisic on one flank, Arda Güler on the other — both left-footed, both lit like leads in a film.]**

Pulisic versus Arda Güler. The American captain against "El Mago." Two left-footed artists on opposite flanks, each carrying a nation's dream. Pulisic, 27, who conquered Chelsea and Milan, wears the weight with quiet dignity. Güler, 21, the "Turkish Messi," already a Champions League winner at Real Madrid — a talent so luminous Bellingham stops in training to watch.

### 3 · THE ARCHITECT AND THE FEARLESS (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: deep, architectural strings.]**
**[ANIMATION: Hakan Çalhanoğlu spraying a pass cross-field; Kenan Yıldız grinning, unbothered by the stakes.]**

Behind Güler lurks Hakan Çalhanoğlu — the Inter architect whose range and set-pieces could unlock any defence from thirty yards. And Kenan Yıldız, the Juventus prodigy who chose Turkey over Germany, plays with the fearlessness of a boy who has never learned what pressure means. *Together they could be better than 2002. Which is exactly the problem.*

### 4 · THE GHOST OF 2002 (2:35–3:25)
**[MUSIC: the Anatolian theme swells, proud and aching.]**
**[ANIMATION: grainy 2002 footage — Şükür's 11-second goal, a bronze celebration — dissolving into the modern XI.]**

For Turkey this is the ghost of 2002 made flesh: third place in Korea and Japan, Hakan Şükür's eleven-second goal, a nation lifted by an impossible dream. For 24 years the fans have waited for a team to match that alchemy. *(beat)* But a golden past is a burden as much as a blessing. For the USA, the maths are simpler and just as cruel — win and advance; lose and the road ends on home soil.

**[SUPPORTERS — full Turkish roar: "Şam-pi-yon Tür-ki-ye!"]**

### 5 · THE 91ST MINUTE (3:25–4:25)
**[MUSIC: everything drops out but a heartbeat — then chaos.]**
**[ANIMATION: 2–2; a corner; Çalhanoğlu standing over the ball, that left foot poised like a scorpion's tail.]**

Picture the 91st minute. 2–2. Corner to Turkey. Çalhanoğlu stands over it, the left foot coiled. He whips in a thunderbolt — and Yıldız rises, connects — but Tyler Adams hurls his head into the path of the shot. The ball deflects wide. **[SUPPORTERS: a stadium-wide scream.]** In the chaos of the box, two nations hold their breath.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: a single suspended chord; the wet-ink shimmer of a story unfinished.]**
**[ANIMATION: the "2002" on SoFi's roof flickers once more, then fades; the pitch glows alone.]**

The beautiful game writes its own poetry, and in Los Angeles the ink is still wet. *(beat)* **But the deeper riddle hangs over the Turkish half like incense: a generation may be more gifted than the ghosts of 2002 — so why does it feel less like a map to follow than a shadow they can never quite escape?** Some empires are remembered. Others are haunted by their own best day.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Tür-ki-ye!" … "U-S-A!"]**
