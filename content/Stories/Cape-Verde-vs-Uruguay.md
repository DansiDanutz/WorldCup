# Cape Verde vs Uruguay — The Sharks and the Charrúas
*Group H · Matchday 3 · June 22, 2026 · Mercedes-Benz Stadium, Atlanta*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** morna guitar braided with candombe drums → island rhythm vs garra-charrúa fury → silence on the 81st-minute strike → a defender's block, a held breath → both rhythms resolving into mutual respect on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Blue Sharks — *"Ca-bo Ver-de!"*, morna; La Celeste — *"¡U-ru-guay!"*, candombe drums, *"¡Va-mos Uru-guay no-má!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Half a million people against two-time world champions. The World Cup's real magic was never in the giant's predictable win — it's in the moment the smallest nation refuses to kneel. Does the shark bow tonight, or bare its teeth?*

### 1 · COLD OPEN — A Meeting That Shouldn't Exist (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a morna guitar and a candombe drum, far apart, then drifting toward each other.]**
**[ANIMATION: Atlanta in blue and sky-blue; morna and candombe music swirling together in the concourses.]**

The final matchday of Group H brings a meeting of worlds that should not exist. Cape Verde — 590,000 people on volcanic Atlantic islands — face Uruguay, two-time world champions and the spiritual home of *garra charrúa*, the relentless, never-surrender soul of South American football. *(beat)* Morna meets candombe. The smallest nation in the tournament meets one of its oldest kings.

**[SUPPORTERS — La Celeste: "¡U-ru-guay! ¡U-ru-guay!"]**

### 2 · EVERYTHING THEY FIGHT AGAINST (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: candombe drums, proud and heavy.]**
**[ANIMATION: Valverde with a Champions League medal for every finger; ghost-faces of Francescoli, Forlán, Suárez behind him; Núñez restless on the touchline.]**

Ryan Mendes looks across at Federico Valverde and sees everything Cape Verde is fighting against — a footballing culture that produced Francescoli, Forlán, Suárez. Darwin Núñez stretches with the restless energy of a man who knows one moment of chaos is all he needs; his runs create space for everyone. *A dynasty of garra, against an island that has none of the history and all of the hunger.*

### 3 · TOO SMALL, TOO SLOW, TOO FAR (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: the morna guitar answers, defiant.]**
**[ANIMATION: Garry Rodrigues, Logan Costa, Steven Fortes — each with a flash of being told "no" — now standing tall in blue.]**

But Cape Verde did not cross the Atlantic to be star-struck. Garry Rodrigues, Logan Costa, Steven Fortes — every one of them has been called too small, too slow, from too small a country. Ryan Mendes has 65-plus caps, *each one a rebellion against the idea that only big nations belong on this stage.*

**[SUPPORTERS — island corner: "Ca-bo Ver-de! Ca-bo Ver-de!"]**

### 4 · THE 81ST MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: garra fury builds — then total silence on the strike.]**
**[ANIMATION: Núñez on the edge of the box; the rocket toward the top corner; then 35-year-old Mendes, of all people, hurling his body across the line; the deflection looping over.]**

81st minute. Núñez receives on the edge of the box and shoots — the ball rockets toward the top corner. But Ryan Mendes, the 35-year-old *forward*, has tracked all the way back and throws his body in front of it. **The deflection loops over the bar.** **[SUPPORTERS: an island gasp, then a roar.]** Núñez stares at Mendes. Mendes stares back. *There is respect in that look — the respect of warriors who recognise each other.*

### 5 · THE REAL MAGIC (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: morna and candombe begin to share a single groove.]**
**[ANIMATION: the panther and the old shark, breathing hard, nodding once.]**

The World Cup's magic is not in the predictable victories of the powerful. *(beat)* It is in moments like this — when a nation of half a million refuses to kneel before two-time world champions, and a striker worth a fortune nods in respect to a 35-year-old who blocked his shot with his ribs.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the two rhythms resolve into one warm chord.]**
**[ANIMATION: blue and sky-blue shirts swapping at the whistle; the Atlantic and the Río de la Plata on one map.]**

The sharks and the charrúas, bound by a single block in the 81st minute. *(beat)* **And the question rides the night air over Atlanta: when the smallest nation on earth refuses to bow to the champions — is the magic of the World Cup in who finally wins, or in the simple, defiant fact that the giant was made to fear at all?** Tonight, an old shark bared its teeth. The kings will not forget the look in its eyes.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "¡U-ru-guay!" … "Ca-bo Ver-de!"]**
