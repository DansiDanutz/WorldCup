# Senegal vs Norway — The King of Teranga Meets the Viking
*Group I · Matchday 3 · June 22, 2026 · Levi's Stadium, Santa Clara*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** sabar drums against Viking war-horns → West African fire vs Scandinavian cold → silence on the 67th-minute through ball → a shot inches wide → both themes resolving into a tender chord on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Lions of Teranga — *"Sé-né-gal!"*, sabar drums, *"Lions de la Te-ran-ga!"*; Norway — *"Hei-a Nor-ge!"*, Viking war-horns
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *A man who walked 400km with rolled-up socks for a ball, against a Viking who breaks scoring records for fun. Only one advances. So what, exactly, does the other one carry home — and is it really the lesser prize?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Fire and Frost, Again (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: sabar drums and Viking war-horns enter from opposite sides.]**
**[ANIMATION: the Casamance heat shimmering; a cold Norwegian fjord; the two landscapes meeting at the centre circle.]**

Levi's Stadium hosts a collision as distinct as the continents that built it. Senegal — the rhythm and fire of West Africa — against Norway — the precision and power of Scandinavia. Sadio Mané against Erling Haaland. The King of Teranga against the Viking goal machine. *(beat)* Two nations who have never met in a World Cup, playing for a place in the knockout rounds. Only one walks on.

**[SUPPORTERS — Lions of Teranga: "Sé-né-gal! Sé-né-gal!"]**

### 2 · NOTHING LEFT TO PROVE (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: sabar drums, dignified and warm.]**
**[ANIMATION: a young Mané practising with a ball of rolled-up socks; then the trophies, the hospital, the school.]**

Mané, at 34, carries himself with the grace of a man with nothing left to prove and everything left to give. From Bambali — where he couldn't afford a real ball and trained with rolled-up socks — to the World Cup: the sport's greatest underdog story. Champions League, Premier League, two Africa Cups, a hospital, a school. *"I do not need luxury cars or big castles,"* he said, accepting an award for humanitarian work. Beside him, Koulibaly organises the line like a man who has captained both club and country.

### 3 · THE VIKING (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: Viking war-horns and cold percussion.]**
**[ANIMATION: Haaland, 6'4", ice in his eyes; Ødegaard conducting; Sørloth towering at the back post.]**

Haaland stands at the other end — 6'4" of pure goalscoring instinct, 120-plus goals, the most prolific forward in Premier League history, finishing cold and devastating. Martin Ødegaard supplies the creativity that unlocks the locked; Alexander Sørloth offers a different, towering threat. *Fire against frost, one more time.*

**[SUPPORTERS — Norwegian end: "Hei-a Nor-ge!"]**

### 4 · THE 67TH MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: drums and horns build — then silence as the ball runs.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mané, tiring, eyes still burning; the perfectly weighted through ball; Jackson's one touch and shot; the keeper sprawled; the ball sailing inches wide.]**

67th minute. Mané collects in midfield — 34 years of sprinting take their toll, but his eyes still burn with the fire of the boy who walked 400 kilometres to chase a dream. He slides a perfectly weighted through ball to Nicolas Jackson. One touch. One shot. The Norwegian keeper, sprawled on the turf, watches it sail *inches wide.* **[SUPPORTERS: a Senegalese groan, then a roar of belief.]** Jackson falls to his knees. Mané jogs over, lifts him up, whispers in his ear — and the cameras catch him smiling.

### 5 · WHAT THE LOSER CARRIES (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: sabar and horns soften toward each other.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mané with his arm around Jackson; the Viking watching, almost respectful.]**

This is what the World Cup does. It brings together a man from a Senegalese village who built a hospital and a Norwegian Viking who breaks records, and asks them to fight for the same patch of grass. *(beat)* Only one can advance. The other goes home with a story worth telling forever — and tonight, watching Mané lift a fallen teammate, you wonder which is truly the greater prize.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the two themes meet in one tender, unresolved chord.]**
**[ANIMATION: the knockout-round door glowing for one team; the other walking off with heads high.]**

The king and the Viking, one door between them. *(beat)* **And the question lingers as the drums and horns fade together: when one man advances and the other carries home only a story — the rolled-up socks, the hospital, the smile for a fallen teammate — is the World Cup really won by the one who walks through the door? Or has Mané, win or lose, already won the only thing worth winning?** Inches kept the door shut tonight. The smile said it didn't matter.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Sé-né-gal!" … "Hei-a Nor-ge!"]**
