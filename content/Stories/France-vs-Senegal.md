# France vs Senegal — The Empire and the Lion
*Group I · Matchday 1 · June 12, 2026 · SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** the ghost of a 2002 anthem → French orchestral grandeur vs Senegalese sabar drums → silence on the 56th-minute tackle → a counter surging → both themes meeting on the question of history
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Les Bleus — *"Allez les Bleus!"*, *"La Marseillaise"*; Lions of Teranga — *"Sé-né-gal! Sé-né-gal!"*, sabar drums, *"Lions de la Te-ran-ga!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *In 2002 the colony beat the empire on opening night and changed history. Twenty-four years on, the ghosts of Seoul still linger. Do the Lions shock the world again — or does the empire finally settle the account?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Ghost of Seoul (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a faint, ghostly echo of a 2002 anthem.]**
**[ANIMATION: grainy 2002 footage — Papa Bouba Diop wheeling away after the goal that beat France — dissolving into SoFi's floodlights.]**

SoFi Stadium trembles under the weight of history. France and Senegal have met only once when it truly mattered — the opening match of the 2002 World Cup, when Senegal, a former French colony, beat the reigning world champions 1–0 in one of the greatest upsets ever. Papa Bouba Diop's goal became a symbol of African football's defiance. *(beat)* Twenty-four years later, the ghosts of Seoul still linger in the dark.

**[SUPPORTERS — Lions of Teranga, sabar drums: "Sé-né-gal! Sé-né-gal!"]**

### 2 · LE PRINCE DE BONDY (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: French orchestral grandeur.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mbappé at the centre circle; a speed-gun flashing 38 km/h as he blurs forward.]**

Kylian Mbappé — "Le Prince de Bondy" — stands at the centre circle with the calm of a man who scored a hat-trick in a World Cup final and still lost. 56 goals in 85 caps, 12 World Cup goals, 38 km/h of pace, surgical intelligence. He grew up with Ronaldo posters on his walls; now he wears the same Real Madrid number 10. *The empire's prince.*

### 3 · THE KING OF TERANGA (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: sabar drums swell, warm and proud.]**
**[ANIMATION: a young Mané walking 400km to Dakar with a pair of boots; then the hospital, school, and mosque he built in Bambali.]**

Sadio Mané — "The King of Teranga," "Dakar Express" — stares back. He grew up in tiny Bambali and travelled 400 kilometres to Dakar with nothing but boots and a dream. Champions League, Premier League, two Africa Cups, his nation's all-time top scorer. He has built a hospital, a school, a mosque in his village. *"I do not need luxury cars or big castles,"* he once said. *"I prefer that my people have a little of what life has given me."*

### 4 · THE 56TH MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: grandeur and drums collide — then silence on the tackle.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mbappé's burst down the left; Mané, 34, tracking all the way back; the perfectly timed slide; Jackson springing the counter.]**

56th minute. Mbappé takes it on the left and accelerates — that devastating burst that leaves the world's best grasping at air. But Mané, even at 34, tracks back with the relentless work rate of his whole career. He catches him — barely — and dispossesses him with a perfect slide. **[SUPPORTERS: the crowd gasps.]** Mané springs up, drives forward, and finds Nicolas Jackson flying down the left. *The counter is on. France's defence scrambles.*

### 5 · DEEPER THAN BORDERS (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the two themes intertwine, charged with history.]**
**[ANIMATION: a colonial-era map dissolving into two modern flags standing as equals on the grass.]**

This is not just a football match. It is a story of empire and independence, of a colonizer and the colonized, of a bond deeper than borders. *(beat)* In 2002, Senegal shocked the world. In 2026, the Lion looks the empire dead in the eye and intends to do it again.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the 2002 ghost-anthem rises one last time, unresolved.]**
**[ANIMATION: Diop's old celebration flickers behind the live match, then fades into Mané's eyes.]**

The prince against the king. The empire against the lion. *(beat)* **And the ghost of Seoul asks its question across twenty-four years: when the colonized once beat the colonizer and rewrote what was possible — is history a thing that repeats, or a debt the empire has come all this way to finally collect?** The drums of Teranga answer the Marseillaise. Somewhere between them, a ghost is still waiting to see.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Allez les Bleus!" … "Sé-né-gal!"]**
