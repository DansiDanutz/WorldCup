# Brazil vs Haiti — The Beautiful Game and the Unbreakable Spirit
*Group C · Matchday 2 · June 16, 2026*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** lone rara horn (Haiti) under a soft samba → joyful Brazilian rhythm building → goals scored to rising drums → sudden silence, then a single triumphant horn on Haiti's goal → both fading into one heartbeat on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Brazil — *"Bra-sil! Bra-sil!"*, samba surdo; Les Grenadiers — *"A-yi-ti! A-yi-ti!"*, rara horns and tanbou drums
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *The greatest football nation in history against a country that has survived earthquakes, collapse, and exile. What is a single goal worth — when one side counts them by the dozen, and the other counts them as proof they still exist?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Supermarket and the Scout (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a lone rara horn, far off.]**
**[ANIMATION: a young Duckens Nazon stacking supermarket shelves; a scout watches from the aisle.]**

Duckens Nazon was working in a supermarket when a scout first saw him play. He is not embarrassed by it — he wears it like armour. Haiti, the first Caribbean nation to reach a World Cup in 1974, the first independent Black republic in the hemisphere, knows more about refusing to surrender than any team in this tournament. *(beat)* Tonight they face five-time champions, twenty-three straight World Cups, the only nation present at every tournament since 1930. On paper, it is no contest. *But Haiti has never read the paper.*

**[SUPPORTERS — rara horns blare: "A-yi-ti!"]**

### 2 · THE GHOST OF SANON (0:55–1:40)
**[MUSIC: a reverent, minor-key motif.]**
**[ANIMATION: grainy 1974 footage — Emmanuel Sanon scoring against Italy — dissolving into Nazon's eyes.]**

In 1974, Emmanuel Sanon scored against Italy at the World Cup — a goal that echoed across the Caribbean and told small nations they were allowed to dream. Every Haitian player knows that story. Derrick Étienne Jr., who chose Haiti over the United States, carries it in every touch. *The ghost of one goal can haunt a nation for fifty years — in the best possible way.*

### 3 · THE BEAUTIFUL GAME (1:40–2:25)
**[MUSIC: samba opens up, warm and free.]**
**[ANIMATION: Brazil's triangles and diagonals, choreographed by music more than tactics; Neymar grinning.]**

Brazil's ball moves in triangles that look composed rather than coached. Vinícius dances past Carlens Arcus; Raphinha tortures the right; and Neymar — in perhaps his final World Cup — plays with the freedom of a man who has won everything and now plays purely for joy.

### 4 · THE FLOOD (2:25–3:15)
**[MUSIC: drums build with each goal.]**
**[ANIMATION: three goals in montage — Vini's strike, Neymar's bending free kick, Raphinha's finish.]**

23rd minute, Vini scores. 34th, Neymar curls a free kick that bends like a question mark. 41st, Raphinha makes it three. **[SUPPORTERS: "Bra-sil! Bra-sil!" in waves.]** The match looks finished. The yellow tide rolls on, unstoppable. *And then the unbreakable does something the favelas will talk about for years.*

### 5 · THE GOAL THAT WASN'T ABOUT THE SCORE (3:15–4:25)
**[MUSIC: cut to silence — then a single rara horn, rising into a full celebration.]**
**[ANIMATION: Nazon drives past one, past two, and strikes from the edge; the net bulges; he slides on his knees, arms wide.]**

First-half stoppage time. Nazon picks it up on halfway. Not fast by Brazilian standards. Not skilful by Brazilian standards. *But Haitian — and Haitian means unbreakable.* He drives past one defender, past another, and lashes it into the top corner. Alisson does not move. **[SUPPORTERS: rara horns scream; the neutral fans rise to applaud.]** A nation that has survived earthquakes just scored against the gods of the game.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: samba and rara fold into one slow heartbeat.]**
**[ANIMATION: final whistle 4–1; Neymar's late tap-in barely celebrated; the camera holds on the dancing Haitian bench.]**

Brazil win 4–1 — Neymar adds a fourth he hardly celebrates. Because the story was never the scoreline. *(beat)* **When one team counts goals by the dozen and the other counts a single goal as proof that it still exists — which goal, truly, was worth more tonight?** Some victories are measured in goals. Others are measured in spirit. And spirit, it turns out, does not appear on the scoreboard at all.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading: "A-yi-ti! A-yi-ti!" still dancing into the dark.]**
