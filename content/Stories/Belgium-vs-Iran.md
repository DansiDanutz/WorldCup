# Belgium vs Iran — The Assassin and the Architect
*Group G · Matchday 2 · June 16, 2026 · Hard Rock Stadium, Miami*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** Florida heat-haze shimmer → a precise architect's theme vs a coiled assassin's motif → silence on the 62nd-minute save → a surgical counter surging → both themes refusing their roles on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Red Devils — *"Allez les Dia-bles Rou-ges!"*, *"Bel-gi-um!"*; Team Melli — *"I-ran! I-ran!"*, Persian drums and chants
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *One nation has every resource and no trophy; the other has nothing but pride and refuses to know its place. When the architect of chances meets the assassin who needs only one — who refuses their appointed role tonight?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Everything to Prove (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a Florida heat-haze shimmer over a low, tense drone.]**
**[ANIMATION: the Miami sun beating down; two teams stepping into the glare, neither blinking.]**

Miami trembles under the Florida heat as two teams with everything to prove walk into the afternoon sun. Belgium, stung by their opening result, need a response. Iran — the perpetual underdogs of Asian football — carry the fierce pride of 88 million people who believe this team, perhaps more than any before it, can shock the world. *(beat)* One side has all the resources. The other refuses to accept its appointed place. Tonight, somebody's certainty breaks.

**[SUPPORTERS — Iranian end: "I-ran! I-ran!"]**

### 2 · THE ASSASSIN (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: a coiled, patient motif — an assassin waiting.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mehdi Taremi still at the centre circle; a ghost-replay of his bicycle kick against Chelsea arcs behind him.]**

Mehdi Taremi — "The Iranian Ronaldo," "Taremi the Terrible" — stands at the centre circle with the quiet intensity of a man who once scored a bicycle kick against Chelsea in the Champions League. He is 33, a late bloomer who worked at a gas station in Bushehr before football saved him. *He needs one chance. He has always only ever needed one.*

### 3 · THE ARCHITECT (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: a precise, blueprint-like theme.]**
**[ANIMATION: Kevin De Bruyne scanning the field like a general; passing lanes light up at his glance.]**

Across from him, Kevin De Bruyne — the man Guardiola called "one of the best I have ever seen in my life" — adjusts his armband and reads the battlefield. *(beat)* Iran's players have always performed under extraordinary pressure — scrutiny, scarce resources, a federation in turmoil — and still they reached three straight World Cups and nearly knocked out Portugal in 2018. Belgium have every resource imaginable and have never converted it into the ultimate prize. *Both know exactly what Taremi can do. Knowing is not stopping.*

**[SUPPORTERS — Belgian bloc: "Allez les Dia-bles Rou-ges!"]**

### 4 · THE 62ND MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: heat shimmer — then silence on the save — then a surgical surge.]**
**[ANIMATION: Taremi peeling into impossible space; the touch, the swing of that right foot; Courtois full-stretch onto the post; the rebound springing De Bruyne's counter.]**

62nd minute. Taremi peels off his marker, finding a pocket between the Belgian centre-backs that should not exist. The ball arrives — one touch to set, one swing of that lethal right foot — but Courtois, "The Wall," who once made nine saves in a Champions League final, reads it early and dives full-stretch to push it onto the post. **[SUPPORTERS: a gasp, then a roar.]** The rebound falls to De Bruyne, who launches a counter of surgical precision — Lukaku sprinting, Doku flying — and suddenly Iran's defence is a wire about to snap.

### 5 · REFUSING THE ROLE (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: architect and assassin themes both push against their own limits.]**
**[ANIMATION: Belgian gold and Iranian red tangled, neither willing to be written off.]**

This is a story of two nations refusing their appointed roles. Belgium will not go quietly into the good night of golden-generation obituaries. Iran will not accept that they do not belong. *(beat)* When the whistle echoes through the Miami heat, one World Cup stays alive — and one hangs by the thinnest thread.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: both themes hold, neither surrendering to the other.]**
**[ANIMATION: the heat-haze swallows the pitch; two silhouettes — architect and assassin — still standing.]**

The architect builds a hundred chances. The assassin needs one. *(beat)* **And the riddle simmers in the Florida air: when a team with everything refuses to die, and a team with nothing refuses to know its place — which refusal is the stronger, the fear of an ending or the hunger for a beginning?** Tonight, the post and a fingertip kept the answer hidden. Somebody's thread is fraying.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "I-ran! I-ran!" … "Bel-gi-um!"]**
