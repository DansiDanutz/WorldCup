# Egypt vs New Zealand — The Pharaoh Meets the All Whites
*Group G · Matchday 3 · June 21, 2026 · NRG Stadium, Houston*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a blade-bright tension theme → Egyptian ney/ultras vs Pacific haka → silence on the 89th-minute header → an exhale, a heartbreak → both themes left hanging on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Pharaohs — *"Masr! Masr!"*, *"Mo Sa-lah!"*, ultras drums; All Whites — *"Ka ma-te! Ka ma-te!"* (haka), *"All Whites!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *A king playing what may be his last World Cup match, against a man chasing the one goal he has never scored. The tournament makes fairy tales and destroys them with equal indifference. Tonight, whose survives?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Blade Falls (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a single bright, blade-like tension note.]**
**[ANIMATION: pharaonic masks in one stand, a Māori haka in another; the Group G table glowing — win or oblivion.]**

The final matchday of Group G arrives like a blade. Egypt and New Zealand — two nations that have never met in a World Cup — face off knowing victory may mean survival and defeat means oblivion. Pharaonic masks sit beside Māori haka, a collision only the World Cup could stage. *(beat)* For one of them, the dream ends tonight.

**[SUPPORTERS — Egyptian end: "Masr! Masr!"]**

### 2 · THE KING'S LAST MATHEMATICS (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: the Egyptian ney, urgent now.]**
**[ANIMATION: Salah's eyes; behind him Omar Marmoush mirroring his stance — the torch mid-pass.]**

Mohamed Salah knows the mathematics. At 33, this is almost certainly his last World Cup match — unless Egypt advance. The man who scored 32 in a 38-game Premier League season, who rode five hours by bus from Nagrig as a boy, *will not go gently.* Beside him, Omar Marmoush — €70 million, who grew up idolising Salah — is Egypt's future. The Pharaoh, passing the torch even as he reaches for one more flame.

### 3 · THE FIRST GOAL HE'S NEVER SCORED (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: the haka pulse, deep and certain.]**
**[ANIMATION: Chris Wood at the far post, timing a career's worth of crosses; a silver fern over his heart.]**

Chris Wood watches from the other end — New Zealand's captain and all-time top scorer, the plumber-turned-record-breaker, 1.91m of aerial dominance. His 35-plus international goals make him the most prolific All White in history. *But a World Cup goal — his first — would mean more than all of them combined.* He has scored against the best in the Premier League. Salah does not frighten him.

**[SUPPORTERS — All Whites haka: "Ka ma-te! Ka ma-te!"]**

### 4 · THE 89TH MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: ney and haka build — then silence on the leap.]**
**[ANIMATION: Egypt 2–1 up; a New Zealand corner; Wood drifting to the far post; the swing-in; the rise; the header drifting inches wide.]**

89th minute. Egypt lead 2–1, but New Zealand have a corner. Wood positions himself at the far post, his timing honed across a whole career of attacking crosses. The ball swings in. Wood climbs above the Egyptian defence — connects — *and the header drifts inches wide.* **[SUPPORTERS: a Pacific gasp, then silence.]** He falls to the turf, hands over his face. On the halfway line, Salah exhales slowly. His World Cup lives another day.

### 5 · NO FAIRY TALES PROMISED (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the haka softens into something elegiac.]**
**[ANIMATION: Wood lifting himself off the grass; Egyptian fans roaring; the inches between the post and the header glowing.]**

The World Cup does not care about fairy tales. It creates them and destroys them with equal indifference. *(beat)* Egypt's Pharaoh lives to fight another day. New Zealand's All Whites return to their islands with hearts broken and heads held high — undone not by a lack of courage, but by inches.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: ney and haka hold one shared, unresolved note.]**
**[ANIMATION: the goalmouth where the header drifted wide; the gap between fairy tale and heartbreak measured in inches.]**

A king survives; a plumber's first World Cup goal stays, forever, the one that got away. *(beat)* **And the tournament's coldest truth lingers in the Houston night: the distance between immortality and heartbreak is not talent, not courage, not destiny — it is a few inches of air, dealt out by a hand that does not care who deserves it.** Tonight, the inches chose the Pharaoh. They will choose someone else tomorrow.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Mo Sa-lah!" … a quiet "All Whites…"]**
