# Egypt vs Iran — The Desert Derby
*Group G · Matchday 2 · June 16, 2026 · Houston*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** an ancient desert wind over twin themes (Egyptian ney / Persian setar) → ultras drums vs Team Melli chants → silence on the 71st-minute strike → a nation's weeping swell → both civilizations' themes folding into the wind on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Pharaohs — *"Masr! Masr!"*, *"Mo Sa-lah!"*, ultras drums; Team Melli — *"I-ran! I-ran!"*, Persian chant and clap
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Two ancient civilizations, two Muslim heroes, two nations that have chased World Cup respect for decades. When their kings finally cross paths in the desert — whose shadow stretches longer across the sand?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Two Ancient Worlds (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: an ancient desert wind; a ney and a setar answering each other across it.]**
**[ANIMATION: pyramids and Persepolis dissolve into the floodlights of Houston; sand drifting across the pitch.]**

Two ancient civilizations, separated by a thousand miles of desert and centuries of history, meet on a football pitch in Houston. Egypt and Iran — giants of North African and West Asian football — rarely cross paths, and when they do, the electricity is undeniable. *(beat)* This is not just a match. It is a cultural collision of two football-obsessed nations that have spent decades chasing the same elusive thing: respect on the world stage.

**[SUPPORTERS — Egyptian ultras drums: "Masr! Masr!"]**

### 2 · THE KING OF NAGRIG (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: the Egyptian ney rises, regal and warm.]**
**[ANIMATION: a young Salah on a five-hour bus to Cairo; the bus becomes the tunnel he now walks through as a king.]**

Mohamed Salah carries the dreams of 110 million people. He grew up in Nagrig, riding five hours by bus to reach training. Now he is Liverpool's greatest ever scorer, a three-time Golden Boot winner, and at 33 he knows this is his last chance to stand beside the pharaohs of old. *A king, one tournament from immortality.*

### 3 · THE GAS-STATION GHOST (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: the Persian setar answers, intense and proud.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mehdi Taremi warming up; a flash of the Bushehr gas station where he once worked; the Chelsea bicycle kick arcing behind him.]**

Across the pitch, Mehdi Taremi — "The Iranian Ronaldo" — warms up with quiet intensity. His journey from a Bushehr gas station to the Champions League is a story Hollywood would reject as too implausible. *(beat)* The irony is rich: Salah and Taremi, two Muslim heroes who built empires in Europe, now carrying nations that see football as identity itself. And in the stands, Iranian women cheer freely in an American stadium — for some, for the very first time — a wall of sound that shakes the concrete.

**[SUPPORTERS — Team Melli end: "I-ran! I-ran!"]**

### 4 · THE 71ST MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: drums and chant lock — then total silence on the cut-in.]**
**[ANIMATION: Salah collecting on the right; the trademark cut inside; the left foot; Taremi watching from the centre circle, helpless; the ball bending toward the far post.]**

71st minute. Salah receives on the right wing and cuts inside — that devastating, signature move — left foot cocked. Taremi watches from the centre circle, knowing exactly what is coming and powerless to stop it. The ball bends through the Houston air toward the far post. The Iranian keeper dives. **The ball kisses the inside of the post and ricochets across the line.** **[SUPPORTERS: "Mo Sa-lah!" erupts; a nation weeps.]** Salah sinks to his knees.

### 5 · TWO SHADOWS ON THE SAND (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: ney and setar entwine, mournful and grand.]**
**[ANIMATION: Salah on his knees; Taremi standing, watching, a warrior who understands.]**

Two desert civilizations, one shared dream — and tonight, the Pharaoh's shadow stretches across the pitch while the other king stands in it. *(beat)* But shadows shift with the sun, and the desert has always known that today's giant is tomorrow's ruin.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the two themes fold back into the ancient wind.]**
**[ANIMATION: the sand drifts back across the pitch, covering both kings' footprints equally.]**

110 million Egyptians rise as one. *(beat)* **And the desert keeps its oldest question: when two ancient peoples send their greatest sons to meet at last — is it the king who scores who is remembered, or the one who, standing in the shadow, vows that the sand will shift again?** Tonight the post chose Egypt. The wind, as ever, makes no promises.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Masr! Masr!" … "I-ran! I-ran!"]**
