# Canada vs Bosnia and Herzegovina — The Bosnian Diamond and the Maple Leaf
*Group B · Matchday 1 · June 11, 2026 · BMO Field, Toronto*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Music arc:** ticking watch (time) → youthful synth-pulse for Davies / weathered cello for Džeko → the two themes trading blows → silence on the chances → both themes resolving into one on the closing handshake

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Two Ages (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a single ticking watch under a low drone.]**
**[ANIMATION: split tunnel — Alphonso Davies bouncing on his toes; Edin Džeko, silver-haired, perfectly still.]**

For only the second time in history, Canada opens a World Cup at home — and the country that gave the world ice hockey is learning that football can freeze the blood just as cold. At one mouth of the tunnel: Alphonso Davies, 24, a refugee born in a Liberian camp who became Canada's greatest footballer. At the other: Edin Džeko, 40, silver in his hair, 65 goals for a nation that did not exist when he was born. *(beat)* One man is lightning. One is memory. Only one can own tonight.

**[SUPPORTERS — red wall rising: "Oh Ca-na-da!"]**

### 2 · THE LIGHTNING (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: a bright, restless synth pulse.]**
**[ANIMATION: Davies blurring down the left, defenders frozen mid-stride.]**

Davies is a Champions League winner with Bayern Munich, a man whose acceleration seems to break physics. Beside him, Jonathan David — the silent assassin who scores without ever seeming to try. They are young, fearless, and carry a nation that has never escaped a World Cup group. *The fear they won't say aloud: that youth, left unchecked, burns too fast and too soon.*

### 3 · THE DIAMOND (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: a weathered, noble cello.]**
**[ANIMATION: Džeko in close-up; ghost-jerseys of Wolfsburg, City, Roma, Inter flicker past.]**

Across the grass, the "Bosnian Diamond." Wolfsburg, Manchester City, Roma, Inter, Fenerbahçe — and 65 goals for 3.5 million people. This is his last dance, and Džeko intends to sparkle one final time. Beside him, Miralem Pjanić maps dimensions of the pitch no one else can see. *Age has stolen Džeko's sprint — but never his timing.*

**[SUPPORTERS — Bosnian corner: "Zma-je-vi! Zma-je-vi!"]**

### 4 · TWO CHANCES, TWO ERAS (2:35–4:10)
**[MUSIC: the synth and the cello trade blows; then silence on each strike.]**

31st minute. Davies takes it on the left and accelerates — violent, almost frightening. Sead Kolašinac, a warrior, is two steps behind and stays there. Davies reaches the byline, cuts it back to Cyle Larin, six yards out — and Nikola Vasilj throws out a reflex from another world. **[SUPPORTERS: a gasp.]** Bosnia survives.
*(beat)* Second half, and it belongs to the old man. Pjanić's free kick curls in like a surgeon's scalpel; Džeko rises — timing that hasn't aged a day — and powers it goalward. The stadium falls silent. The ball flashes wide by inches. **[MUSIC: the cello holds one aching note.]** Forty years old, and the margins are still cruel.

### 5 · THE HANDSHAKE (4:10–4:40)
**[MUSIC: the two themes begin, at last, to harmonise.]**
**[ANIMATION: Davies and Džeko swap shirts at the whistle, youth and age reflected in each other.]**

A draw that satisfies neither and eliminates no one. At the whistle, Davies and Džeko exchange shirts — the lightning and the memory, bound by simple respect.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:40–5:00)
**[MUSIC: ticking watch returns, slower now.]**

So the oldest question goes unanswered for one more night. *(beat)* **Speed will get its rematch. Wisdom will get its farewell. But which one the World Cup ultimately rewards — that secret BMO Field kept to itself tonight.** The watch keeps ticking. For one of these men, it is ticking louder.

**[SUPPORTERS — overlapping, fading: "Ca-na-da!" … "Bos-na!"]**
