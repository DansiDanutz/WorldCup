# Argentina vs Algeria — The Echo of the Desert Fox
*Group J · Matchday 1 · June 11, 2026*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a lone bandoneón over a desert wind → Argentine tango vs Algerian darbuka → silence on the 83rd-minute curl → the ball kissing the post → both themes folding into the question of a forty-year wound
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** La Albiceleste — *"¡Va-mos Ar-gen-ti-na!"*, *"Mu-cha-chos, a-ho-ra nos vol-vi-mos a i-lu-sio-nar…"*; Desert Foxes — *"Vi-va l'Al-gé-rie!"* (One, two, three!), darbuka
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *The GOAT's last dance against the son of the desert who chose the desert over the throne. Algeria has carried a forty-year wound since the Shame of Gijón. Tonight, against the heirs of the Hand of God — does magic finally open the door, or do some wounds never close?*

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Two Magicians, One Page (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a lone bandoneón breathes over a faint desert wind.]**
**[ANIMATION: Messi wrapping the armband; across the pitch, Mahrez's quiet smile; two ink-wells, one blue, one green, dripping onto the same blank page.]**

The stadium hums with a tension that has nothing to do with the scoreline. On one side: Lionel Messi, La Pulga, wrapping the armband for what may be his final World Cup dance. On the other: Riyad Mahrez — Algeria's son, who conquered the Premier League and chose the desert over the throne. *(beat)* Two magicians, two stories written in different ink — about to share the same page.

**[SUPPORTERS — La Albiceleste, low and swelling: "Mu-cha-chos…"]**

### 2 · THE WEIGHT OF HEAVEN (0:55–1:40)
**[MUSIC: an Argentine tango, proud and aching.]**
**[ANIMATION: Messi's eyes; Dibu Martínez prowling his line like a wolf; Julián Álvarez buzzing.]**

Messi's eyes carry the weight of a nation that has already touched heaven. Beside him, Emiliano "Dibu" Martínez prowls his goalmouth like a wolf scenting blood; Julián "La Araña" Álvarez hunts in packs. *Champions, carrying the impossible expectation of staying champions.*

### 3 · THE SON OF THE DESERT (1:40–2:30)
**[MUSIC: Algerian darbuka and a curling string line.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mahrez's quiet smile from the Leicester miracle; Bennacer, tiny, metronomic; Gouiri silky and hungry.]**

Across the grass, Mahrez wears that quiet smile — the one from when Leicester shocked the world, when City conquered Europe. Ismaël Bennacer waits in midfield with 90% pass completion in his bones; Amine Gouiri stands ready to prove Algeria's golden generation is no mirage. *(beat)* This is the Mediterranean speaking to the Atlantic — and a wound that will not heal. Argentina's 1986 glory was built on one man's genius. Algeria's 1982 — the "Shame of Gijón" — was stolen by the cynicism of others. *For forty years, Algerian football has carried that scar. Tonight, they face the descendants of the Hand of God.*

**[SUPPORTERS — Desert Foxes: "Vi-va l'Al-gé-rie!"]**

### 4 · THE 83RD MINUTE (2:30–3:50)
**[MUSIC: everything drops to silence as the ball reaches his feet.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mahrez on the right; the signature cut inside everyone sees coming and no one stops; the curl rising, dipping; Dibu launching left, fingertips, the post.]**

83rd minute. The ball arrives at Mahrez's feet on the right. He cuts inside — that signature move, the one every defender knows is coming and nobody can stop. The curl rises, dips, and for a heartbeat the whole stadium holds its breath. Dibu Martínez launches to his left, fingers outstretched — **the ball kisses the post and spins away.** **[SUPPORTERS: a desert gasp, then a roar.]** Mahrez looks to the sky. *Some doors even magic cannot open.*

### 5 · WORTH THE FORTY-YEAR WAIT (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: tango and darbuka entwine, charged with history.]**
**[ANIMATION: 1982 Gijón ghosts beside 1986 Mexico ghosts, dissolving into the two living captains.]**

When the whistle blows, two teams will have written a chapter that history demanded. *(beat)* The desert fox and the GOAT, sharing a World Cup pitch at last — a meeting worth the forty-year wait, even if the old wound is still, somehow, open.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the bandoneón and the desert wind hold one unresolved chord.]**
**[ANIMATION: the post still trembling; Mahrez and Messi exchanging a glance across the grass.]**

A curl an inch wide. A wound forty years deep. *(beat)* **And the question rises with the desert wind: Algeria has waited a lifetime to face the heirs of the team that broke their hearts — so if even magic could not open the door tonight, does that mean some wounds are never meant to close, or only that the desert fox must wait a little longer for the door to swing?** Tonight the post chose Argentina. The echo of Gijón is still waiting for its answer.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "¡Va-mos Ar-gen-ti-na!" … "Vi-va l'Al-gé-rie!"]**
