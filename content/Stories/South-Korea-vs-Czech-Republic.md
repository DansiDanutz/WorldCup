# South Korea vs Czech Republic — The Last Waltz of Sonaldo

*Group A, Matchday 1 — June 11, 2026*

Son Heung-min touches the captain's armband and looks at the sky. This is his fourth World Cup. It will be his last. At 33, the Premier League's greatest Asian goalscorer knows that time is a river flowing in one direction, and he can feel the current pulling. Every touch, every sprint, every shot is now measured against the knowledge that there will be no next time. Against the Czech Republic, in the opening match of Group A, Sonaldo begins his farewell.

The Czechs understand farewells. Edin Dzeko waited twelve years between World Cups. Patrik Schick scored from the halfway line and then disappeared from the international stage for years before returning. The Czech football tradition is built on patience — waiting for the right moment, then striking with devastating precision.

Kim Min-jae, the "Monster," anchors the Korean defense. The man who conquered Serie A with Napoli's title win, who became one of Europe's most feared centre-backs, now faces Schick — a striker who thrives in the margins, who finds space where none exists, who chips penalties with the audacity of a man who has already calculated every possible outcome. Their duel is the match within the match.

In the 41st minute, the old magic. Son receives the ball on the left, cuts inside onto his right foot — the move that has beaten a thousand defenders — and strikes. The ball bends, dips, and nestles in the top corner. The South Korean bench erupts. Son doesn't celebrate wildly. He bows his head, touches the armband, and jogs back to the halfway line. There is work to do.

The Czechs respond in the second half. Schick drifts wide, loses Kim Min-jae for the first time all match, and receives Hlozek's pass in space. One touch to control, one swing of the boot — but Son's shadow, Hwang Hee-chan, tracks back with the desperation of a man who knows what this goal would mean. His tackle is perfect. The ball spins away.

When the final whistle blows, South Korea has three points and Son has a goal. In the land of the morning calm and the heart of Europe, a warrior's farewell begins with a victory. The river flows on, but for one night, Sonaldo made it stand still.
