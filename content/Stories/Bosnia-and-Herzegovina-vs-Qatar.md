# Bosnia and Herzegovina vs Qatar — The Dragon's Last Fire
*Group B · Matchday 2 · June 17, 2026 · Gillette Stadium, Boston*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a single ember-crackle and low cello → a slow-burning Balkan theme → desert oud rising in answer → strings swelling to the header → near-silence, just the ember, on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Zmajevi — *"Bos-na! Bos-na!"*, *"Zma-je-vi!"* (Dragons), a low terrace hum from Sarajevo; Al-Annabi — *"Yal-la Qa-tar!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *A fire that has burned for twenty years is almost out. Tonight it flares one more time. The question every Bosnian fears to ask — how bright does a dying flame burn before the dark?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The First Goal and the Last (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: an ember crackles over a low cello.]**
**[ANIMATION: 2007 sepia footage of a young Džeko scoring his first Bosnia goal melts into the floodlit present.]**

Edin Džeko has scored 65 goals for Bosnia and Herzegovina. The first came in 2007 — a 21-year-old with dreams bigger than his talent. The last might come tonight. The "Bosnian Diamond" has been the face of a nation that did not exist when he was born, a country that survived a war and rebuilt itself through football. *(beat)* Every fire has a final flare. Tonight, Sarajevo holds its breath to see if this is it.

**[SUPPORTERS — low terrace hum, then: "Zma-je-vi!"]**

### 2 · THE REBORN MAROON (0:55–1:40)
**[MUSIC: desert oud, defiant.]**
**[ANIMATION: Qatar passing in crisp Aspire triangles; the 2022 ghost-scoreboard burns away behind them.]**

Qatar arrive with their own story of rebirth. 2022 was a humiliation — zero points, one goal, the worst host record in history. But Al-Annabi has returned hardened, carrying Aspire Academy's technical polish and a burning need to prove that failure was an anomaly, not a verdict.

**[SUPPORTERS — maroon corner: "Yal-la Qa-tar!"]**

### 3 · THE GEOMETRY OF AGE (1:40–2:35)
**[MUSIC: the Balkan theme, patient, smouldering.]**
**[ANIMATION: Džeko drifting unseen between two markers; a faint geometric grid traces his angles.]**

At forty, Džeko can no longer sprint past anyone — and he never needed to. His game is geometry: reading space, anticipating movement, arriving a heartbeat before the rest. When Pjanić swings a corner, Džeko's timing is a clock that never stops. When young Ermedin Demirović makes a decoy run, Džeko is already in position, unnoticed, waiting. *The fire isn't in his legs. It's in his mind.*

### 4 · THE FLARE (2:35–3:45)
**[MUSIC: strings build, slow and inevitable — then the ember roars into flame.]**

44th minute. Pjanić's free kick from the right curves in like a question. Džeko attacks the space between two Qatari defenders, rises, and connects — a header carrying the weight of a twenty-year career. The ball flies in. **[SUPPORTERS: Sarajevo erupts — "Bos-na! Bos-na!"]** *(beat)* He does not sprint away. He stands still, closes his eyes, and lifts both arms to the sky. In the stands, fans who travelled from Mostar and Banja Luka weep.

### 5 · THE DRAGON DEFENDS (3:45–4:30)
**[MUSIC: tension; the oud presses, the cello holds the wall.]**
**[ANIMATION: Afif slicing passes; Almoez Ali forcing a sprawling Vasilj save; then Džeko, in his own box, heading clear.]**

Qatar surge. Afif finds gaps that shouldn't exist; Almoez Ali draws a flying save from Vasilj. But Bosnia hold. And in the 87th minute — the image of the night — a forty-year-old striker drops into his own penalty area to clear a corner, defending like a centre-back, because tonight means everything.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:30–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the ember crackle returns, alone.]**
**[ANIMATION: Džeko walks off; a single spark drifts up from the pitch and winks out.]**

Three points. His 66th international goal. *(beat)* Some fires burn brightest just before they go out — and no one in this stadium, not even the Diamond himself, can say whether tonight was the brightest flare or merely the warning before it. **The dragon breathed fire once more. How many breaths remain is a secret the dark keeps close.**

**[SUPPORTERS — fading into the night: "Zma-je-vi… Zma-je-vi…"]**
