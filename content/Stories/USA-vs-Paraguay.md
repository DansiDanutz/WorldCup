# USA vs Paraguay — The Captain's Homecoming
*Group D · Matchday 1 · June 12, 2026 · AT&T Stadium, Arlington*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** stadium-sized anthem swell → a quiet, watchful counter-theme for Paraguay → Texas-heat shimmer → tension building to the 78th-minute freeze → a single unresolved note on the closing question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** USA — *"U-S-A! U-S-A!"*, *"I believe that we will win!"*; Albirroja — *"¡Va-mos Al-bi-rro-ja!"*, *"¡Pa-ra-guay!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *A whole country is finally watching. But what happens when the weight of hosting collides with a team that has nothing to lose — and is not the slightest bit afraid?*

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Constellation (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a vast anthem swell, then it cuts to a single held breath.]**
**[ANIMATION: AT&T Stadium's lights blaze on like a constellation brought to earth; 80,000 red-white-and-blue scarves ripple to the rafters.]**

The lights of Arlington blaze like a constellation brought down to the prairie. Eighty thousand voices rise — the home nation's World Cup is finally here, and the air itself seems to vibrate. Four years of waiting have built to this single night. *(beat)* But somewhere across the halfway line stands a team that did not come to admire the lights. They came to switch them off.

**[SUPPORTERS — wall of sound: "U-S-A! U-S-A!"]**

### 2 · THE WEIGHT OF THE ARMBAND (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: a noble, slightly heavy theme.]**
**[ANIMATION: Christian Pulisic touches the captain's armband; ghost-frames of his 2022 knockout goal flicker behind him.]**

At the heart of it: Christian Pulisic, the captain carrying a continent. The AC Milan winger dragged American soccer through its darkest hours and its brightest mornings — from the goal that reached the 2022 knockouts to the armband that turned a prodigy into a leader. *The crown is heavy. Tonight he must wear it and run.*

### 3 · THE QUIET MEN (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: the watchful counter-theme, cool and patient.]**
**[ANIMATION: Miguel Almirón surveying a stadium he knows by heart; Julio Enciso bouncing on the halfway line, eyes alight.]**

Paraguay arrived as the quietest team in Group D — no hype, no headlines, just cold-eyed men forged in the crucible of CONMEBOL qualifying. Miguel Almirón knows these American grounds intimately: six years at Newcastle, a title in Atlanta. Beside him, Julio Enciso dances with the recklessness of youth. *They know Pulisic. They know the machine. And they are not afraid.*

**[SUPPORTERS — Albirroja corner: "¡Va-mos Al-bi-rro-ja!"]**

### 4 · THE 78TH MINUTE (2:35–3:45)
**[MUSIC: Texas-heat shimmer; percussion tightening like a fist.]**
**[ANIMATION: 1–1 on the board; sweat, heat-haze; Pulisic one-on-one with Alderete.]**

Picture the 78th minute. 1–1. The Texas heat presses down like a hand. Pulisic takes it on the left, one-on-one with Omar Alderete — feint left, shift right, instant, devastating. He drives in and slashes a cross to Balogun — but Gustavo Gómez hurls his body into the lane, a wall of Paraguayan defiance. **[SUPPORTERS: a gasp.]** The ball cannons out to Enciso, forty yards from goal, who looks up and sees the American line scrambling.

### 5 · THE LINE BETWEEN (3:45–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the two themes collide and hold, refusing to resolve.]**
**[ANIMATION: freeze-frame on Enciso's eyes as he loads the shot; the whole stadium suspended.]**

*(beat)* What happens next will write the opening chapter of Group D. In the crucible of a home World Cup, heroes are forged and dreams are shattered in the very same breath — and on this knife-edge, no one in eighty thousand can say which is coming.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: a single unresolved note, left hanging in the night.]**
**[ANIMATION: the floodlit constellation dims to one bright point, then black.]**

When the whistle blows in Arlington, the story will write itself. *(beat)* **But here is the question the host nation cannot answer on its grandest night: is it the team carrying a country's hopes that runs hardest — or the team carrying nothing at all?** The constellation burns over Texas. Somewhere beneath it, the freedom of having nothing to lose is the most dangerous weapon on the pitch.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "U-S-A!" … "¡Pa-ra-guay!"]**
