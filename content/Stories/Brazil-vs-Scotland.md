# Brazil vs Scotland — The Tartan Army Meets the Hexa
*Group C · Matchday 1 · June 12, 2026*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a lone bagpipe drone → samba rising against it → silence on the first goal → defiant pipes through Scotland's siege → samba crescendo on Vini's solo → the pipes refuse to stop on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Brazil — *"Bra-sil! Bra-sil!"*, samba surdo; Tartan Army — *"O Flower of Scotland…"*, *"Yes Sir, I Can Boogie!"*
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Twenty-eight years of waiting, and fate hands Scotland the very team that ended them last time. Does facing your oldest fear set you free — or break you all over again?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — The Cruel Joke (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a single bagpipe drone, lonely in the dark.]**
**[ANIMATION: 1998 sepia footage — Scotland losing to Brazil in the opener — bleeds into 2026 floodlights.]**

Scotland has waited twenty-eight years for this. Since 1998 — when they last reached a World Cup and lost the opener to Brazil — the Tartan Army has endured near-misses, playoff heartbreak, and the slow erosion of hope. *(beat)* Now they are back. And fate, with its cruel sense of humour, has handed them Brazil. Again. *The same nightmare, twenty-eight years older.*

**[SUPPORTERS — 20,000 strong: "O Flower of Scotland…"]**

### 2 · THE MOST DECORATED SON (0:55–1:40)
**[MUSIC: the pipes find a prouder key.]**
**[ANIMATION: Andy Robertson leading the line out; behind him McTominay, jaw set.]**

Andy Robertson — Premier League and Champions League winner, the most decorated Scot in a generation — leads them out. Behind him, Scott McTominay: Serie A Player of the Year, "the McTominator," whose bicycle kick against Denmark dragged Scotland here. They have stood on football's grandest stages. *But Scotland's DNA carries a curse — eight World Cups, never once past the group.*

### 3 · AN EMBARRASSMENT OF RICHES (1:40–2:25)
**[MUSIC: samba drums roll in over the drone.]**
**[ANIMATION: Vini, Neymar, Raphinha, Alisson in turn; Ancelotti still on the touchline.]**

Brazil answer with riches that border on cruelty: Vinícius, the most dangerous winger alive; Neymar, their all-time top scorer; Raphinha's relentless joy; Alisson, perhaps the planet's best keeper; and Ancelotti, the most decorated club coach in history, prowling the line.

### 4 · THE FEAR MADE REAL (2:25–3:20)
**[MUSIC: drone and drums collide — then silence on the goal.]**
**[ANIMATION: Vini and Robertson one-on-one; the stepover, the burst, the cross; Neymar's volley.]**

15th minute — the moment Scotland dreaded. Vini takes it on the left, Robertson three yards off. The Scot has faced him in the Premier League and survived; he knows the stepover, the burst, the cut inside. But knowing and stopping are different countries. Vini beats him for pace, crosses, and Neymar volleys home. **[SUPPORTERS: a samba roar.]** The oldest fear, made real.

### 5 · THE SIEGE, AND THE SOLO (3:20–4:25)
**[MUSIC: defiant pipes under a rising samba crescendo.]**
**[ANIMATION: McTominay striding past two; McGinn's shot; Alisson flying; then Vini's solo from halfway.]**

But Scotland do not crumble. McTominay drives forward; in the 39th, his long stride beats two and finds McGinn, whose shot draws a world-class Alisson save. **[SUPPORTERS: "Yes Sir, I Can Boogie!" defiant in the rain.]** *(beat)* The second half is a siege. 72nd minute: Vini collects on halfway and goes alone — past one, past two, past three — and finishes, leaving four Scots staring at each other. The 85th: Raphinha makes it three.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: samba fades; the pipes play on, alone, unbroken.]**
**[ANIMATION: 3–0 on the board; the stadium empties — except the Tartan Army, still singing.]**

Brazil win 3–0. And yet the Scottish fans stay long after the whistle, singing, refusing to leave. *(beat)* Twenty-eight years of waiting ended in defeat — but the Tartan Army knows the group has three matches, and **here is the riddle they carry into the night: they came face to face with their oldest fear, and it did not break them. So is the curse a wall… or a door they have simply never had the nerve to walk through?** The pipes do not answer. They just keep playing.

**[SUPPORTERS — undimmed: "O Flower of Scotland, when will we see your like again…"]**
