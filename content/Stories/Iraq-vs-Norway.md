# Iraq vs Norway — Zidane vs the Vikings
*Group I · Matchday 1 · June 12, 2026 · Stanford Stadium, California*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a wry, fated music-box over Iraqi maqam → maqam strings vs Viking war-horns → tension to the 23rd-minute break → a shot just over, a diaspora song rising → the music-box holding, amused, on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Lions of Mesopotamia — *"I-raq! I-raq!"*, *"U-sood Al-Ra-fi-dain!"*, diaspora drums and song; Norway — *"Hei-a Nor-ge!"*, Viking war-horns
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *A boy named Zidane leads a war-scarred nation against an army of Vikings. The World Cup keeps writing stories Hollywood would reject as too improbable. So why do we keep watching — and what is this one trying to tell us?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — A Storybook With a Wicked Humour (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a wry, fated music-box melody over a low maqam drone.]**
**[ANIMATION: an open storybook; its pages turn to reveal Zidane Iqbal facing a line of horned Viking silhouettes.]**

A boy named after a French legend leads Iraq onto the World Cup pitch against a nation of Vikings. If football is a storybook, this chapter was written by a poet with a wicked sense of humour. *(beat)* Stanford Stadium is the stage, and the narrative is irresistible: Zidane Iqbal, 23, born in England, against Erling Haaland, the 6'4" machine who has demolished every record in his path.

**[SUPPORTERS — Iraqi diaspora, drums and song: "U-sood Al-Ra-fi-dain!"]**

### 2 · THE STEAL OF THE CENTURY (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: Iraqi maqam strings, proud and intimate.]**
**[ANIMATION: young Iqbal training beside Ronaldo at United; then choosing the Iraqi shirt over the English one.]**

Iqbal's story is already extraordinary. Named after Zinedine Zidane by his football-obsessed father, he grew up in Manchester, trained beside Ronaldo at United, and chose Iraq — the land of his heritage — over England. One first-team appearance, then a move to FC Utrecht for just £850,000, a fee some United fans called *"the steal of the century."* His teammate Ali Al-Hamadi shares the story: *"We have a special bond because we both grew up in England. Zidane is like family."*

### 3 · EVERYTHING IRAQ IS FIGHTING (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: Viking war-horns, cold and relentless.]**
**[ANIMATION: Haaland, Ødegaard, Sander Berge — a modern machine assembling in formation.]**

Haaland and Ødegaard represent everything Iraq is fighting against — resources, infrastructure, a culture that produces world-class players with metronomic consistency. 120-plus City goals, Ødegaard's elegance, Berge's midfield steel: a Norway built for the modern game. *(beat)* But Iraq's players carry something no statistic measures — the pride of a nation that has endured decades of conflict and still finds joy in a football.

**[SUPPORTERS — full Iraqi roar: "I-raq! I-raq!"]**

### 4 · THE 23RD MINUTE (2:35–3:50)
**[MUSIC: tension gathers — then silence as the shot flies.]**
**[ANIMATION: Iqbal pressed by two Norwegians; the calm one-two with Al-Ammari; the press broken; Hussein turning and firing from 20 yards; the ball flashing just over the bar.]**

23rd minute. Iqbal receives under pressure from two Norwegian players. He does what he has done since he was a boy at Manchester United — stays calm, plays a one-two with Amir Al-Ammari, and suddenly Iraq have broken the press. The ball finds Aymen Hussein, who turns and shoots from 20 yards. **The ball flies just over the bar.** **[SUPPORTERS: the diaspora — who travelled from across America — erupts in song.]** *Their team is not here to make up the numbers.*

### 5 · TOO IMPROBABLE FOR HOLLYWOOD (3:50–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the music-box returns, almost laughing.]**
**[ANIMATION: the name "Zidane" on a red Iraqi shirt, charging at a wall of Viking shields.]**

A boy named Zidane against a Viking army. *(beat)* The World Cup writes stories Hollywood would reject as too improbable — and that is precisely why we watch. We come for the goals, but we stay for the impossible: a war-scarred nation, led by a boy named after a legend, refusing to know its place.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the fated music-box holds its final, amused note.]**
**[ANIMATION: the storybook page glowing; the next page deliberately left blank.]**

A name, a nation, an army of Vikings, and a single shot that flew just over. *(beat)* **And the storybook asks the question it always asks: if the World Cup keeps writing tales too improbable to invent — a boy named Zidane, a country that plays through war — then is it really chance that authors these nights, or is the game trying to tell us that the impossible is the whole point?** The poet with the wicked humour isn't finished. The next page is blank — and Iraq is holding the pen.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "I-raq! I-raq!" … "Hei-a Nor-ge!"]**
