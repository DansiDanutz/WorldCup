# Germany vs Ivory Coast — The Chocolate War
*Group E · Matchday 2 · June 17, 2026*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** a soft cocoa-warm motif → mechanical German percussion vs thunderous djembe → rising heat to the 71st-minute volley → crossbar clang, then a held breath → the cocoa motif souring on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Germany — *"Deutsch-land! Deutsch-land!"*, *"Schland!"*; Les Éléphants — *"É-lé-phants! É-lé-phants!"*, *"Côte d'Ivoire!"*, djembe drums
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Two nations bound by chocolate, divided by ninety minutes. Ivory Coast have buried a golden generation that never delivered — so can the new sons outrun the ghosts of the ones who failed?*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Bound by Cocoa (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: a soft, warm motif, almost sweet.]**
**[ANIMATION: cocoa pods in Abidjan dissolve into pralines in a Cologne window; a map-line of trade connects the two.]**

Two nations that share more than they know. Germany and the Ivory Coast are bound by chocolate — the cocoa beans of Abidjan become the pralines of Cologne, a partnership decades deep. *(beat)* But on the football pitch, there is no sweetness. There is only the beautiful, brutal pursuit of victory. And one of these teams is haunted.

**[SUPPORTERS — Ivorian djembe and voices: "É-lé-phants! É-lé-phants!"]**

### 2 · BORN THREE MONTHS APART (0:55–1:40)
**[MUSIC: precise, telepathic German percussion.]**
**[ANIMATION: Musiala and Wirtz mirroring each other's movement, born of the same 2003 spring.]**

Jamal Musiala and Florian Wirtz — "Bambi" and "The Prodigy," born three months apart in 2003, two minds that seem to share a single thought. Musiala dances like a street performer; Wirtz threads passes through geometries that should not exist. *The machine has rebuilt itself around them.*

### 3 · THE NEW ELEPHANTS (1:40–2:35)
**[MUSIC: thunderous djembe, proud and hungry.]**
**[ANIMATION: Amad Diallo's feet rattling like percussion; Franck Kessié rolling off a challenge; Adingra blurring past a full-back.]**

Against them stands a golden new generation: Amad Diallo, whose dribbling turns defenders into traffic cones; Franck Kessié, the midfield colossus of power and surprising grace; Simon Adingra, whose pace terrifies the Premier League. Ousmane Diomande anchors the back with a maturity beyond his years. *(beat)* But this team carries a weight — for a decade the Ivory Coast were Africa's great underachievers, a squad of Drogbas and Tourés who never broke through. *These young men grew up watching that failure, and swore to be different.*

### 4 · THE 71ST MINUTE (2:35–3:45)
**[MUSIC: heat rising — then everything stops for the volley.]**
**[ANIMATION: Kessié shrugging off Kimmich; Adingra burning past Rüdiger; Diallo's flying volley; ter Stegen's fingertip; the crossbar.]**

71st minute. Kessié takes it in midfield, shrugs Kimmich off with a roll of those shoulders, and drives. Germany retreat. He slides a perfect ball to Adingra, who accelerates past Rüdiger and whips a cross to the far post — and Diallo meets it with a flying volley that rockets toward the top corner. Ter Stegen somehow gets a fingertip. **The ball cannons off the crossbar.** **[SUPPORTERS: the stadium loses its mind — djembe thunder.]** Chocolate may bind these nations. On this pitch, there is only the bitter taste of so-close.

### 5 · THE LANGUAGE OF THE GAME (3:45–4:25)
**[MUSIC: the two themes briefly speak as one.]**
**[ANIMATION: German and Ivorian players colliding, rising, shaking hands mid-battle.]**

When continents collide, the World Cup reminds us football speaks every language — cocoa and steel, machine and elephant, all of it understood without a single word. *(beat)* But understanding is not the same as forgiving, and tonight neither side gives an inch.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:25–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the warm cocoa motif curdles into a minor key.]**
**[ANIMATION: ghost-images of Drogba, Touré, Gervinho fading behind the young Ivorians, who do not look back.]**

The new Elephants are younger, faster, fearless. The machine is precise as ever. *(beat)* **And the question hangs over the Ivorian half like incense at a memorial: a crossbar away from beating Germany — but can a generation finally outrun the ghosts of the golden one who had everything and won nothing?** Some inheritances are gifts. Others are hauntings. Tonight, the bar decided which.

**[SUPPORTERS — fading, overlapping: "Côte d'Ivoire!" … "Deutsch-land!"]**
