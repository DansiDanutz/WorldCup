# Canada vs Switzerland — The General's Last Campaign

*Group B, Matchday 3 — 2026*

Granit Xhaka has 145 international caps. He has played at three World Cups and three European Championships. He has won the Bundesliga with Bayer Leverkusen and captained Arsenal through their darkest hours and brightest redemption. He has played through brother-against-brother matches at Euro 2016, survived being stripped of the Arsenal captaincy by his own fans, and rebuilt himself into one of the most respected midfielders in European football. At 33, "The General" marches into his final World Cup campaign with the weight of a nation's expectations on shoulders that have never buckled.

Across the midfield battleground stands Stephen Eustaquio, the Canadian with Portuguese roots who orchestrates Jesse Marsch's high-pressing system with the calmness of a man who has played in the cauldrons of Porto. He is not Xhaka's equal in caps or honors, but in this moment, on this pitch, in front of 50,000 screaming Canadians at BMO Field, he does not need to be.

The match is chess at sprint pace. Switzerland's 3-4-2-1, built on Manuel Akanji's ball-playing elegance at the back, tries to choke Canada's vertical attacks. Dan Ndoye's dribbling on the wing tests Alphonso Davies' defensive discipline — can the world's most exciting left-back resist the urge to charge forward and instead hold his position? Breel Embolo, the powerful Swiss striker, uses his body like a battering ram against the Canadian defense.

In the 38th minute, Xhaka stands over a free kick 28 yards from goal. His left foot is a weapon, honed over a decade of set-piece mastery. He strikes — the ball curves, dips, and cracks against the crossbar. The rebound falls to Akanji, whose shot is blocked by a desperate, diving tackle from Eustaquio. The Canadian bench holds its breath.

The second half is Canada's. Davies, released from his defensive shackles, torments the Swiss right flank. In the 71st minute, his cross finds Jonathan David, who controls, turns, and shoots in one motion. Yann Sommer, the Swiss goalkeeper, gets a hand to it — but the ball spins over the line. Canada leads. BMO Field shakes.

In the 88th minute, Xhaka receives the ball one final time. He looks up, sees Embolo's run, and plays a pass that only he can see — 40 yards, over the defense, into Embolo's path. The Swiss striker controls and finishes. The General's last campaign has another chapter. The final score: 1-1. Two warriors, one battlefield, mutual respect.
