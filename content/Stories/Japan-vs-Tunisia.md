# Japan vs Tunisia — The Samurai and the Eagle
*Group F · Matchday 3 · June 22, 2026*

> **Runtime:** ~5:00 · **Genre:** narrated mystery film
> **Narrator:** Brian (ElevenLabs) — warm, cinematic, mysterious delivery
> **Music arc:** taiko and darbouka trading rhythms → precision theme vs passion theme → ticking tension to the 91st minute → time stops on the swing → both rhythms suspended on the question
> **Supporters (crowd-SFX layer):** Blue Samurai — *"Ni-ppon! Ni-ppon!"*, taiko drums; Carthage Eagles — *"Tou-nes! Tou-nes!"*, *"Aâcha Tou-nes!"*, darbouka
> **THE MYSTERY (recurring spine):** *Two nations who rebuilt themselves entirely through football, both forever stranded at the edge of the knockout rounds. Tonight the door is right there — and the game does not care about either of their histories, only their courage in the present moment.*

---

### 1 · COLD OPEN — Two Nations Remade (0:00–0:55)
**[MUSIC: taiko and darbouka, two rhythms circling.]**
**[ANIMATION: 1980s Japan with no football culture dissolves into players in Madrid, Liverpool, Cologne; Tunisia's eight World Cup badges line up like medals.]**

Two nations that transformed themselves through football. Japan — a country with no footballing tradition forty years ago — now sends sons to the Premier League, La Liga, the Bundesliga. Tunisia — twelve million people — has been the most consistent African qualifier in World Cup history. *(beat)* Both have reached the very edge of the knockout rounds. Both have fallen just short. Tonight, the door is right in front of them.

**[SUPPORTERS — both ends: "Ni-ppon!" answered by "Tou-nes!"]**

### 2 · THE TORNADO (0:55–1:45)
**[MUSIC: darbouka builds, chaotic and alive.]**
**[ANIMATION: Hannibal Mejbri pressing, harrying, a human tornado in midfield.]**

Hannibal Mejbri is Tunisia's wild card — 23, a Manchester United academy product who chose Tunisia over France, playing with controlled chaos. Not the most talented man on the pitch, but perhaps the most intense — a tornado who presses, harries, and creates havoc in equal measure.

### 3 · THE FOUNDATION (1:45–2:35)
**[MUSIC: a calm, disciplined koto-and-taiko theme.]**
**[ANIMATION: Wataru Endo doing the quiet work — the screen, the cover, the reset — that holds Japan together.]**

Against him, Wataru Endo embodies everything Japan stands for: discipline, intelligence, the willingness to do the work others won't. Not flashy — *essential*, the foundation on which Japan's beautiful football is built. *(beat)* Japan have beaten European giants but sometimes stumble against teams who refuse to play their game. Tunisia have beaten France but sometimes lack the final, killing touch. *Precision against passion — a match that defies prediction.*

**[SUPPORTERS — Carthage corner: "Aâcha Tou-nes!"]**

### 4 · THE 91ST MINUTE (2:35–3:55)
**[MUSIC: a ticking clock under both rhythms — then everything stops.]**
**[ANIMATION: Mitoma, quiet all night, finding one yard; Achouri's legs finally gone; the burst, the byline, the cut-back; Kubo arriving unmarked, left foot swinging.]**

91st minute. 1–1. Kaoru Mitoma picks it up on the left — marked out of the match all night by Elias Achouri's tireless pressing. But now, both teams exhausted, the match on a knife-edge, he finds a single yard. He accelerates — that trademark burst that leaves defenders grasping at air — and drives at the box. Achouri chases; the legs are gone. Mitoma reaches the byline and cuts it back. Takefusa Kubo arrives, unmarked, and swings his left foot. The ball flies toward goal. **[SUPPORTERS: the stadium rises; taiko and darbouka fall silent.]** Time stands still.

### 5 · COURAGE IN THE PRESENT (3:55–4:30)
**[MUSIC: a single held breath; the rhythms suspended.]**
**[ANIMATION: the ball frozen in flight; both benches half-risen; eleven million in two nations leaning toward the light.]**

The samurai and the eagle, locked in one moment that will define both their World Cups. *(beat)* Football's greatest gift is that it does not care about your history — only your courage in the present. And right now, suspended in the Group F night, the present is all there is.

### 6 · THE MYSTERY, UNDERLINED (4:30–5:00)
**[MUSIC: the rhythms hold, refusing to resolve.]**
**[ANIMATION: the frozen ball; the knockout-round door glowing just beyond the goal frame.]**

Two nations remade by football, one swing of a left foot from everything they ever built toward. *(beat)* **And the game whispers its only law as the ball hangs there: it does not remember your eight near-misses or your forty-year climb — it asks one question, once, in the ninety-first minute — how brave are you, right now?** The door is open. The ball is still in the air. The answer is about to land.

**[SUPPORTERS — suspended, then erupting into the dark: "Ni-ppon!" … "Tou-nes!"]**
