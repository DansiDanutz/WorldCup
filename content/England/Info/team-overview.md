## Team History

### The Birthplace of Football

England is widely recognized as the **birthplace of modern football**, with the Football Association founded in 1863 and the first standardized rules of the game codified in England. Despite this rich history, England has won the World Cup just **once** -- on home soil in **1966**.

### World Cup Record

| Year | Host | Result | Key Moment |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| 1966 | England | **Champions** | Geoff Hurst's hat-trick in the 4-2 final win over West Germany |
| 1990 | Italy | 4th Place | Lost to West Germany on penalties in the semi-final |
| 2018 | Russia | 4th Place | Reached semi-final under Gareth Southgate |
| 2022 | Qatar | Quarter-finals | Lost to France 2-1 in a tight quarter-final |

### The "Years of Hurt"

England fans have endured decades of near-misses and heartbreak since 1966. The team has frequently entered tournaments with high expectations -- dubbed "the best squad in a generation" -- only to fall short, often on penalties.

### The Southgate Era & Beyond

Gareth Southgate transformed England's tournament fortunes from 2016-2024, reaching a World Cup semi-final (2018) and a Euro final (2021). Under new management for 2026, England brings a golden generation of talent hoping to finally end the 60-year wait.

## Playing Style

England typically plays a **4-2-3-1 or 4-3-3** formation emphasizing:
- **Physicality and pace**: Strong, athletic players who excel in transition
- **Set-piece threat**: Historically one of the most dangerous teams from corners and free-kicks
- **Wide play**: Attacking full-backs and pacy wingers stretching defenses
- **Youthful exuberance**: A squad blending experienced veterans with exciting young talent

---

## World Cup 2026 Prospects

England enters the 2026 tournament as one of the **favorites**, boasting arguably the most talented squad in world football. With world-class players in nearly every position, expectation is sky-high.

**Key Question**: Can this golden generation finally bring football home?
