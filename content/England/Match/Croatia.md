**Date:** June 12, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

England and Croatia have a rich competitive history:
- **2018 World Cup (Russia):** Croatia 2-1 AET (Semi-final — Croatia advanced after extra time)
- **2021 Euro (England):** England 2-1 AET (Round of 16 — England won in extra time)
- **2018 Nations League:** England 2-1 (Group stage)

The series is intensely competitive. Both nations have won crucial knockout matches against each other. This rivalry has produced some of the most dramatic international football of the past decade.

---

## The Storyline: The 2018 Rematch

The ghosts of Moscow 2018 still haunt England. A World Cup semi-final that seemed destined for Harry Kane's name until Mario Mandzukic's extra-time winner shattered the dream. England got their revenge at Euro 2021, winning in extra time at Wembley — but the World Cup wound still stings.

Now, eight years later, the rivalry resumes. Luka Modric, at 40, plays what may be his final competitive match against England. The maestro against Jude Bellingham, the apprentice who grew up watching Modric's masterclasses at Real Madrid. It's a generational baton-passing wrapped in a fierce competitive contest.

For England, this match sets the tone. A victory over Croatia — the team that broke their hearts in 2018 — would announce their title credentials. A defeat would reopen old wounds.

## Key Player Matchups

### Jude Bellingham vs Luka Modric
The past vs the future of midfield excellence. Bellingham's power and drive against Modric's vision and control. This duel transcends the match — it's a symbolic changing of the guard.

### Harry Kane vs Josko Gvardiol
Kane's movement and finishing against Gvardiol's defensive class. Two elite players at the peak of their powers. Kane will drop deep; Gvardiol must decide whether to follow.

### Phil Foden vs Mateo Kovacic
Foden's creativity against Kovacic's ball retention. If Foden finds space between the lines, England's attack becomes unstoppable.

### Bukayo Saka vs Borna Sosa
Saka's dribbling against Sosa's defensive work rate. The wide areas could be where this match is won and lost.
