# England vs Panama — Group L, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

England and Panama have met once in a World Cup match:
- **2018 World Cup (Russia):** England 6-1 Panama (Group Stage — England's biggest World Cup victory since 1954)

The 2018 meeting was historic for Panama — Felipe Baloy scored their first-ever World Cup goal, a moment celebrated as a national triumph despite the heavy defeat.

---

## The Storyline: Redemption and Respect

Panama remembers 2018. The 6-1 thrashing in Nizhny Novgorod was painful, but Felipe Baloy's late goal — Panama's first-ever World Cup strike — turned heartbreak into history. That moment, celebrated by an entire nation, is the foundation of Panama's World Cup identity.

Eight years later, Panama returns with experience and a point to prove. Thomas Christiansen has built a more disciplined, organized team than the one that conceded six. But the gap in quality remains enormous.

For England, this is about professionalism. The memory of scoring six in 2018 could breed complacency — exactly the trap that Panama will set. If England doesn't match Panama's intensity, the Canaleros will make them earn every inch.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Harry Kane vs Panama's Center-Backs
Kane's movement against Panama's deep defensive block. In 2018, Kane scored a hat-trick — Panama must prevent a repeat.

### Bukayo Saka vs Michael Amir Murillo
Saka's dribbling against Murillo's defensive work rate. The wide areas are where England can stretch Panama's compact shape.

### Adalberto Carrasquilla vs Declan Rice
Carrasquilla's creativity against Rice's ball-winning. If Panama's playmaker can find space, the Canaleros can create dangerous moments.

---

## Tactical Preview

**England (4-2-3-1):** Expected to dominate possession and territory. Patient build-up, looking to stretch Panama's defensive block with width and movement. Set pieces are a major weapon — England scored three from set pieces in 2018.

**Panama (5-4-1):** Maximum defensive discipline. Deep block, narrow lines, and physical defending. Counter-attacks through Waterman and Barcenas offer the only realistic path to goal. Set pieces are Panama's best hope for a historic result.

---

## X-Factor

**Panama's pride.** The memory of 2018 burns. Panama's players have talked about earning respect — not just scoring a goal, but competing for a result. That pride could make them more dangerous than the talent gap suggests.
