## Skills and Strengths

- **Aerial dominance:** At 1.99m, Souttar wins virtually every header in both boxes -- his 11 international goals come almost exclusively from headers
- **Set-piece weapon:** Australia's most dangerous attacking threat from corners and free kicks
- **Physical presence:** One of the most physically imposing defenders at the World Cup
- **Reading the game:** Despite his size, he is surprisingly mobile and reads danger well
- **Bravery:** Willing to put his body on the line, as evidenced by his recovery from a serious ACL injury in 2022
- **Composure on the ball:** Has improved his distribution significantly under Popovic

## Interesting Facts

### 1. Born in Scotland, Chose Australia

Harry Souttar was born in Aberdeen, Scotland, and grew up in the Scottish football system. His mother is Australian, which made him eligible for both countries. He played for Scotland at youth level before switching to Australia in 2019 -- a decision that surprised Scottish football, as he was considered a top prospect. His older brother, John Souttar, chose to represent Scotland and plays for Rangers. The brothers could theoretically have played against each other at a World Cup, creating a unique family storyline.

### 2. 11 Goals From Centre-Back

Souttar's record of 11 goals in 37 international appearances is genuinely remarkable. At one point early in his Socceroos career, he had 7 goals in just 17 caps -- a strike rate that many strikers would envy. Almost all of his goals come from headers at set pieces, and opposing teams have specifically game-planned to stop him at corners. His goal-scoring ability from the back makes Australia a threat every time they win a corner or wide free kick, and it is a central pillar of Tony Popovic's tactical approach.
