## Interesting Facts

### 1. The Bundesliga Pioneer

When Jackson Irvine joined FC St. Pauli in 2020, the club was in the 2. Bundesliga. He was instrumental in their promotion to the Bundesliga, becoming one of the very few Australians to play regularly in Germany's top division. St. Pauli's cult following -- the club is famous for its left-leaning, anti-fascist fan culture -- embraced Irvine as one of their own. His committed performances and leadership made him a fan favorite at the Millerntor-Stadion, where the supporters are among the most passionate in German football.

### 2. Born in Melbourne, Made in Scotland

Irvine was born in Melbourne but moved to Scotland as a teenager to join Celtic's youth academy. He qualifies for Australia through his Melbourne birth but grew up immersed in Scottish football culture. His accent still carries traces of both countries, and he has spoken about feeling a deep connection to both nations. He represented Australia at youth level and was cap-tied to the Socceroos with his senior debut in 2013, but the Scottish media occasionally lamented that he was "one that got away" from the Scottish national team setup.
