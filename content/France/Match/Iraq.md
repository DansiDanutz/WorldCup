# France vs Iraq — Group I, Matchday 2

**Date:** June 16, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## The Lions and the Lions of Mesopotamia

France's second group match pits them against Iraq — a team that has waited 40 years between World Cup appearances. The match carries geopolitical overtones that transcend football. Two nations with complex histories meet on the neutral ground of American stadiums, where the only battle is fought with the ball.

---

## Head-to-Head History

No recorded senior international meetings between France and Iraq.

This is a **first-ever meeting** between the two nations.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Kylian Mbappe vs Rebin Sulaka
Mbappe's devastating pace against the Port FC centre-back with 55 caps. Sulaka is experienced in Asian football but has never faced anything like Mbappe's acceleration.

### Aurelien Tchouameni vs Zidane Iqbal
The Real Madrid midfielder against the FC Utrecht playmaker named after Zidane. Iqbal came through Manchester United's academy and carries the hopes of Iraqi football. Facing Tchouameni — a player who embodies everything Iqbal aspires to be — is a career-defining moment.

### Ousmane Dembele vs Merchas Doski
The PSG winger against Viktoria Plzen's left-back. Dembele's trickery against Doski's Czech league experience. The gap in quality is significant.

### Ali Al-Hamadi vs William Saliba
Ipswich Town's striker against Arsenal's defensive colossus. Al-Hamadi is the first Iraqi to play in the Premier League. Saliba might be the best centre-back in the world. The Premier League reunion adds spice.

---

## Storyline: The Airport Incident

Aymen Hussein, Iraq's captain and all-time top scorer with 22 goals, was held and questioned for nearly seven hours at Chicago's O'Hare airport upon arriving for the World Cup. The incident sparked outrage across Iraq and the Middle East. For Hussein, who had done nothing wrong, it was a humiliating introduction to America.

The incident has galvanized the Iraqi squad. Coach Graham Arnold, the Australian who led his own nation to the 2022 World Cup, has used it as motivation. His players feel disrespected, overlooked, and underestimated. Against France — the ultimate football aristocracy — Iraq play with the fury of a team that has been wronged.

For France, this is a potential trap. After the emotional intensity of facing Senegal, complacency against Iraq could be fatal. Deschamps will demand focus, but human nature is harder to control than tactics.

## Tactical Preview

**France (4-2-3-1):** Maignan; Kounde, Saliba, Konate, Theo Hernandez; Tchouameni, Kante; Dembele, Mbappe, Olise; Thuram

**Iraq (4-2-3-1):** Fahad Talib; Hussein Ali, Zaid Tahseen, Rebin Sulaka, Merchas Doski; Kevin Yakob, Zidane Iqbal; Ibrahim Bayesh, Amir Al-Ammari, Youssef Amyn; Ali Al-Hamadi

Iraq will sit deep, compact, and disciplined. Arnold's Australian-style organization means France won't find easy spaces. The plan is to frustrate, absorb, and counter through Al-Hamadi's pace and Iqbal's creativity. France must be patient — force the issue and Iraq's counter becomes dangerous.

---

## X-Factor

**Zidane Iqbal.** The 23-year-old, named after the great Zinedine Zidane, carries a nation's hopes. Born in Manchester to Iraqi parents, he came through United's academy before moving to Utrecht. Against France, he faces Tchouameni — the player he grew up watching. One moment of Iqbal brilliance could immortalize him in Baghdad.
