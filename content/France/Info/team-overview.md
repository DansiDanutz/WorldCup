## Team History

The France national football team, known as **Les Bleus**, represents one of the most storied footballing nations in the history of the sport. France has been a member of FIFA since 1904 and UEFA since its founding in 1954.

### Early Years

France was one of the four European nations that participated in the inaugural 1930 World Cup in Uruguay. The team's golden era began in the 1950s with the legendary Just Fontaine, who scored 13 goals in a single World Cup (1958) -- a record that still stands.

### The Platini Era (1980s)

Michel Platini led France to their first major international success, winning UEFA Euro 1984 on home soil. Platini scored nine goals in five matches, a tournament record that has never been surpassed.

### First World Cup Triumph (1998)

Under manager Aime Jacquet, France won their first FIFA World Cup on home soil in 1998. The team, led by Zinedine Zidane's two goals in the final, defeated Brazil 3-0 at the Stade de France. The squad featured legends like Lilian Thuram, Marcel Desailly, and a young Thierry Henry.

### Euro 2000 and the Dry Spell

France completed a rare double by winning Euro 2000, but then suffered a stunning group-stage exit at the 2002 World Cup as defending champions. The 2000s saw inconsistent performances despite a World Cup final appearance in 2006, where Zidane's infamous headbutt on Marco Materazzi overshadowed the final.

### The Deschamps Dynasty (2012-Present)

Didier Deschamps took over as head coach in 2012 and transformed France into a global powerhouse once again. Under his leadership, France has reached three of the last four major tournament finals:

- **2016 Euro:** Runners-up (lost to Portugal in the final)
- **2018 World Cup:** Champions (defeated Croatia 4-2 in the final)
- **2022 World Cup:** Runners-up (lost to Argentina on penalties)

## Playing Style

Under Deschamps, France has traditionally favored a pragmatic, counter-attacking style built on defensive solidity and devastating pace on the break. However, the 2026 squad reflects a deliberate shift toward a more proactive and offensive approach.

### Key Tactical Characteristics

- **Defensive Foundation:** Built around William Saliba and Dayot Upamecano at centre-back, with Ibrahima Konate providing depth. The backline is among the most physically dominant in world football.
- **Midfield Control:** Aurelien Tchouameni anchors the midfield with N'Golo Kante's experience and Warren Zaire-Emery's youthful dynamism. Adrien Rabiot and Manu Kone provide additional options.
- **Attacking Firepower:** The front line is arguably the most feared at the tournament -- Kylian Mbappe, Ousmane Dembele (the 2025 Ballon d'Or winner), Michael Olise, and Desire Doue offer pace, creativity, and finishing in abundance.
- **Width and Overlapping:** Jules Kounde and Theo Hernandez provide attacking thrust from full-back, while Lucas Digne and Malo Gusto offer reliable alternatives.

### Deschamps' Evolution

For years criticized for conservative tactics, Deschamps has embraced a more expansive system for 2026, motivated by the sheer offensive talent at his disposal. The inclusion of creative players like Rayan Cherki and Michael Olise signals a team that intends to dominate possession and create chances, not merely absorb pressure.

## Expectations for 2026

France enters the 2026 World Cup as one of the outright favorites. Key factors shaping expectations:

1. **Mbappe's Chase for History:** Kylian Mbappe needs just two goals to surpass Olivier Giroud as France's all-time leading scorer (57 goals). The stage is set for a historic tournament from the captain.
2. **Dembele's Golden Moment:** The 2025 Ballon d'Or winner is yet to score at a World Cup despite appearing in the last two tournaments. His transformation into a world-class forward makes him the player to watch.
3. **Deschamps' Farewell:** This will be Deschamps' final tournament as France manager. He has announced he will step down after the World Cup, adding emotional weight and motivation to the campaign.
4. **Generational Talent:** With Saliba (25), Tchouameni (26), Zaire-Emery (20), Olise (24), and Cherki (22), France's core is built for both the present and the future.
5. **Group I Outlook:** France faces Senegal (June 16, New Jersey), Iraq (June 22), and Norway (June 26). They are expected to top the group comfortably, though Senegal will provide a stern opening test.
