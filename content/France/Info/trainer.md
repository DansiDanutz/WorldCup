## Playing Career

### "The Water Carrier"

Deschamps was a defensive midfielder known for his exceptional reading of the game, tireless work rate, and leadership. He earned the nickname "The Water Carrier" (le porteur d'eau) from Eric Cantona, intended as an insult but worn as a badge of honor -- Deschamps did the unglamorous work that allowed stars to shine.

## Managerial Career

### AS Monaco (2009-2012)

Took Monaco from Ligue 2 to a Champions League quarterfinal and a Coupe de la Ligue title. Established his reputation as a tactically astute coach who could get the most out of limited resources.

### Juventus (2014-2015)

Won the Serie A title and reached the Champions League final in his only season, losing to Barcelona. Demonstrated he could handle the pressure of one of Europe's biggest clubs.

### Marseille (2015-2016)

Brief stint back at his former club as manager.

## Tactical Approach

### Core Philosophy

Deschamps is often described as a pragmatist. He prioritizes results over aesthetics, believing that tournament football rewards defensive organization and clinical finishing above all else.

### Key Principles

1. **Defensive Solidity First:** France under Deschamps concedes few goals. He builds from a compact defensive block, rarely allowing opponents space between the lines.
2. **Counter-Attacking Lethality:** With pace merchants like Mbappe and Dembele, France is devastating on the break. Deschamps designs his team to absorb pressure and strike with devastating speed.
3. **Squad Harmony Above All:** Deschamps famously selects "a squad, not the 26 best players." He values chemistry, versatility, and players who accept their roles over individual brilliance.
4. **Experience Matters:** He has consistently trusted veterans (Kante, Hernandez, Rabiot) alongside young stars, creating a balanced dressing room.

### Evolution for 2026

For the 2026 World Cup, Deschamps has made a notable tactical shift. The inclusion of creative, ball-dominating players like Rayan Cherki, Michael Olise, and Desire Doue signals a more proactive approach. The wealth of attacking talent has pushed him toward a system that controls possession and creates chances, rather than purely sitting deep.

Likely formation: **4-2-3-1** with Mbappe as the focal point, Dembele and Olise on the flanks, and Tchouameni anchoring the midfield.

## Achievements as France Manager

- **FIFA World Cup Winner:** 2018
- **FIFA World Cup Runner-up:** 2022
- **UEFA European Championship Runner-up:** 2016
- **UEFA Nations League Winner:** 2021
- Only the third person in history to win the World Cup as both a player and a manager (after Mario Zagallo and Franz Beckenbauer)

## Personal Style

- Intensely private and rarely reveals tactical plans publicly
- Known for his dry humor and pointed responses in press conferences
- Fiercely loyal to players who perform for him
- Has weathered numerous controversies (Benzema exclusion, Pogba controversy) with remarkable resilience
- Announced in January 2025 that the 2026 World Cup will be his final tournament as France manager
