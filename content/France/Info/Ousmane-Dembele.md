## Skills and Strengths

### Ambidexterity
Dembele's most remarkable trait is his ability to use both feet with equal proficiency. He can dribble, pass, cross, and shoot with either foot, making him virtually impossible for defenders to predict. In an era of specialists, he is a true two-footed rarity.

### Dribbling and Flair
One of the most exciting dribblers in world football, Dembele combines lightning-fast feet with creative trickery. He uses stepovers, elastico moves, and body feints to beat defenders in tight spaces. His low center of gravity and quick changes of direction make him lethal in one-on-one situations.

### Pace and Directness
Dembele's explosive acceleration allows him to blow past defenders on the flanks. He is at his most dangerous when running at full speed toward a backpedaling defender, capable of cutting inside or going outside with equal menace.

### Creativity and Vision
Beyond scoring goals, Dembele is an exceptional creator. His crossing from wide areas is pinpoint, and his ability to play defense-splitting passes makes him a dual threat -- scorer and provider. In the 2025/26 season at PSG, he accumulated 14 assists across all competitions.

### Pressing and Work Rate
The most dramatic improvement in Dembele's game under Luis Enrique at PSG has been his off-the-ball work. Once criticized for laziness, he now presses relentlessly, tracks back, and contributes defensively. This transformation was key to his Ballon d'Or campaign.

## Funny and Interesting Facts

### 1. The Pizza Incident
Dembele's love of food is legendary among his teammates and fans. During a France national team camp, he was famously caught on camera munching on a pizza while Didier Deschamps was giving a team talk just meters away. The image went viral, and Dembele became affectionately known as a "foodie footballer." At Barcelona, teammates reportedly had to hide snacks from him during training camps. Despite this, his metabolism and training intensity keep him in peak physical condition.

### 2. From "Barcelona's Biggest Flop" to Ballon d'Or
When Dembele signed for Barcelona in 2017 for a fee of 135 million euros, he was expected to replace Neymar. Instead, he suffered repeated injuries, was criticized for poor professionalism, and was even labeled Barcelona's "most frustrating signing" by the Spanish press. At one point, Barcelona tried to sell him for a fraction of his transfer fee. His redemption at PSG -- where he won the Champions League and Ballon d'Or in 2025 -- is considered one of football's greatest comeback stories. He went from a cautionary tale to an inspiration, proving that talent plus maturity can overcome any setback.

### Bonus Facts

- Dembele's transfer to Barcelona in 2017 made him the most expensive French player in history at the time, and the joint-second most expensive player ever alongside Paul Pogba.
- He speaks French, some Spanish, and a little German from his time at Dortmund. Teammates joke that he communicates primarily through gestures and smiles.
- Despite being a right winger, Dembele's preferred foot is his left. He often cuts inside to shoot or cross with his stronger foot from the right flank.
- He is yet to score a World Cup goal despite being part of the 2018 winning squad and the 2022 runners-up -- a stat he is desperate to change in 2026.
