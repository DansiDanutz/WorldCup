## Team History

### The Desert Foxes Return

Algeria's national football team, known as the **Desert Foxes** (Les Fennecs), has qualified for their fifth FIFA World Cup after a 12-year absence. Their qualification marks a return to form for a nation that has won the Africa Cup of Nations twice (1990 and 2019) and produced some of Africa's most talented footballers.

### The Shame of Gijon (1982)

Algeria's most infamous World Cup moment came in 1982. After beating West Germany 2-1, they were eliminated when West Germany and Austria played out a mutually convenient 1-0 result that sent both European teams through. The match was so brazenly fixed that it led FIFA to introduce simultaneous final group matches. Algeria has waited 44 years for another chance to prove themselves against the world's best.

### Playing Style

Under coach Vladimir Petkovic, Algeria play an attacking 4-3-3 system built on technical skill and pace. The wide players -- Mahrez, Gouiri, Amoura -- are dangerous one-on-one, while the midfield trio of Aouar, Boudaoui, and Chaibi combines creativity with pressing intensity. Ait-Nouri and Bensebaini provide attacking width from full-back.
