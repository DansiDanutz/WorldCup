# Morocco vs Scotland — Group C, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**Very limited history.** These nations have barely met. This is their first competitive encounter, creating a blank canvas for drama.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Achraf Hakimi vs Andy Robertson
PSG's right-back against Liverpool's left-back — two of the world's best in their position. Robertson's leadership and tenacity against Hakimi's devastating attacking quality. This is a champion vs champion battle.

### Sofyan Amrabat vs Scott McTominay
Two midfield warriors who never stop running. Amrabat's pressing against McTominay's combination of physicality and surprising goal threat (15 international goals). The engine room battle will be fierce.

### Youssef En-Nesyri vs Kieran Tierney
The aerial specialist against Celtic's versatile defender. Tierney's commitment in the air will be tested — En-Nesyri has scored some of his biggest goals with his head.

---

## Storyline: History on the Line

Both teams enter this match potentially fighting for survival. Morocco, the 2022 semifinalists, carries the weight of African expectations. Scotland, desperate to escape the group stage for the first time in their history, carries the Tartan Army's endless hope. Walid Regragui against Steve Clarke — two coaches who have built teams greater than the sum of their parts. The 22nd of June could be the night one of these nations makes history. SoFi Stadium, bathed in California sunset, will bear witness.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Morocco (4-2-3-1)** — Proven, disciplined, dangerous. Amrabat screens, Hakimi attacks, Ziyech creates, En-Nesyri finishes. El Khannouss adds youthful creativity.

**Scotland (5-4-1 or 4-4-2)** — Clarke will match Morocco's shape. Robertson and Tierney as wing-backs, McTominay and McGinn central, Shankland leading the line. Physical, organized, brave.

---

## X-Factor: John McGinn's Goal Threat

With 20 international goals, McGinn is Scotland's most dangerous midfielder. His late runs into the box against Morocco's defense — which can be stretched when Hakimi pushes high — could produce the goal that sends Scotland through.
