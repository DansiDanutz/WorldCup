# Morocco vs Haiti — Group C, Matchday 2

**June 17, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**Very limited history.** These nations share African/Caribbean connections but have rarely met in football. This is their first competitive encounter.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Achraf Hakimi vs Carlens Arcus
PSG's right-back against Auxerre's attacking right-back. Hakimi's world-class quality against Arcus's Ligue 1 experience. Hakimi could dominate this flank single-handedly.

### Youssef En-Nesyri vs Jean-Kevin Duverne
Fenerbahce's aerial threat against Nantes' centre-back. En-Nesyri's heading ability is among the best in the tournament. Duverne must be flawless in aerial duels.

### Hakim Ziyech vs Bryan Alceus
"The Wizard" against Le Mans' midfielder. Ziyech's left foot can produce magic from any distance; Alceus must close space instantly and not allow Ziyech to set his feet.

---

## Storyline: The African Connection

Morocco and Haiti share deep cultural ties — both nations have significant African heritage and have produced football talent through diaspora communities. But on the pitch, the gap is enormous. Morocco is a World Cup semifinalist with players at PSG, Bayern Munich, and Barcelona (former). Haiti is appearing in only their second World Cup. For Haiti, this is about respect — showing they can compete against Africa's finest. For Morocco, it's about professionalism — getting the job done without complacency.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Morocco (4-2-3-1)** — Regragui's trusted system. Dominant possession, Hakimi attacking from right-back, Ziyech creating, En-Nesyri finishing. The quality is overwhelming.

**Haiti (4-4-2)** — Deep block, physical, counter-attacking. Nazon's pace on the break, Foster's hold-up play, Placide's shot-stopping. They'll absorb pressure and hope for moments.

---

## X-Factor: Duckens Nazon's Speed

If anyone can exploit Morocco's high defensive line, it's Nazon. The KV Kortrijk forward has genuine pace and a scorer's instinct. One counter-attack, one clean strike, and Haiti could produce the upset of the tournament.
