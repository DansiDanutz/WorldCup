# Morocco vs Brazil — Group C, Matchday 1

**June 12, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

| Matches | Morocco Wins | Draws | Brazil Wins |
|---------|--------------|-------|-------------|
| 3 | 1 | 0 | 2 |

Morocco stunned Brazil **2-1 in a 2023 friendly** — their first-ever win against the Seleção. Brazil won the only World Cup meeting 3-0 in 1998. But that was a different Morocco. The Atlas Lions of 2026 are semifinalists.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Achraf Hakimi vs Vinicius Junior
The marquee matchup. PSG's right-back against Real Madrid's winger — arguably the two best players in their positions worldwide. Hakimi has the pace to match Vini, but Vini's trickery is otherworldly. This battle could define the entire group stage.

### Sofyan Amrabat vs Bruno Guimaraes
Fenerbahce's tireless engine against Newcastle's midfield general. Amrabat covers every blade of grass; Guimaraes dictates play. The team that wins midfield controls this match.

### Youssef En-Nesyri vs Alisson
The Fenerbahce target man against Liverpool's wall. En-Nesyri's aerial ability is elite — he scored multiple World Cup 2022 headers. Alisson is one of the world's best shot-stoppers. Set pieces will be crucial.

---

## Storyline: The Semifinalists vs the Kings

Morocco's 2022 World Cup run was the story of the tournament — the first African team to reach the semifinals. Walid Regragui built a team that beat Belgium, Spain, and Portugal. Now they face the ultimate test: Brazil, five-time champions, coached by Carlo Ancelotti in his World Cup debut. SoFi Stadium, 70,000 fans, Hollywood lights. The Atlas Lions believe they belong on this stage. Brazil believes they own it. This collision of confidence could produce the match of the tournament.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Morocco (4-2-3-1)** — Regragui's proven system. Amrabat screens, Hakimi attacks from right-back, Ziyech creates, En-Nesyri finishes. Bilal El Khannouss (Leicester, 22) adds youth and creativity from the #10 position.

**Brazil (4-3-3)** — Ancelotti's controlled chaos. Vinicius left, Raphinha right, Neymar floating. Alisson provides safety, Bruno Guimaraes controls midfield.

---

## X-Factor: Bilal El Khannouss' Youth

The 22-year-old Leicester playmaker is Morocco's future. Against Brazil's midfield, his fearlessness and creativity could catch theSeleção off guard. Young players don't carry the weight of history — they just play.
