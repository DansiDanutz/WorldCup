## Team History

The Morocco national football team (Arabic: منتخب المغرب لكرة القدم), known as the **Atlas Lions**, is governed by the Royal Moroccan Football Federation (FRMF). Morocco is one of Africa's most storied football nations and the first African team to reach a FIFA World Cup semifinal.

### World Cup Record

| Year | Result |
|------|--------|
| 1970 | Group Stage |
| 1986 | Round of 16 |
| 1994 | Group Stage |
| 1998 | Group Stage |
| 2018 | Group Stage |
| 2022 | **Semifinals (4th place)** |

Morocco has qualified for **6 FIFA World Cups**. Their breakthrough came in 1986 under Jose Faria when they topped a group containing England, Poland, and Portugal, becoming the first African team to advance from the group stage. In 2022 under Walid Regragui, Morocco made history by reaching the semifinals -- the first African and Arab nation to do so -- defeating Belgium, Spain (on penalties), and Portugal before falling to France in the semifinal and Croatia in the third-place match.

### Africa Cup of Nations Record

Morocco won the Africa Cup of Nations in **1976** and were runners-up in 2004. They hosted the 2015 tournament (withdrawn as hosts) and the CHAN (African Nations Championship) in 2018 and 2020, winning both editions.

### Key Achievements

- **1970**: First African team to qualify for a World Cup via a playoff (vs. Tunisia)
- **1976**: Africa Cup of Nations champions
- **1986**: First African team to top a World Cup group
- **2012**: First team to win CHAN
- **2018**: First team to win back-to-back CHAN titles (2018, 2020)
- **2022**: First African/Arab team to reach a World Cup semifinal

## Playing Style

Under Mohamed Ouahbi, Morocco employs a flexible tactical system built around:

### Formation
- Primary: **4-3-3** with occasional shifts to **3-4-3** in possession
- The formation adapts based on opposition and match state

### Key Principles
1. **Space Control**: Compact defensive shape that denies opponents space between the lines
2. **Tempo Management**: Ability to slow down or accelerate play based on game situation
3. **Wide Play**: Utilizing overlapping fullbacks (Hakimi, Mazraoui) as primary attacking weapons
4. **Flexible Front Line**: Use of a false nine or rotational forward positions to create unpredictability
5. **Player-First Philosophy**: Ouahbi's approach prioritizes putting players in positions that maximize their individual strengths

### Strengths
- Elite-level fullbacks providing width and attacking threat
- Physical and athletic midfield capable of both pressing and controlling possession
- Tournament experience from the 2022 World Cup run
- Strong diaspora talent pipeline from European academies

### Weaknesses
- Coaching transition just months before the tournament (Ouahbi replaced Regragui in early 2026)
- Dependence on a core group of players who may carry fatigue from European seasons
- Need to adapt to a new tactical system in a short timeframe

## 2026 World Cup Expectations

Morocco enters the 2026 World Cup with **high expectations** following their historic 2022 semifinal run. The squad retains much of that core while integrating exciting young talent.

### Group C Outlook

Morocco faces a challenging but navigable group:

- **Brazil**: The group favorites and tournament contenders. Morocco will likely target a draw or narrow result
- **Haiti**: A winnable fixture where Morocco will be strong favorites
- **Scotland**: A competitive European side; Morocco has the quality to win

**Target**: Advance from the group stage (top 2 guaranteed, or as one of the best third-placed teams in the expanded 48-team format)

### Tournament Ambitions

Given the experience from 2022 and the talent in the squad, Morocco's realistic ambition is a **quarterfinal run**, with anything beyond that considered a bonus. The coaching change introduces uncertainty, but Ouahbi's familiarity with Moroccan football and the FRMF system provides continuity.

### Key Storylines

1. **Can Ouahbi maintain the Regragui momentum?** The coaching change just months before the tournament is the biggest question mark
2. **Brahim Diaz impact**: The Real Madrid star chose Morocco over Spain and could be the tournament's breakout player
3. **Hakimi's leadership**: As captain and one of the world's best right-backs, he sets the tone
4. **Youth integration**: Players like Bouaddi (18), Talbi (21), and El Aynaoui (24) represent the next generation
