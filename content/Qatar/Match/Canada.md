# Qatar vs Canada — Group B, Matchday 2

**June 17, 2026** | BMO Field, Toronto | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**No previous meetings.** First-ever encounter between Qatar and Canada.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Almoez Ali vs Canadian Defense
The 2019 Asian Cup Golden Boot winner (9 goals) is Qatar's most clinical finisher. His movement in the box is elite — Canada's center-backs must track him constantly.

### Akram Afif vs Stephen Eustaquio
Qatar's creative force against Porto's midfield engine. Afif's vision and trickery will test Eustaquio's pressing intensity. If Afif finds space, Qatar can control the game.

### Hassan Al-Haydos vs Alphonso Davies
Qatar's veteran captain against Bayern Munich's speedster. Al-Haydos provides leadership and experience; Davies provides relentless energy. The contrast encapsulates these two teams.

---

## Storyline: Redemption Tour

Qatar's 2022 World Cup was a disaster — three losses, zero points, first host nation ever eliminated in the group stage. The scars run deep. Tintín Márquez has rebuilt the team's confidence through the Aspire system, but the ghost of 2022 lingers. Canada, buoyed by home support, will press from the first whistle. Qatar must prove they can survive in hostile environments. This is their chance to rewrite the narrative.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Qatar (4-3-3)** — Possession-based, patient, technically excellent. Afif creates, Almoez finishes, Khoukhi anchors. The system is Spanish-influenced and designed to control tempo.

**Canada (4-4-2)** — High-energy pressing, direct attacking. Marsch won't let Qatar settle. David and Larin offer finishing quality.

---

## X-Factor: Almoez Ali's Instincts

The Al-Duhail striker has scored 50+ international goals through pure goalscorer's instinct. In a match where chances might be limited for Qatar, Ali's ability to sniff out half-chances could be the difference between another group-stage exit and a historic result.
