## Key Player Matchups

### Almoez Ali vs Sead Kolasinac
Qatar's Golden Boot winner against Atalanta's "Tank." Ali's intelligent movement against Kolasinac's raw physicality. The Bosnian defender won't give Ali an inch.

### Akram Afif vs Rade Krunic
Qatar's magician against Fenerbahce's versatile midfielder. Afif's creativity against Krunic's defensive discipline. If Afif gets time on the ball, Qatar creates danger.

### Boualem Khoukhi vs Benjamin Sesko
The veteran defender against the rising star. Khoukhi's 80+ caps of experience against Sesko's pace and power. Can old-school defending neutralize new-school talent?

---

## Storyline: The Aspire Acid Test

Qatar's Aspire Academy has spent nearly two decades developing a Spanish-influenced playing style. Bosnia represents the ultimate test of that system: Balkan pragmatism against Iberian idealism. If Qatar's possession game can break down Bosnia's deep block, it validates the entire Aspire project. If Sesko scores on the counter, questions about Qatar's defensive resilience will resurface. Two nations that few expected to be here, both desperate to prove they belong.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Qatar (4-3-3)** — Patient possession, wide play through Afif and Al-Haydos, finishing through Almoez Ali. Tintín Márquez emphasizes ball retention.

**Bosnia (4-2-3-1)** — Deep, compact, counter-attacking. Sesko is the weapon, Pjanic the architect. They'll let Qatar have the ball and strike on transition.

---

## X-Factor: Hassan Al-Haydos' Leadership

Qatar's long-serving captain has 100+ caps and has seen every type of opponent. In a match where Qatar might dominate possession but struggle to break through, Al-Haydos' composure and decision-making in the final third could be the key.
