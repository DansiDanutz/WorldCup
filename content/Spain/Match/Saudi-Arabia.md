## The Ghost of 2022

Spain remember Qatar. They remember the 0-0 draw against Morocco in the Round of 16, the 120 minutes of sterile possession, and the penalty shootout that sent them home. They remember the humiliation. Now, against Saudi Arabia — another Arab nation with a point to prove — the ghosts of 2022 return.

## Key Player Matchups

### Lamine Yamal vs Saud Abdulhamid
The Barcelona teenager against the only Saudi player based in Europe (Lens). Abdulhamid has 35 caps and French league experience — the best-prepared Saudi defender for this challenge.

### Rodri vs Mohamed Kanno
The world's best defensive midfielder against Al-Hilal's enforcer. Kanno has 52 caps and Saudi Pro League quality, but Rodri operates on a different planet.

### Nico Williams vs Moteb Al Harbi
Williams' pace against Al-Hilal's left-back. The Athletic Bilbao winger's dribbling could torment Al Harbi all match.

### Salem Al-Dawsari vs Dani Carvajal
The 2025 AFC Player of the Year against Real Madrid's veteran right-back. Al-Dawsari's trickery against Carvajal's 15 years of elite-level defending. This is the matchup Saudi Arabia need to win.

---

## Storyline: Salem's Last Stand

Salem Al-Dawsari is the king of Saudi football. The Al-Hilal winger has 101 caps, 31 goals, and the 2025 AFC Player of the Year award. His goal against Argentina in 2022 — a stunning left-foot strike — announced Saudi Arabia to the world. But that was a tournament that ended in group-stage heartbreak despite the Argentina miracle.

At 34, this is Al-Dawsari's final World Cup. He carries a nation's hopes on his shoulders, and his battle with Dani Carvajal is the defining individual contest of the match. If Al-Dawsari can produce one moment of magic — one goal like the one against Argentina — Saudi Arabia have a chance.

New coach Georgios Donis, appointed after Herve Renard's sacking in April 2026, has brought discipline but limited time to implement his system. The squad is almost entirely domestic-based, with exceptional chemistry but limited exposure to elite European opposition.

## Tactical Preview

**Spain (4-3-3):** Unai Simon; Carvajal, Le Normand, Laporte, Cucurella; Rodri, Pedri, Fabian Ruiz; Yamal, Morata, Nico Williams

**Saudi Arabia (4-2-3-1):** Al Aqidi; Saud Abdulhamid, Hassan Tambakti, Abdulelah Al Amri, Moteb Al Harbi; Mohamed Kanno, Nasser Al Dawsari; Sultan Mandash, Alaa Al Hejji, Salem Al-Dawsari; Feras Al Brikan

Saudi Arabia will press high in the first 20 minutes — their only window to capitalize on Spain's slow starts. If Spain survive the early pressure and score first, the game is over. If Saudi Arabia score first, the match becomes exactly the kind of low-block, counter-attacking nightmare that cost Spain against Morocco.

## X-Factor

**Saudi Arabia's team chemistry.** With 25 of 26 players from the Saudi Pro League, this squad knows each other intimately. They've played together for years at Al-Hilal, Al-Nassr, and Al-Ittihad. That chemistry can compensate for individual quality gaps — but only if they stay disciplined for 90 minutes.
