## Team History

### La Roja: From Underachievers to Dynasty

Spain's transformation from perennial underachievers to global dominators is one of football's greatest stories. Between 2008 and 2012, La Roja won three consecutive major tournaments — an unprecedented feat.

### World Cup Record

- **Best result**: Champions (2010)
- **Appearances**: 16 (including 2026)
- **Titles**: 1 (2010)
- **European Championships**: 4 (1964, 2008, 2012, 2024)

## Playing Style

Under **Luis de la Fuente**, Spain has evolved from the pure tiki-taka era:

- **Possession with purpose**: Still dominates the ball but attacks more directly
- **Youth revolution**: Yamal (18), Williams (22), Pedri (23), Gavi (22) form the spine
- **Wing play**: Uses rapid wingers (Yamal, Williams) to stretch defenses
- **Defensive solidity**: Rodri as the world's best defensive midfielder
- **Set-piece improvement**: No longer vulnerable from aerial balls

## 2026 Outlook

Spain arrives as one of the tournament favorites after winning Euro 2024. The squad blends youth (Yamal, Pedri, Williams) with experience (Rodri, Morata, Carvajal). No Real Madrid players were selected — a remarkable decision — but the Barcelona/Atletico/Bilbao core is formidable. A semifinal is the minimum expectation, with a second World Cup title the genuine ambition.
