# Portugal vs DR Congo — Group K, Matchday 1

**Date:** June 12, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Portugal and DR Congo have **never met** in an official FIFA match. This is their first encounter at any level.

---

## The Storyline: The Leopards' 52-Year Return

DR Congo's return to the World Cup after a 52-year absence is one of the tournament's most remarkable stories. In 1974, they became the first sub-Saharan African team to qualify — now, more than half a century later, the Leopards are back.

Their reward? A match against Cristiano Ronaldo's Portugal, a team brimming with Nations League confidence and stacked with talent from Europe's biggest clubs. For DR Congo's players — many born and raised in Europe — this is a clash of identities. Aaron Wan-Bissaka, Arthur Masuaku, and Yoane Wissa ply their trade in Europe's top leagues. They know Portuguese football intimately.

For Ronaldo, at 41, every World Cup match is potentially his last. He will want to make a statement from the opening whistle. But DR Congo's physical power and counter-attacking threat should not be underestimated.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Cristiano Ronaldo vs Chancel Mbemba
Ronaldo's aerial dominance and movement against Mbemba's physical defending. Every cross into the box is a potential goal.

### Bruno Fernandes vs Cedric Bakambu (midfield battle)
Fernandes' creativity against DR Congo's compact midfield. If Fernandes finds space between the lines, Portugal will dominate.

### Yoane Wissa vs Ruben Dias
Wissa's pace on the counter against Dias' positioning. One breakaway could shock the tournament.

## Tactical Preview

**Portugal (3-4-2-1 / 3-4-3):** Martinez will set up to dominate possession. Vitinha, Bernardo Silva, and Bruno Fernandes will control midfield, while the wing-backs provide width. Ronaldo is the focal point in attack.

**DR Congo (4-3-3):** Defensive discipline, physical presence, and explosive counters. The Leopards will sit deep, absorb pressure, and look to release Wissa, Silas, and Bakambu on the break. Set pieces are a major weapon.

---

## X-Factor

**DR Congo's emotional power.** A 52-year wait creates an intensity that tactics cannot account for. If the Leopards play with the fury of a generation denied, Portugal could be in for a uncomfortable afternoon.
