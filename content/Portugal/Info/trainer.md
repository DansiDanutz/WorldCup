He is best remembered as part of the famous "Three Amigos" at Wigan Athletic alongside Jesus Seba and Isidro Diaz, who were among the first Spanish players to play in English football.

## Managerial Career

### Swansea City (2007-2009)
- Led Swansea to the **League One title** in 2007-08 with an attractive passing style
- Built a reputation for possession-based football that caught the attention of bigger clubs
- Win rate: ~50%

### Wigan Athletic (2009-2013)
- Kept Wigan in the Premier League against the odds for multiple seasons
- Won the **FA Cup in 2013**, beating Manchester City 1-0 in a famous upset
- Relegated from the Premier League in the same season as the FA Cup triumph
- Known for his bold tactical decisions and willingness to give young players a chance

### Everton (2013-2016)
- Led Everton to a club-record Premier League points tally (72) in his first season (2013-14)
- Reached the **FA Cup semi-finals** (2016) and **League Cup semi-finals** (2015-16)
- Developed young talents including Romelu Lukaku, John Stones, and Ross Barkley
- Sacked in May 2016 after inconsistent results

### Belgium National Team (2016-2022)
- Led Belgium to their **best-ever World Cup finish (3rd place) at the 2018 World Cup** in Russia
- Belgium topped the FIFA World Rankings for the first time in their history under his tenure
- Quarter-final exit at Euro 2020 (lost to eventual winners Italy)
- Group stage exit at the 2022 World Cup in Qatar, a major disappointment
- Overall win rate with Belgium: ~70%

### Portugal National Team (2023-Present)
- Appointed on January 9, 2023, replacing Fernando Santos
- Won the **UEFA Nations League in 2025**, making Portugal the first two-time winner
- Implemented a fluent 3-4-2-1 system that maximizes Portugal's technical talent
- Achieved strong qualifying results and built squad depth across all positions
- Tasked with leading a golden generation of Portuguese talent at the 2026 World Cup

## Tactical Approach

Martinez is a disciple of possession-based, attacking football with clear principles:

- **Build from the back**: Goalkeeper and center-backs are the first phase of attack
- **Positional play**: Structured movement patterns to create numerical superiority
- **Inverted wing-backs**: Cancelo, Dalot, and Nuno Mendes tuck inside to overload midfield
- **Creative freedom**: Bruno Fernandes and Bernardo Silva given license to roam and create
- **Flexible front line**: Interchanging forwards with Ronaldo as the focal point
- **High pressing**: Aggressive pressing when possession is lost to win the ball back quickly

### Strengths
- Excellent at maximizing the potential of technically gifted players
- Strong man-management skills and ability to handle big egos
- Tactically flexible -- can switch between 3-at-the-back and 4-at-the-back systems
- Experience at major tournaments (2018 WC 3rd place, Euro 2020, 2022 WC)

### Criticisms
- Occasionally too pragmatic in knockout matches at major tournaments
- Belgium's 2022 World Cup group-stage exit was a significant failure
- Questions about defensive solidity against elite counter-attacking teams
