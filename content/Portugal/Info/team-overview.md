### Best Result: 3rd Place (1966)

Portugal's greatest World Cup achievement came in their debut tournament in 1966, held in England. Led by the legendary **Eusebio**, who won the Golden Boot with 9 goals, Portugal finished third after a remarkable campaign that included a 5-3 quarter-final comeback against North Korea after trailing 3-0.

## Key Achievements

- **UEFA European Championship Winners (2016)** - Defeated France 1-0 in the final in Paris
- **UEFA Nations League Winners (2019)** - Beat Netherlands 1-0 in the inaugural final
- **UEFA Nations League Winners (2025)** - Reigning champions entering the 2026 World Cup
- **FIFA World Youth Championship (1989, 1991)** - Back-to-back titles featuring the "Golden Generation"
- **Olympic Bronze Medal (1996)** - Atlanta Olympics

## Playing Style

Under Roberto Martinez, Portugal plays a possession-based attacking system, typically deploying a **3-4-2-1** or **3-4-3** formation. Key characteristics include:

- **Fluid positional play**: Players interchange positions freely in the attacking phase
- **High defensive line**: Pushing opponents back to compress the pitch
- **Technical excellence**: Emphasis on quick passing, close control, and creativity
- **Wing-back aggression**: Dalot, Cancelo, and Nuno Mendes provide width and attacking threat
- **Midfield dominance**: Vitinha, Bruno Fernandes, and Bernardo Silva form one of the tournament's most technically gifted midfields
- **Ronaldo as focal point**: Even at 41, Cristiano Ronaldo remains the primary goal threat and team leader

## Expectations for 2026

Portugal enter the 2026 World Cup as one of the tournament favorites, backed by:

- A **reigning Nations League title** won in 2025
- A squad blending elite experience (Ronaldo, Bernardo Silva, Cancelo) with exciting young talent (Joao Neves, Nuno Mendes, Francisco Conceicao)
- One of the deepest and most technically accomplished squads in the tournament
- The emotional motivation of potentially being Cristiano Ronaldo's final World Cup (his 6th)

The biggest questions surrounding the team are whether Ronaldo, at 41, can still deliver at the highest level, and whether Roberto Martinez's tactical setup can overcome elite opposition in the knockout rounds -- something that has been a hurdle in recent tournaments.

## Notable Records

- **Cristiano Ronaldo** holds the record for most World Cup qualifying goals (41) and is appearing in his record-tying 6th World Cup
- Portugal's 2026 squad features players from some of Europe's biggest clubs: Manchester City, Manchester United, PSG, Barcelona, AC Milan, Juventus, and Chelsea
- The 2025 Nations League triumph made Portugal the first nation to win the competition twice
