## Interesting Facts

### Fact 1: From Quininde to the Champs-Elysees
Willian Pacho was born in Quininde, a small town in the Esmeraldas province of northwestern Ecuador. Quininde is known for its banana and cacao plantations, not for producing footballers. Pacho's journey from this rural town to Paris Saint-Germain — one of the world's most glamorous clubs — is extraordinary. He was spotted by Independiente del Valle's extensive scouting network, which has become renowned for unearthing talent from Ecuador's most remote regions. His progression from IDV to Antwerp in Belgium, then to Eintracht Frankfurt in Germany's Bundesliga, and finally to PSG for a significant transfer fee, is a testament to both his talent and the increasingly effective South American-to-Europe player pipeline.

### Fact 2: The 80 Million Euro Man
Pacho's current market value of 80 million euros makes him the most valuable Ecuadorian player in history. PSG signed him from Eintracht Frankfurt on a contract running until 2029, and he has since signed a one-year extension. He won the Ligue 1 title in his first season (2024/25), becoming an integral part of PSG's defense. His rise has been so rapid that Transfermarkt has had to repeatedly upgrade his valuation — from under 5 million euros at Independiente del Valle to 80 million in just four years. This represents a 1,500% increase in market value, one of the most dramatic appreciations in recent football history.
