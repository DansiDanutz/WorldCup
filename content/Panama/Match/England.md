# Panama vs England — Group L, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Panama and England have met once in a World Cup match:
- **2018 World Cup (Russia):** England 6-1 Panama — England's biggest World Cup win since 1954. Felipe Baloy scored Panama's first-ever World Cup goal.

---

## The Storyline: Seeking Respect

Panama's 2018 World Cup was defined by two moments: the 6-1 loss to England and Felipe Baloy's historic goal. The loss hurt. The goal healed. Eight years later, Panama wants to rewrite the narrative — not to win, necessarily, but to compete.

Thomas Christiansen has transformed Panama from the naive team of 2018 into a disciplined, organized unit. They won't concede six again. The question is whether they can score — and whether they can keep England close enough to make it interesting.

For England, the memory of 2018's six-goal romp could be dangerous. Complacency is the enemy. Panama will fight with the desperation of a team that has nothing to lose, and everything has changed since Nizhny Novgorod.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Adalberto Carrasquilla vs Declan Rice
Carrasquilla's creativity against Rice's ball-winning. If Panama's playmaker can find space, the Canaleros can create chances.

### Cecilio Waterman vs John Stones
Waterman's pace against Stones' positioning. One counter-attack could produce Panama's most memorable World Cup moment.

### Harry Kane vs Panama's Center-Backs
Kane scored a hat-trick against Panama in 2018. The Canaleros will be desperate to prevent a repeat — doubling down, fouling, doing whatever it takes.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Panama (5-4-1):** Deep defensive block, maximum discipline. Every player behind the ball. Counter-attacks through Waterman and Barcenas. Set pieces offer the best hope for a historic result.

**England (4-2-3-1):** Expected to dominate possession. Patient build-up, width from the full-backs, and movement from the front four. England must break down Panama's block without becoming frustrated.

---

## X-Factor

**Panama's 2018 motivation.** The Canaleros remember the scoreline. They've talked about it for eight years. This isn't just a match — it's a chance at redemption. That emotion could make them more dangerous than the talent gap suggests.
