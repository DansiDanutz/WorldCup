# Panama - FIFA World Cup 2026

## Group L: England, Croatia, Ghana, Panama

---

## Team History

### The Canaleros' Journey

Panama is making only its **second-ever World Cup appearance**, having debuted in **2018** in Russia. The small Central American nation of just 4.4 million people has punched above its weight to qualify for back-to-back tournaments.

### World Cup Record

| Year | Host | Result | Key Moment |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| 2018 | Russia | Group Stage | Lost all 3 matches; scored historic first World Cup goal (vs England) |

### The 2018 Debut

- Panama's first-ever World Cup was emotional — the entire nation watched as they took the field against Belgium
- **Felipe Baloy** scored Panama's first-ever World Cup goal in a 6-1 loss to England — the stadium erupted and fans at home wept
- Despite losing all three games, qualifying itself was celebrated as a national triumph

### Road to 2026

- Panama qualified through **CONCACAF** with a strong campaign
- Beat traditional powers including the United States and Costa Rica
- Coach **Thomas Christiansen** built a disciplined, counter-attacking team
- The squad features players based in Europe, MLS, and Latin America

---

## Playing Style

Under **Thomas Christiansen**, Panama plays a disciplined **5-4-1 / 4-5-1**:

- **Defensive solidity**: Deep block with organized lines; difficult to break down
- **Counter-attacking**: Uses the pace of Waterman and Barcenas on the break
- **Set pieces**: A major threat — Panama is physical and good in the air
- **Experience**: Several players were part of the 2018 squad and bring World Cup experience
- **Fighting spirit**: Panama never gives up — they play with heart and passion

### Current FIFA Ranking: ~45th

---

## 2026 World Cup Outlook

Panama is drawn in a tough Group L with England, Croatia, and Ghana. Realistic expectations are to compete hard in every match and try to secure the country's first-ever World Cup point. A draw or upset win against Ghana would be considered a massive achievement. The team has nothing to lose and plays with the freedom of underdogs.
