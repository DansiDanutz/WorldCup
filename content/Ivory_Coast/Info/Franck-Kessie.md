## Skills and Strengths

- **Physical Dominance:** Kessie is a mountain of a midfielder — powerful, athletic, and almost impossible to knock off the ball. His combination of size and speed is rare for a central midfielder.
- **Box-to-Box Energy:** He covers every blade of grass, contributing defensively with tackles and interceptions, then arriving in the opposition box to score goals. His trademark late runs into the penalty area have produced countless goals.
- **Penalty Taking:** An ice-cold penalty taker with a near-perfect record from the spot. His calmness under pressure is legendary.
- **Leadership:** As Ivory Coast captain, Kessie is the emotional and spiritual leader of the team. His presence alone elevates those around him.
- **Big-Game Player:** Scores in finals and crucial matches. He scored in the AFC Champions League final for Al-Ahli and has delivered in AFCON knockout matches for Ivory Coast.

## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. The "President" Nickname and Tribute to His Father
Kessie's father, a military officer, died when Franck was a young child. Every time Kessie scores, he performs a military salute celebration — a tribute to his late father. The celebration has become iconic in both Milan and Ivory Coast. Teammates and fans have dubbed him "The President" (Il Presidente) for his commanding presence both on and off the pitch. At AC Milan, he was so beloved that fans created a giant banner reading "Kessie, our President" that hung in the Curva Sud at the San Siro.

### 2. From Ivorian Village to AFC Champions League Glory
Kessie grew up in Ouragahio, a small village in southern Ivory Coast, where he played football barefoot on dirt pitches. His journey from those humble beginnings to scoring in the AFC Champions League final for Al-Ahli — helping them win their first-ever Asian title — is one of football's great success stories. After the Champions League victory, Kessie reportedly paid for the construction of a new football pitch in his home village, complete with artificial turf and floodlights, so that the next generation wouldn't have to play on bare earth like he did.
