---

## Nickname

**Les Elephants** (The Elephants) — one of the most iconic nicknames in world football, reflecting the strength, power, and pride of the Ivorian nation.

---

## Federation

Federation Ivoirienne de Football (FIF), founded in 1960. Affiliated to FIFA since 1964 and to CAF since 1962.

### The Group Stage Curse
Ivory Coast have appeared in three World Cups and have been eliminated in the group stage on every occasion. Incredibly, each time they were drawn into extremely difficult groups:
- **2006:** Argentina, Netherlands, Serbia & Montenegro ("Group of Death")
- **2010:** Brazil, Portugal, North Korea
- **2014:** Colombia, Greece, Japan

## Playing Style

Under Emerse Fae, Ivory Coast have developed a distinctive identity built on defensive solidity and explosive counter-attacking football. The numbers from qualifying are extraordinary: **8 wins, 2 draws, 0 losses, 25 goals scored, 0 goals conceded.**

### Key Tactical Principles

- **Defensive Fortress:** The backline of Ousmane Diomande, Evan Ndicka, and Wilfried Singo is one of the strongest in the tournament. Zero goals conceded in 10 qualifiers speaks for itself.
- **Midfield Control:** Franck Kessie anchors the midfield with physical dominance and leadership, while Seko Fofana and Ibrahim Sangare provide energy and technical quality.
- **Pace on the Flanks:** Amad Diallo and Simon Adingra provide blistering pace and creativity on the wings, stretching defenses and creating space.
- **Youth and Fearlessness:** Young stars like Yan Diomande (19), Bazoumana Toure, and Ange-Yoan Bonny bring an X-factor that makes this team unpredictable.

### Likely Formation
A **4-2-3-1** or **4-3-3**, with Kessie as the midfield anchor, and a front line featuring Diallo and Adingra supporting a central striker (likely Elye Wahi or Ange-Yoan Bonny).

## The Golden Generation That Fell Short (2006-2014)

Ivory Coast's previous World Cup appearances featured a legendary "Golden Generation" including Didier Drogba, Yaya Toure, Kolo Toure, Didier Zokora, Gervinho, and Salomon Kalou. Despite the incredible individual talent, the team never escaped the group stage, largely due to brutal draws. Drogba's broken arm before the 2010 tournament and cruel late goals conceded in 2014 added to the heartbreak.

---

## The 2023 AFCON Miracle

Ivory Coast's qualification for the 2026 World Cup was propelled by the confidence gained from winning the 2023 Africa Cup of Nations on home soil. The tournament was a fairy tale: the team nearly went out in the group stage after a 4-0 loss to Equatorial Guinea, but scraped through as one of the best third-placed teams. They then sacked coach Jean-Louis Gasset and promoted assistant Emerse Fae to interim manager. Fae led the team on a miraculous run to the final, where they beat Nigeria 2-1. It was one of the greatest comeback stories in AFCON history.

## 2026 Expectations

This is arguably the strongest Ivory Coast squad ever assembled for a World Cup:

- **Minimum target:** Advance from the group stage for the first time in history.
- **Realistic goal:** Quarter-finals, which would be a landmark achievement for Ivorian football.
- **Key advantage:** The blend of experienced leaders (Kessie, Ndicka, Singo) and exciting young talent (Yan Diomande, Amad Diallo) gives the squad both stability and unpredictability.
- **Key challenge:** The opening match against Ecuador is crucial — a win would set up a potential qualification-deciding match against Germany.
