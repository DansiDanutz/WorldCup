# Cape Verde vs Uruguay — Group H, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## The Little Giant and the Big Beast

Cape Verde's final group match is against Uruguay — a two-time World Cup champion with Bielsa's relentless pressing machine. For Cape Verde, this is the ultimate test of their tournament credentials. For Uruguay, it's a match they must win to secure progression.

---

## Head-to-Head History

No recorded senior international meetings between Cape Verde and Uruguay.

This is a **first-ever meeting** between the two nations.

## Key Player Matchups

### Ryan Mendes vs Mathias Olivera
Cape Verde's captain against Napoli's left-back. Mendes' 65+ caps of experience against Olivera's Serie A quality. A fascinating battle of wits.

### Darwin Nunez vs Roberto Lopes
The Al-Hilal striker's pace against the Shamrock Rovers centre-back. Nunez's physicality is overwhelming; Lopes' 25+ caps of determination are his only weapon.

### Federico Valverde vs Jamiro Monteiro
The Real Madrid engine against the San Jose Earthquakes midfielder. Valverde's stamina and quality against Monteiro's industry. A mismatch on paper.

### Garry Rodrigues vs Guillermo Varela
The Sivasspor winger's trickery against Flamengo's right-back. Rodrigues' flair against Varela's experience.

## Storyline: The Ultimate Underdog

Cape Verde's World Cup journey, regardless of results against Spain and Saudi Arabia, has already been a triumph of the human spirit. A nation of 600,000 people playing football on the world's biggest stage is the kind of story that makes the World Cup magical.

Against Uruguay, they face a team that defines football intensity. Bielsa's Uruguay press like animals, run like machines, and fight like warriors. For Cape Verde's players — many of whom play in modest leagues — the physical and mental demands are extraordinary.

But football has a way of rewarding courage. Cape Verde have nothing to lose and everything to gain. One famous result against Uruguay, and their World Cup becomes legendary.

## Tactical Preview

**Cape Verde (4-2-3-1):** Vozinha; Moreira, Lopes, Costa, Joao Paulo; Monteiro, Kenny Rocha Santos; Rodrigues, Andrade, Mendes; Julio Tavares

**Uruguay (4-3-3):** Rochet; Vina, Gimenez, Araujo, Olivera; Ugarte, Bentancur, Valverde; Pellistri, Nunez, B. Rodriguez

Uruguay will press high and suffocate Cape Verde's attempts to build from the back. The Blue Sharks must play direct — long balls to Julio Tavares and quick transitions through Mendes and Rodrigues. Surviving Uruguay's press is the challenge; capitalizing on rare counter-attacking opportunities is the path to an upset.

---

## X-Factor

**Fatigue.** If Uruguay have already played two intense matches against Spain and Saudi Arabia, Bielsa's pressing machine may be running on empty legs by Matchday 3. Cape Verde's best chance is a Uruguay team that's physically depleted. Fresh legs could level the playing field.
