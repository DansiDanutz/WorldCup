### Goalkeeper Notes

- **Dominik Livakovic** is one of the best goalkeepers at the tournament. His heroics in the 2022 World Cup -- where he saved four penalties across two shootouts -- established him as a big-game goalkeeper. After spells at Fenerbahce, he returned to Dinamo Zagreb and remains Croatia's undisputed number one.
- **Dominik Kotarski** has impressed at Copenhagen and provides reliable backup with 4 caps.
- **Ivor Pandur** (0 caps) is the squad's third-choice goalkeeper, gaining valuable tournament experience. His inclusion at Hull City shows Dalic's willingness to look beyond traditional powerhouses.

### Defensive Notes

- **Josko Gvardiol** is the standout defender. At just 24, he already has 48 caps and is one of the world's most complete center-backs. His combination of pace, technical quality, and defensive intelligence makes him the cornerstone of Croatia's backline. Playing at Manchester City under Pep Guardiola has refined his game to elite levels.
- **Josip Stanisic** provides versatility across the back line and has valuable experience at Bayern Munich.
- **Josip Sutalo** has developed well at Ajax and forms a strong partnership with Gvardiol at center-back.
- **Luka Vuskovic** (19) is the squad's youngest player. The Hamburg defender is regarded as one of Europe's most exciting young center-backs and represents the future of Croatian defense.
- **Marin Pongracic** and **Duje Caleta-Car** provide experienced depth with a combined 58 caps.

### Midfield Notes

- **Luka Modric** (captain) is the heart and soul of this team. At 40 years old and with 198 caps (Croatia's all-time record), he remains one of the world's finest midfielders. His vision, passing range, leadership, and technical quality are the foundation upon which Croatia's entire system is built. The 2026 World Cup is expected to be his international farewell.
- **Mateo Kovacic** is the midfield's driving force with 113 caps. His ball-carrying ability, pressing resistance, and technical quality complement Modric perfectly. The Manchester City midfielder is now entering his peak years.
- **Mario Pasalic** provides goals from midfield with 12 in 85 caps. The Atalanta midfielder is a clinical finisher and intelligent runner who excels at arriving late in the box.
- **Nikola Vlasic** (63 caps, 10 goals) is a creative force who can play as a number 10 or in deeper midfield roles.
- **Martin Baturina** (23) and **Petar Sucic** (22) represent the next generation. Both have already made significant moves to Serie A clubs (Como and Inter Milan respectively) and are seen as the heirs to Modric's midfield throne.
- **Luka Sucic** (no relation to Petar) has been impressive at Real Sociedad and adds technical quality and versatility.

### Forward Notes

- **Andrej Kramaric** is Croatia's most prolific active scorer with 36 goals in 116 caps. The Hoffenheim striker has been remarkably consistent for club and country, providing the clinical finishing that Croatia has sometimes lacked.
- **Ivan Perisic** (37) is a tournament specialist. With 154 caps and 38 goals, the veteran provides invaluable experience, versatility (he can play anywhere across the front line), and a reputation for delivering in big matches. His goal in the 2018 World Cup final is one of Croatian football's most iconic moments.
- **Ante Budimir** offers a physical target man option with 6 goals in 38 caps. The Osasuna striker is effective in the air and at holding up play.
- **Igor Matanovic** (23) represents the future of Croatian forward play. The Freiburg striker has shown promising signs in limited international appearances.
- **Marco Pasalic** and **Petar Musa** provide additional depth and different attacking profiles.
