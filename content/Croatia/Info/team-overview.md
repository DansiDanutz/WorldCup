## The Road to 2026

### Qualification Campaign (UEFA)

Croatia navigated a typically demanding European qualifying campaign under Zlatko Dalic, the steady hand who has guided the team since 2017. The qualification process showcased Croatia's enduring ability to produce technically gifted, tactically intelligent footballers regardless of the generation.

The campaign was marked by the usual Croatian traits: tactical discipline, technical excellence in midfield, and an unshakeable belief that they can compete with any team in the world. While no longer possessing the depth of the 2018 or 2022 squads, this Croatia team retains the midfield engine -- Modric, Kovacic, and an emerging generation -- that has defined the nation's football identity.

### The Modric Farewell

The 2026 World Cup is widely expected to be Luka Modric's final tournament. The 40-year-old continues to defy age and logic, maintaining his position as one of the world's best midfielders. Every match he plays is now treated as potentially his last for Croatia, adding an emotional dimension to the campaign.

## Playing Style Under Dalic

### Tactical Setup: 4-3-3

Zlatko Dalic has maintained a consistent tactical identity throughout his tenure:

**1. Midfield Supremacy**
Croatia's greatest strength has always been its midfield. The trio of Modric, Kovacic, and emerging talents like Sucic and Baturina provides technical quality, passing range, and ball retention that few teams can match. Croatia's approach is built on controlling possession through midfield dominance.

**2. Tactical Discipline**
Dalic instills a level of tactical discipline that maximizes the talent available. Every player knows their role, their positioning, and their responsibilities in each phase of play. This discipline allows Croatia to compete with nations that have far greater resources.

**3. Tournament Mentality**
Croatia has an extraordinary record in tournament knockout matches that go to extra time or penalties. The team's mental resilience and composure under pressure has become a defining characteristic, with multiple victories in penalty shootouts across the 2018 and 2022 World Cups.

**4. Defensive Solidity**
While known for midfield creativity, Croatia's defensive organization under Dalic is often underappreciated. The backline is well-drilled, compact, and difficult to break down, providing the foundation upon which the midfield can express itself.

**5. Patient Build-Up**
Croatia does not rush in possession. They are comfortable circulating the ball, waiting for the right moment to penetrate. This patience can frustrate opponents and create openings as defenses lose their shape.

### Outlook

Croatia enters the tournament with realistic ambitions of reaching the knockout stages, as they have done in every World Cup since 2014 (and indeed in every World Cup they have qualified for since 1998).

**Best Case:** Modric delivers a fitting farewell, Gvardiol anchors a rock-solid defense, and Croatia's midfield once again controls matches against elite opposition. A deep run to the quarterfinals or beyond is achievable.

**Worst Case:** The emotional weight of Modric's farewell becomes a distraction, the defense is exposed by pace, and the younger generation is not quite ready to fill the void left by departed stars from 2018.

### Key Questions

1. How much does Luka Modric have left in the tank at 40?
2. Can Josko Gvardiol become the defensive leader Croatia needs?
3. Will the younger generation -- Baturina, Sucic, Vlasic -- step up on the biggest stage?
4. Can Croatia maintain their extraordinary knockout-stage resilience?

## Legacy and Culture

Croatian football culture is built on technical excellence, tactical intelligence, and an almost mystical ability to produce world-class midfielders. From Robert Prosinecki to Zvonimir Boban to Luka Modric, Croatia's midfield tradition is one of the richest in world football relative to the nation's size.

The famous red-and-white checkered jersey is one of the most iconic in international football. The pattern, derived from the Croatian coat of arms, is instantly recognizable and symbolizes national pride and identity.

The 2026 World Cup represents the end of an era. Modric's generation -- the one that reached the 2018 final and the 2022 semifinal -- is fading. But Croatia's football infrastructure, youth development, and cultural commitment to technical excellence ensure that the next generation will continue to punch above its weight.

This tournament is both a farewell to one of football's greatest players and a test of whether Croatia's remarkable World Cup pedigree can be maintained by a new wave of talent.
