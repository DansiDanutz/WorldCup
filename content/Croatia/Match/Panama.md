# Croatia vs Panama — Group L, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Croatia and Panama have **never met** in an official FIFA match.

---

## The Storyline: The Professionals vs the Dreamers

Croatia enters this match as heavy favorites. A nation of 3.9 million that has reached a World Cup final and two third-place finishes, facing a nation of 4.4 million making only their second World Cup appearance.

But Panama has nothing to lose. Their 2018 debut — despite three defeats — was celebrated as a national triumph. In 2026, they return with more experience and the same fighting spirit. Against a Croatia side that may be rotating or managing the group table, Panama will sense an opportunity.

For Luka Modric, this could be his final group-stage match for Croatia. If Croatia has already secured advancement, Dalic may rest his legend — but Modric might demand to play, wanting every possible moment in the checkered jersey.

## Key Player Matchups

### Josko Gvardiol vs Cecilio Waterman
Gvardiol's defensive class against Waterman's pace on the counter. If Waterman gets behind Croatia's high line, he could create a historic moment.

### Andrej Kramaric vs Panama's Center-Backs
Kramaric's movement against Panama's deep defensive block. The Croatian forward has the quality to find space where none seems to exist.

### Adalberto Carrasquilla vs Mateo Kovacic
Carrasquilla's creativity against Kovacic's ball retention. If Panama's playmaker can disrupt Croatia's midfield control, anything is possible.

## Tactical Preview

**Croatia (4-3-3):** Expected to dominate possession and territory. Patient build-up, looking to stretch Panama's defensive block. Modric's passing range (if he plays) can unlock any defense.

**Panama (5-4-1):** Maximum defensive discipline. Deep block, physical defending, and counter-attacks through Waterman and Barcenas. Set pieces are Panama's best hope for a result.

## X-Factor

**Croatia's motivation.** If Croatia has already qualified, their intensity could drop. Panama will fight for 90 minutes regardless of the score — that disparity in motivation has caused World Cup upsets before.
