# Croatia vs England — Group L, Matchday 1

**Date:** June 12, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

The Croatia-England rivalry has produced iconic moments:
- **2018 World Cup Semi-final:** Croatia 2-1 AET — One of Croatia's greatest ever victories
- **Euro 2021 Round of 16:** England 2-1 AET — England's revenge at Wembley

Each nation has won one crucial knockout match. This is a true rivalry, not a one-sided affair. The 2026 World Cup meeting adds a new chapter to a compelling story.

---

## The Storyline: Moscow Revisited

For Luka Modric, this match carries the weight of an era. The 2018 World Cup semi-final victory over England was arguably Croatia's greatest football moment — a match that defined Modric's Ballon d'Or year. Now, at 40, he faces England one final time on the world stage.

The symmetry is beautiful. In 2018, Modric was the master and Bellingham was a 14-year-old in Birmingham's academy. In 2026, Bellingham is Real Madrid's superstar and Modric is his mentor-turned-club-rival. The generational handover makes this match feel like a passing of the torch.

Zlatko Dalic will remind his players: Croatia beat England when it mattered most. That belief, forged in Moscow, still burns. But England's golden generation believes this is their time.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Luka Modric vs Jude Bellingham
The master and the heir. Modric's orchestration against Bellingham's power and drive. Both wear the number 5 for Real Madrid. This is more than a matchup — it's a narrative.

### Josko Gvardiol vs Harry Kane
Gvardiol's defensive intelligence against Kane's movement and finishing. Kane will drop deep; Gvardiol must decide whether to follow or hold the line.

### Dominik Livakovic vs England's Attackers
Livakovic was the hero of Croatia's 2022 penalty shootout victories. If this match stays tight, his big-game mentality could be the difference.

### Andrej Kramaric vs John Stones
Kramaric's movement against Stones' positional play. The Croatian forward has a habit of scoring in big matches.

## Tactical Preview

**Croatia (4-3-3):** Patient possession through midfield dominance. Modric and Kovacic will try to control tempo. Croatia will absorb England's pressing and strike through Kramaric's movement and set pieces. Their tournament experience is invaluable.

**England (4-2-3-1):** Aggressive pressing and quick transitions. Bellingham, Rice, and Foden provide a powerful midfield. England will try to overwhelm Croatia with pace and energy. Set pieces are always a threat.

---

## X-Factor

**Extra-time pedigree.** Both of their previous major tournament meetings went to extra time. Croatia has an extraordinary record in tournament knockout matches that go beyond 90 minutes. If this is close, Croatia's extra-time mentality could be decisive.
