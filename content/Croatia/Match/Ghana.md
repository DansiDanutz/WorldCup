# Croatia vs Ghana — Group L, Matchday 2

**Date:** June 17, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Croatia and Ghana have **never met** in an official FIFA match. This is their first encounter at any level.

---

## The Storyline: Technique vs Physicality

Croatia's technically gifted midfield against Ghana's physical, counter-attacking power — this is the classic European vs African tactical clash that World Cup group stages were made for.

Luka Modric, in his farewell tour, faces a Ghanaian midfield anchored by Thomas Partey. The contrast is stark: Modric's balletic passing range against Partey's muscular ball-winning. If Modric has time and space, Croatia will dominate. If Partey can disrupt Croatia's rhythm, Ghana can make this uncomfortable.

For Ghana, this is a crucial match. With England likely too strong and Panama the expected win, the Croatia match could determine whether the Black Stars advance. Carlos Queiroz will have a specific tactical plan to frustrate Croatia's patient build-up.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Luka Modric vs Thomas Partey
Modric's vision against Partey's power. This is the midfield duel that will decide the match. If Partey can unsettle Modric, Croatia's entire system is disrupted.

### Josko Gvardiol vs Inaki Williams
Gvardiol's defensive positioning against Williams' pace. One counter-attack could change everything.

### Andrej Kramaric vs Ghana's Center-Backs
Kramaric's movement against Ghana's physical defending. The Croatian forward thrives in tight spaces.

### Mateo Kovacic vs Antoine Semenyo
Kovacic's ball retention against Semenyo's pressing. If Semenyo can force turnovers, Ghana's counter-attack comes alive.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Croatia (4-3-3):** Patient possession, looking to control tempo through Modric and Kovacic. Croatia will try to draw Ghana's defensive block out of shape with patient passing before striking through Kramaric.

**Ghana (4-3-3):** Compact, physical, and direct. Queiroz's defensive organization will make Croatia work for every chance. On the counter, Williams, Semenyo, and Abdul Fatawu Issahaku have genuine pace. Set pieces are a major threat with Ghana's aerial strength.

---

## X-Factor

**Thomas Partey's fitness and form.** If Partey is at his best, he can single-handedly disrupt Croatia's midfield control. If he's not at full capacity, Modric will run the game.
