# Switzerland vs Bosnia and Herzegovina — Group B, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | BMO Field, Toronto | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Switzerland and Bosnia have met in European qualifying with Switzerland generally dominant. The familiarity from regional competition adds an extra layer of intensity to this matchup.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Granit Xhaka vs Miralem Pjanic
Two Balkan-origin midfield legends going head-to-head. Xhaka (Albanian-Kosovan heritage) against Pjanic (Bosnian). Both are 33-36 years old, both have played for the biggest clubs in the world. This could be the final time these two maestros share a pitch. It's a beautiful, bittersweet subplot.

### Manuel Akanji vs Benjamin Sesko
Inter Milan's "Professor" against Leipzig's rising star. Akanji has trained with Haaland — he knows how to handle big, fast strikers. But Sesko is his own player, and at 23, he's only getting better.

### Dan Ndoye vs Sead Kolasinac
Forest's "Flash" against Atalanta's "Tank." Speed against power on the flank. Ndoye's acceleration will test Kolasinac's recovery pace — a battle that could produce chances at both ends.

---

## Storyline: Balkan Brothers, Different Shirts

The Balkan connection makes this match uniquely emotional. Switzerland's squad is filled with players of Albanian, Kosovan, Bosnian, and Macedonian heritage — many could have played for the very nations they're facing. Xhaka, Akanji, Embolo, Ndoye, Jashari — all carry the DNA of the Balkans while wearing Swiss red. Bosnia's players carry the pride of a small nation that has produced extraordinary football talent despite its size. This match is about identity, heritage, and the beautiful complexity of European football.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Switzerland (3-4-2-1)** — Controlled, possession-based. Xhaka pulls strings, Embolo provides power, Akanji sweeps. Disciplined and experienced.

**Bosnia (4-2-3-1)** — Deep block, counter-attack. Sesko is the weapon, Dzeko the wise head, Pjanic the architect. They'll make Switzerland work for every chance.

---

## X-Factor: Breel Embolo's Moment

The Cameroonian-Swiss striker thrives in physical battles. Against Bosnia's rugged defense, Embolo's ability to hold the ball, draw fouls, and create space for Ndoye and Jashari could be the key to unlocking a stubborn opponent.
