# Switzerland vs Canada — Group B, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | BMO Field, Toronto | FIFA World Cup 2026

| Matches | Switzerland Wins | Draws | Canada Wins |

Switzerland has never lost to Canada. The most recent meeting was a **2-0 Swiss win** in June 2022. Canada will be desperate to break that streak on home soil.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Granit Xhaka vs Stephen Eustaquio
Two midfield generals, two different approaches. Xhaka (130+ caps, Sunderland) orchestrates with his left foot; Eustaquio (Porto) presses with relentless energy. The midfield that wins this battle wins the match.

### Manuel Akanji vs Jonathan David
Inter Milan's "Professor" against Lille's predator. Akanji is one of the world's best ball-playing defenders; David is one of Europe's most clinical strikers. This is a showcase of elite talent.

### Dan Ndoye vs Alphonso Davies
The "Flash" against Bayern Munich's flying wing-back. Ndoye's acceleration against Davies' recovery pace. Two of the fastest players in the tournament going head-to-head.

---

## Storyline: The Home Crowd Factor

Every previous meeting has been on neutral ground. This time, BMO Field will be a sea of red and white — Canadian red and white. Murat Yakin's Switzerland is tournament-tested: they've beaten France and Spain in recent major tournaments. But they've never faced a hostile crowd quite like this. Jesse Marsch will urge his team to feed off the energy. Switzerland's calm, methodical approach will be tested by Canada's chaos. Composure against intensity. Which wins?

---

## Tactical Preview

**Switzerland (3-4-2-1)** — Structured, possession-based, clinical. Akanji plays out from the back, Xhaka controls tempo, Embolo and Ndoye provide the thrust. Ardon Jashari (AC Milan, 23) adds youthful energy.

**Canada (4-4-2)** — High press, transition speed, physicality. Davies and Buchanan are the wide weapons, David and Larin the finishers.

---

## X-Factor: Ardon Jashari's Engine

The 23-year-old AC Milan midfielder covers ground like few others. His box-to-box energy against Canada's pressing system could be decisive. If Jashari wins the physical battle, Switzerland controls the match.
