### Best Result

**Quarter-finals** on four occasions (1934, 1938, 1954, and most notably a deep run at Euro 2024 where they reached the quarter-finals before losing on penalties to England). Switzerland has never reached a World Cup semi-final but has consistently been a difficult knockout opponent.

### Recent Tournament Record

## Road to 2026 Qualification

Switzerland topped their qualifying group ahead of Sweden, securing automatic qualification without the need for play-offs. The campaign was characterized by defensive solidity and the continued excellence of Granit Xhaka in midfield. Murat Yakin's team won the group with games to spare, demonstrating the kind of tournament-ready mentality that has become their trademark. Key results included home and away victories over Sweden and a disciplined defensive record throughout the campaign.

---

## Playing Style

Under Murat Yakin, Switzerland plays a **structured 3-4-2-1 / 4-2-3-1** hybrid that emphasizes:

- **Defensive Organization:** Built on the foundation of Manuel Akanji's ball-playing ability at the back, complemented by Nico Elvedi's consistency
- **Midfield Control:** Granit Xhaka orchestrates play from deep, combining with Remo Freuler and Ardon Jashari to dominate possession
- **Attacking Flexibility:** Dan Ndoye's dribbling and directness on the wing, combined with Breel Embolo's physical presence up front
- **Set-Piece Threat:** Ricardo Rodriguez's delivery from wide areas and set pieces is among the best in the tournament
- **Tournament Experience:** This squad has been together for multiple tournaments, giving them a collective understanding that few teams can match

---

## Expectations for 2026

Switzerland enters Group B as one of the favorites to advance. With Canada, Bosnia & Herzegovina, and Qatar as opponents, the group is favorable on paper. Expectations are high for a deep run.

**Realistic target:** Winning the group and reaching at least the quarter-finals. With the experience of Xhaka, Akanji, and the rest of the core, Switzerland has the quality to beat most teams in the tournament. A semi-final appearance would be historic but is not out of reach given the favorable bracket path.

**Key question:** Can the attack be clinical enough? The defense and midfield are proven, but Switzerland has historically struggled to score goals against elite opposition. The form of Embolo, Ndoye, and Zeki Amdouni will be crucial.
