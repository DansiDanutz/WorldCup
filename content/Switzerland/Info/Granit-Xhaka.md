# Granit Xhaka - Player Profile

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Granit Xhaka
- **Nickname(s):** "The General," "Xhakattack"
- **Date of Birth:** September 27, 1992
- **Age:** 33
- **Position:** Defensive Midfielder / Central Midfielder
- **Current Club:** Sunderland AFC (England)
- **Nationality:** Swiss (Albanian-Kosovan heritage)
- **Height:** 186 cm (6'1")
- **Preferred Foot:** Left
- **Role:** Captain of Switzerland National Team

---

## Skills and Strengths

- **Passing Range:** One of the best long-range passers in European football; can switch play effortlessly with either foot
- **Leadership:** Inspirational captain who leads by example; Switzerland's heartbeat for over a decade
- **Tackling and Interception:** Excellent at reading the game and breaking up opposition attacks
- **Set-Piece Delivery:** Deadly from free kicks and corners with his lethal left foot
- **Durability:** Remarkably consistent availability throughout his career; rarely injured
- **Experience:** 145 international caps -- Switzerland's all-time record appearance holder
- **Competitiveness:** Fierce will to win that sometimes crosses the line but inspires teammates

## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. Brother vs Brother at Euro 2016

One of the most extraordinary stories in European Championship history: Granit Xhaka played for Switzerland against his own brother, Taulant Xhaka, who represented Albania, at Euro 2016 in France. Both brothers were born in Basel to Albanian-Kosovan parents, but Granit chose Switzerland (where he grew up) while Taulant chose Albania (their ancestral homeland). The match was deeply emotional for the entire family. Their mother was photographed wearing a half-Swiss, half-Albanian jersey. Switzerland won 1-0, and both brothers embraced at full-time. Granit later said it was the most difficult match of his career.

### 2. The Redemption Arc at Arsenal

Xhaka's Arsenal career was defined by extreme highs and lows. In October 2019, he was stripped of the captaincy after an angry confrontation with his own fans during a match against Crystal Palace, where he cupped his ear and told supporters to "f*** off" as he was substituted. Many thought his Arsenal career was over. Instead, under Mikel Arteta, he rebuilt his reputation completely, becoming one of Arsenal's most important players and a leader in the dressing room. He left Arsenal in 2023 as a beloved figure, moving to Bayer Leverkusen where he won the Bundesliga, before transferring to Sunderland in 2025. The redemption arc is considered one of the most remarkable in recent Premier League history.
