## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. From Lucerne Local Hero to AC Milan Star

Jashari came through the youth system at FC Lucerne, a mid-table Swiss Super League club, and was considered a local talent rather than a can't-miss prospect. He made his debut at 17 and quickly became the team's most important player, catching the eye of bigger clubs with his performances in European competition. When AC Milan signed him in 2024, the transfer fee was reported at around 15 million euros -- a record sale for FC Lucerne. The people of Lucerne celebrated his success as their own, and Jashari has spoken about how the club gave him the platform to develop without too much pressure too early. His rise from Swiss league regular to AC Milan starter in just two years surprised even Swiss football observers.

### 2. The Albanian-Swiss Brotherhood with Xhaka

Jashari shares Albanian heritage with Switzerland captain Granit Xhaka, and the two have developed a special bond within the national team. Both players' families originate from the Balkans, and they communicate in Albanian as well as German and English during matches. Xhaka has taken Jashari under his wing, mentoring the younger midfielder in the same way that older players once mentored him. The connection goes beyond football -- both understand the experience of growing up between two cultures in Switzerland. Jashari has said that having Xhaka as a mentor has been "worth more than any tactical instruction" and that the captain's belief in him gave him the confidence to succeed at AC Milan.
