# Switzerland - Head Coach: Murat Yakin

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Murat Yakin
- **Date of Birth:** September 14, 1974
- **Age:** 51
- **Nationality:** Swiss (Turkish heritage)
- **Current Role:** Head Coach, Switzerland Men's National Team (since August 2021)

---

## Playing Career

Murat Yakin was a accomplished centre-back who enjoyed a successful playing career in Switzerland before moving into management.

### Club Career

- **Grasshopper Club Zurich** (1991-1994, 2003-2004) -- Won Swiss league titles
- **VfB Stuttgart** (1994-1995) -- Brief spell in the Bundesliga
- **Fenerbahce** (1995-1996) -- Turkish Super Lig experience
- **FC Basel** (1996-2001, 2002-2003) -- Won multiple Swiss titles and cups; club legend
- **FC Kaiserslautern** (2001-2002) -- Bundesliga
- **FC Lucerne** (2005-2006) -- Final playing years

### International Career

- **49 caps for Switzerland** -- scored 4 goals
- Represented Switzerland at Euro 2004
- Was a rock-solid defender known for his leadership and tactical intelligence

---

## Managerial Career

### FC Basel (2012-2014)

Yakin's first major managerial role came at Basel, where he achieved significant success:

- **Two Swiss Super League titles** (2012-13, 2013-14)
- **Swiss Cup winner** (2012-13)
- Guided Basel to the **Champions League knockout stages** and notable victories over Chelsea
- His attacking, high-tempo football won widespread praise

### Spartak Moscow (2014-2015)

A brief and difficult spell in Russia. Yakin struggled to adapt to the different footballing culture and was sacked after less than a year. The experience was humbling but educational.

### FC Zurich (2015-2016)

Returned to Switzerland but the stint was short-lived, with mixed results at a club in transition.

### FC Schaffhausen (2017-2018)

Worked with limited resources in the Swiss Challenge League, further developing his coaching philosophy.

### FC Sion (2018-2020)

Managed in the Swiss Super League, gaining experience working with diverse squads.

### Grasshopper Club Zurich (2020-2021)

A return to his boyhood club, where he helped stabilize the team before being approached for the national team job.

### Switzerland National Team (2021 - Present)

Appointed in August 2021, Yakin has transformed Switzerland into one of Europe's most reliable tournament teams:

- **Qualified for 2022 World Cup** -- reached Round of 16
- **Euro 2024 Quarter-finals** -- best European Championship run in modern history, beating Italy in the Round of 16 before losing on penalties to England
- **Qualified for 2026 World Cup** as group winners, ahead of Sweden
- Built a cohesive, tactically flexible team that punches above its weight

---

## Tactical Approach

Yakin's Switzerland is characterized by a **blend of defensive solidity and attacking ingenuity**:

- **Flexible Shape:** Comfortable switching between 3-4-2-1 and 4-2-3-1 depending on the opponent
- **Build-Up Play:** Emphasizes playing out from the back, with Akanji and Elvedi comfortable in possession
- **Midfield Control:** Xhaka and Freuler provide the platform, with Jashari and Rieder adding creativity
- **Defensive Discipline:** Organized pressing triggers and compact defensive shape make Switzerland difficult to break down
- **Bold In-Game Decisions:** Not afraid to make early substitutions or tactical changes when things are not working
- **Set-Piece Expertise:** Meticulous preparation on dead-ball situations at both ends

His assistant, Giuseppe Contini, is credited with much of the tactical innovation, providing a flexible and creative tactical brain alongside Yakin's man-management.

## Personal Notes

- Born in Basel to Turkish immigrant parents
- His younger brother, Hakan Yakin, was also a Swiss international (87 caps, 20 goals) and one of Switzerland's greatest-ever players
- Known for his calm, measured demeanor on the touchline -- rarely seen gesticulating wildly
- Speaks German, Turkish, French, and English fluently
- Deeply respected by his players for his honesty and tactical knowledge
