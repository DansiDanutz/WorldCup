## Interesting Facts

### Fact 1: Born in Bavaria, Chose Turkey
Kenan Yildiz was born in Regensburg, Bavaria, Germany, to a Turkish father and German mother. He grew up in Bayern Munich's youth academy — one of the world's best — before making the bold decision to leave for Juventus at age 17. Despite being eligible for both Germany and Turkey, he chose to represent Turkey internationally, a decision that was celebrated across Turkey and regretted in Germany. He reportedly received personal calls from both federation presidents before making his choice.

### Fact 2: Record-Breaking Youngster
Yildiz scored 15 goals in 30 matches for Juventus's Primavera (youth) team — a goal every two games — which prompted then-manager Massimiliano Allegri to promote him to the first team. At the time of his senior debut, he was the youngest Turkish player to ever appear in Serie A. His 2025/26 season was his breakthrough, scoring 10 league goals and winning Serie A's Rising Star award, cementing his status as one of Europe's most exciting young players.
