# Mexico vs South Korea — Group A, Matchday 2

**June 16, 2026** | Estadio Akron, Guadalajara | FIFA World Cup 2026

| Matches | Mexico Wins | Draws | South Korea Wins |

| 6 | 4 | 1 | 1 |

Mexico dominates this fixture. In World Cup meetings, Mexico is **2-0** — winning 3-1 in 1998 and 2-1 in 2018. Son Heung-min scored a consolation goal in that 2018 defeat, a memory that still burns.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Son Heung-min vs Cesar Montes
"Sonaldo" against Mexico's defensive rock. Son has 52+ international goals and is South Korea's greatest-ever player. Montes, the Almeria centre-back, must track Son's intelligent diagonal runs — give him an inch and he'll score from anywhere.

### Lee Kang-in vs Edson Alvarez
PSG's creative genius against Fenerbahce's midfield destroyer. Lee Kang-in's vision and close control can unlock any defense, but Alvarez has 90+ caps of experience in shutting down playmakers. This is the battle that decides the game.

### Kim Min-jae vs Santiago Gimenez
"The Monster" Kim (Bayern Munich) against "Bebelo" Gimenez (AC Milan). Two players at European giants, both desperate to announce themselves on the World Cup stage. Kim's physicality against Gimenez's movement is heavyweight vs heavyweight.

---

## Storyline: Son's Last Dance?

At 33, this is likely Son Heung-min's final World Cup. The Premier League Golden Boot winner has done everything in club football but has never led South Korea past the Round of 16 on the biggest stage. Mexico stands in his way again — the team that eliminated him in 2018. Meanwhile, Mexico's Hirving Lozano knows the Korean league well from his time studying Asian football's rise. Two nations with World Cup quarterfinal ceilings, both desperate to break through. Something has to give.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Mexico (4-3-3)** — Aguirre will press high, using Lozano's speed to pin back South Korea's wingbacks. Obed Vargas pulling strings from deep.

**South Korea (4-2-3-1)** — Hong Myung-bo sets up to protect Kim Min-jae and counter through Son. Hwang Hee-chan's "Bull" mentality from Wolves gives them physical presence on the break.

---

## X-Factor: Hwang In-beom's Control

The Feyenoord midfielder is South Korea's unsung hero. If Hwang In-beom can establish possession rhythm against Mexico's press, South Korea can dictate terms. If Mexico overwhelms him, it's a long night for the Taegeuk Warriors.
