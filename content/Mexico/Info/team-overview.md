## Team History

### Three-Time Hosts

Mexico is making history as the **first nation to host a World Cup three times** (1970, 1986, 2026). El Tri is one of the most iconic teams in CONCACAF, with a passionate fanbase that turns every stadium into a sea of green.

### World Cup Record

- **Best result**: Quarterfinals (1970, 1986)
- **Appearances**: 17 (including 2026)
- **Titles**: 0
- **CONCACAF Gold Cups**: 12 (record)

### World Cup Performances

| Year | Host | Result | Key Moment |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| 1970 | Mexico | Quarterfinals | Hosted a brilliant tournament; lost 4-1 to Italy in quarters |
| 1986 | Mexico | Quarterfinals | Hosted again after Colombia withdrew; knocked out by West Germany on penalties |
| 2014 | Brazil | Round of 16 | Won group stage but lost to Netherlands in controversial late goals |
| 2018 | Russia | Round of 16 | Beat Germany 1-0 in group stage but fell to Brazil in R16 |
| 2022 | Qatar | Group Stage | Failed to advance from group for first time since 1978 |

## Playing Style

### Mexican Football Identity

Mexico's style blends technical skill with explosive counter-attacking football. Under Javier Aguirre, El Tri has adopted a more pragmatic approach:

- **Defensive solidity**: Organized mid-block with disciplined pressing
- **Quick transitions**: Rapid counter-attacks utilizing pacy wingers
- **Set-piece threat**: Strong aerial presence from Montes and Reyes
- **Home advantage**: The Estadio Azteca at 2,240m altitude is a fortress

## 2026 Outlook

As co-hosts, Mexico has automatic qualification and home advantage. Group A is winnable, with no traditional powerhouse. A Round of 32 appearance is the minimum expectation, with dreams of a first-ever quarterfinal on home soil.
