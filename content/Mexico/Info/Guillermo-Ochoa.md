## Career Journey

### Club Career
- **Club America** (2004–2011) — Debuted at 18, became Mexico's top young goalkeeper
- **Ajaccio** (2011–2014) — French Ligue 1, proved himself in Europe
- **Malaga** (2014–2017) — La Liga experience
- **Granada** (2017–2019) — Returned to Spain
- **Club America** (2019–2022) — Second stint, legendary status
- **Salernitana** (2022–2023) — Serie A
- **AEL Limassol** (2023–present) — Cyprus

### World Cup Legend
Ochoa's 2014 World Cup performance against Brazil (8 saves, clean sheet) is considered one of the greatest individual goalkeeping performances in World Cup history. His point-blank save from Neymar's header became iconic.

---

## Playing Style

- **Shot-stopping**: World-class reflexes, especially in big matches
- **Leadership**: Commands his area, organizes the defense
- **Experience**: Unmatched composure in high-pressure situations
- **Distribution**: Good with feet, comfortable playing out from the back
- **Penalty saving**: Historically strong penalty saver

## Fun Facts

- One of only 4 players in history to be named to 6 World Cup squads (alongside Gianluigi Buffon, Andres Guardado, and Rafael Marquez)
- His 2014 Brazil game earned him the nickname "San Memo" (Saint Memo) in Mexico
- Has played professionally in 5 different countries
- At 40, he's the oldest player at the 2026 World Cup
- His pre-match ritual includes touching both goalposts and the crossbar
