## Key Player Matchups

### Julio Enciso vs Tyler Adams
The creative fulcrum against the midfield destroyer. Enciso — nicknamed "La Joya" (The Jewel)" — possesses the kind of talent that could illuminate this World Cup. His Goal of the Season against Manchester City proved he can produce moments of pure magic. But Adams' relentless pressing and ball-winning could suffocate the space Enciso needs to operate. If "La Joya" finds room to shine, the USA could be in trouble.

### Miguel Almiron vs USA's Left Side
The Paraguayan speedster returns to American soil where he became an MLS legend at Atlanta United. Almiron knows these stadiums, these crowds, this football culture. His Premier League experience — 223 appearances for Newcastle — has sharpened his game to a fine edge. The USA's left-back will face a severe test of pace and endurance.

### Gustavo Gomez vs Folarin Balogun
The captain's burden. Gomez, with 88+ caps, is the defensive rock upon which Paraguay's entire system is built. He must contain Balogun's intelligent movement and predatory finishing. The battle between Gomez's experience and Balogun's youth could determine the outcome.

## Storyline: 16 Years of Waiting

Imagine being told you weren't good enough for the biggest party in world football — for 16 years. That's the reality Paraguay has lived since their glorious 2010 quarter-final run in South Africa. A nation of just 7 million people, wedged between Brazil and Argentina, watched as World Cup after World Cup passed them by.

Gustavo Alfaro changed everything. The Argentine coach arrived in August 2024 and immediately instilled the belief that had been missing. A nine-match unbeaten run in CONMEBOL qualifying — surviving matches against Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, and Uruguay — earned Paraguay their ticket back to the World Cup.

Now they open against the host nation. In a stadium packed with 80,000 American fans. With the weight of a continent's footballing pride on their shoulders. Nobody expects Paraguay to win. But CONMEBOL teams have a history of silencing doubters at World Cups.

## Tactical Preview

**Paraguay (4-4-2 / 4-2-3-1):** Alfaro will set up in a compact, disciplined block designed to frustrate. Almiron and Enciso provide the counter-attacking threat, while Gomez and Alderete form a centre-back partnership that conceded few goals in qualifying. Set pieces are a major weapon — Paraguay's aerial threat from corners and free kicks is genuine.

**USA (4-3-3):** Expect the Americans to dominate possession and probe for openings. Pulisic's dribbling and Reyna's creativity will test Paraguay's defensive patience. The key for Paraguay is maintaining shape and discipline — any loss of concentration could be punished by a team with genuine attacking quality.

---

## X-Factor

**Miguel Almiron's homecoming.** The winger spent two spectacular seasons at Atlanta United before moving to Newcastle. He knows American football culture intimately. In AT&T Stadium, just a short flight from Atlanta, there may even be fans who remember him fondly. But on the pitch, there will be no sentiment — only the blistering pace and relentless work rate that made him a Premier League regular for six seasons.
