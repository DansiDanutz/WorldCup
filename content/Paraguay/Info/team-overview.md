## Team History

### The Heart of South America

Paraguay's national football team, known as **La Albirroja** (The White and Red), carries a proud football tradition disproportionate to the country's small population of approximately 7 million. Nestled between the footballing giants of Brazil and Argentina, Paraguay has consistently punched above its weight on the international stage, producing world-class players and achieving results that defy the nation's size.

Football arrived in Paraguay in the late 19th century via Dutch immigrant William Paats, and the Paraguayan Football Association was founded in 1906. The domestic league, while modest by global standards, has been a consistent producer of talent, particularly defensive midfielders, rugged defenders, and clinical strikers.

### The Golden Era (1998-2010)

Paraguay's most successful period came between 1998 and 2010, when they qualified for four consecutive World Cups:

- **1998 France:** Group Stage (lost to eventual champions France in the Round of 16 via a Laurent Blanc golden goal)
- **2002 Korea/Japan:** Round of 16 (eliminated by eventual runners-up Germany)
- **2006 Germany:** Group Stage
- **2010 South Africa:** **Quarter-finals** -- Paraguay's greatest-ever World Cup achievement, eliminated 1-0 by eventual champions Spain

The 2010 campaign was particularly heroic. Coached by Gerardo Martino, Paraguay topped their group ahead of Italy, beat Japan on penalties in the Round of 16, and pushed Spain to the limit in the quarter-finals. In that match against Spain, both teams missed penalties before David Villa's late winner ended the fairy tale.

### The Wilderness Years (2014-2022)

After the 2010 high, Paraguay entered a difficult period. They failed to qualify for both the 2014 and 2018 World Cups and also missed the 2022 tournament. The decline was attributed to a generational transition, with the golden-era players retiring and insufficient quality coming through to replace them.

## Playing Style

Under coach **Gustavo Alfaro**, Paraguay has returned to the qualities that have historically defined them:

- **Defensive solidity:** Built around captain Gustavo Gomez and Omar Alderete, the defense is organized, physical, and difficult to break down
- **Set-piece threat:** Paraguay is dangerous from dead-ball situations, leveraging the aerial ability of their defenders
- **Counter-attacking football:** With the pace and creativity of Julio Enciso, Miguel Almiron, and Ramon Sosa, Paraguay can hurt teams on the break
- **Tactical discipline:** Alfaro is known for meticulous preparation and disciplined team shape
- **CONMEBOL grit:** Qualifying through South America means Paraguay has faced Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, and Uruguay -- they are battle-tested against the world's best

---

## Expectations for 2026

Paraguay returns to the World Cup after a 16-year absence with a team that blends youth and experience:

- **United States:** The host nation will be the group favorite, but Paraguay's CONMEBOL experience makes them a dangerous opponent
- **Australia:** A physically similar opponent -- this match could determine who finishes third or higher
- **Turkey:** European flair vs South American grit -- a fascinating tactical battle

**Realistic target:** Advancing from the group stage would be a major achievement after 16 years away. The expanded 48-team format gives Paraguay a realistic chance even if they finish third.

**X-Factor:** **Julio Enciso** -- the 22-year-old playmaker, nicknamed "La Joya" (The Jewel), is considered one of the most talented Paraguayan players in the last 20 years. If he can reproduce his Strasbourg form on the World Cup stage, Paraguay could surprise many observers.
