# Haiti National Football Team — Les Grenadiers

## Overview

The Haiti national football team, known as **Les Grenadiers** (The Grenadiers), represents Haiti in international football. Haiti has a proud football history and was the first Caribbean nation to qualify for a FIFA World Cup, achieving this feat in 1974.

## World Cup Record
| Year | Result | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1974 | Group Stage | First Caribbean team to qualify for World Cup |
| 2026 | Qualified | Second World Cup appearance, 52-year gap |

- **Appearances:** 2 (1974, 2026)
- **CONCACAF Gold Cup:** Champions (1973, as CONCACAF Championship)
- **Caribbean Cup:** Champions (2007)

## Playing Style

Haiti plays an athletic, physical brand of football with emphasis on pace and power. Many players are developed in France's youth system (Haiti's large diaspora in France provides significant talent).

### Key Tactical Elements
- **Athleticism:** Speed and strength across all positions
- **Counter-Attacking:** Rapid transitions using pace
- **Physicality:** Strong challenges and aerial dominance
- **Resilience:** Fighting spirit that reflects the nation's character

## Notable Achievements
- First Caribbean team to qualify for a World Cup (1974)
- CONCACAF champions (1973)
- 52-year gap between World Cup appearances (tied with DR Congo)

## Famous Players
- **Emmanuel Sanon** — Scored against Italy at 1974 World Cup
- **Jean-Jacques Tizié** — Goalkeeping hero
- **Jeff Louis** — Creative midfielder
