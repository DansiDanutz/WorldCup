# Haiti vs Brazil — Group C, Matchday 2

**June 17, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Brazil has dominated this fixture historically. The most notable meeting was the **7-1 thrashing** at the 2016 Copa América Centenario — Haiti's heaviest competitive defeat. But that was a different era. Haiti has rebuilt through their French diaspora pipeline.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Duckens Nazon vs Alisson
KV Kortrijk's powerful forward against Liverpool's world-class goalkeeper. Nazon has pace and finishing ability — he'll need the game of his life to beat Alisson, but one clean strike is all it takes.

### Derrick Etienne Jr vs Bruno Guimaraes
Toronto FC's creative midfielder against Newcastle's midfield general. Etienne has 150+ MLS appearances and technical quality. But Guimaraes operates at a different level. Etienne must use his creativity to find space between Brazil's lines.

### Johny Placide vs Neymar
Caen's veteran goalkeeper against Santos' returning king. Placide has 300+ career appearances and won't be intimidated. But stopping Neymar at a World Cup requires more than experience — it requires a miracle.

---

## Storyline: The Ultimate Underdog Story

Haiti's return to the World Cup after 52 years is one of the most emotional stories of the tournament. Les Grenadiers carry the hopes of a nation that has endured unimaginable hardship — the 2010 earthquake, political instability, and economic challenges. Football offers joy, unity, and pride. Gabriel Calderón, the Argentine coach, has instilled discipline and fight. Nobody expects Haiti to beat Brazil. But in a sport that has produced Cameroon beating Argentina in 1990, Senegal beating France in 2002, and Saudi Arabia beating Argentina in 2022, anything is possible for one magical night.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Haiti (4-4-2)** — Deep block, physical, counter-attacking. Nazon and Foster provide pace on the break. Placide organizes from the back. They'll concede possession and territory — and pray for moments.

**Brazil (4-3-3)** — Ancelotti's side will dominate the ball and probe for openings. Even with rotation, the quality gap is enormous. Brazil will attack in waves.

---

## X-Factor: Haiti's Diaspora Heart

Many of Haiti's players grew up in France's youth system — Duverne at Nantes, Arcus at Auxerre, Nazon in French academies. They carry both cultures. That dual identity — French technical education with Caribbean passion — could produce something unexpected.
