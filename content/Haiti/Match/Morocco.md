# Haiti vs Morocco — Group C, Matchday 2

**June 17, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**Very limited history.** First competitive encounter between these nations. The African-Caribbean connection adds cultural intrigue to an unfamiliar fixture.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Duckens Nazon vs Romain Saïss
KV Kortrijk's pacy forward against Al-Shabab's veteran centre-back (80 caps). Nazon's acceleration against Saïss's positioning intelligence. The Moroccan defender has seen faster players, but Nazon's directness is a weapon.

### Derrick Etienne Jr vs Sofyan Amrabat
Toronto FC's creative midfielder against Fenerbahce's tireless engine. Etienne's trickery against Amrabat's relentless pressing. If Etienne can escape Amrabat's shadow, Haiti creates chances.

### Johny Placide vs Youssef En-Nesyri
Caen's veteran goalkeeper against Fenerbahce's aerial specialist. Placide's experience against En-Nesyri's heading ability. Set pieces will be critical — En-Nesyri is lethal from crosses.

---

## Storyline: Caribbean Warriors vs Atlas Lions

Haiti and Morocco share a bond through Africa — Haiti's population has deep West African roots, and both nations have produced football talent through diaspora communities. But Morocco is Africa's gold standard: a World Cup semifinalist with players at the world's biggest clubs. Haiti is appearing in only their second World Cup. The gap in resources, infrastructure, and player quality is vast. But heart cannot be measured in resources. Gabriel Calderón will have Les Grenadiers organized, disciplined, and fearless. Morocco must match that intensity or risk a shock.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Haiti (4-4-2)** — Deep and physical, counter through Nazon's pace and Foster's hold-up play. Placide commands from the back. They'll fight for every ball.

**Morocco (4-2-3-1)** — Regragui's proven system. Hakimi attacks from right-back, Ziyech creates, En-Nesyri finishes. The quality advantage is significant.

---

## X-Factor: Carlens Arcus's Overlapping Runs

The Auxerre right-back is Haiti's most European-tested defender. His overlapping runs provide width and could surprise Morocco's defense. If Arcus can get forward and deliver quality crosses, Haiti has forwards who can finish.
