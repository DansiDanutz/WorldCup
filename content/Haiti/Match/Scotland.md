# Haiti vs Scotland — Group C, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**No previous meetings.** First-ever encounter between Haiti and Scotland. Two nations with passionate football cultures but no shared history on the pitch.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Duckens Nazon vs Kieran Tierney
KV Kortrijk's forward against Celtic's versatile defender. Nazon's pace against Tierney's commitment and toughness. Both players give everything for their national team — this will be a battle of pure will.

### Derrick Etienne Jr vs John McGinn
Toronto FC's creator against Aston Villa's midfield powerhouse. Etienne's technical quality against McGinn's 20-goal international record from midfield. McGinn's goal threat makes him dangerous even when Scotland doesn't have the ball.

### Johny Placide vs Lawrence Shankland
Caen's veteran goalkeeper against Hearts' prolific striker. Placide has 40 caps and 300+ career appearances; Shankland has 7 goals in 20 Scotland caps. The battle of the finishers.

---

## Storyline: Two Nations, One Dream

Both Haiti and Scotland enter this match knowing it could be their best chance for points in Group C. Haiti's World Cup story is emotional — 52 years between appearances, a nation united by football. Scotland's is agonizing — eight World Cups, never past the group stage. The Tartan Army will fill SoFi Stadium with bagpipes and passion. Haiti's fans will bring Caribbean energy and rhythm. Two sets of supporters who know heartbreak intimately, both praying for a moment of joy. This match will be played with raw emotion from first whistle to last.

## Tactical Preview

**Haiti (4-4-2)** — Physical, direct, counter-attacking. Nazon and Foster offer pace and power. Calderón's Argentine influence brings defensive structure.

**Scotland (5-4-1 or 4-4-2)** — Clarke's organized system. Robertson and Tierney provide width, McTominay and McGinn add goals from midfield, Shankland leads the line. They'll be favorites but won't take Haiti lightly.

---

## X-Factor: Andy Robertson's Leadership

The Liverpool captain has 94 Scotland caps and leads by example. His emotional investment in Scotland's World Cup campaign is absolute. If the match is tight in the final minutes, Robertson's drive and determination could push Scotland over the line. But he'll need to be careful — Haiti's counter-attacks can punish overcommitment.
