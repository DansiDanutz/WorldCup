# Japan - FIFA World Cup 2026

## Group F: Netherlands, Japan, Sweden, Tunisia

## Team History

### Samurai Blue

Japan has established itself as Asia's premier footballing nation, qualifying for every World Cup since 1998 and consistently reaching the knockout stages.

### World Cup Record

| Year | Host | Result | Key Moment |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| 2002 | South Korea/Japan | Round of 16 | Co-hosted; reached knockout stage for first time |
| 2010 | South Africa | Round of 16 | Lost on penalties to Paraguay |
| 2018 | Russia | Round of 16 | Led Belgium 2-0 before heartbreaking 3-2 loss |
| 2022 | Qatar | Round of 16 | Beat Germany and Spain in group stage; lost to Croatia on penalties |

### Giant Killers

Japan's 2022 World Cup campaign was historic -- stunning both Germany and Spain to win their group. The team has proven it can compete with football's elite nations.

## Playing Style

Japan plays a **technical, high-tempo 4-2-3-1** emphasizing:
- **Technical precision**: Every player is comfortable on the ball
- **Quick combinations**: Rapid one-touch passing in tight spaces
- **Discipline**: Exceptionally well-organized defensively
- **Set-piece efficiency**: Well-drilled routines from corners and free-kicks
- **Never-say-die spirit**: Famous for late goals and comebacks

---

## World Cup 2026 Prospects

Japan enters 2026 with its most talented squad ever, featuring players at Liverpool, Brighton, Celtic, and Monaco. Drawn in a tough group with the Netherlands, Sweden, and Tunisia, they will need their best performance to advance.

**Key Question**: Can Japan finally win a World Cup knockout match in 90 minutes?
