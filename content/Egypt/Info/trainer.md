## Playing Career — A Legend of African Football

Hossam Hassan is not just a manager — he is one of the greatest players in African football history:

- **Egypt's all-time top scorer** — 69 goals in 177 international appearances
- **Three-time AFCON champion** (1986, 1998, 2006) — A record that demonstrates his extraordinary longevity
- **20-year international career** — Debuted in 1985 and played until 2006
- **World Cup appearances** — Played at the 1990 World Cup in Italy
- **Club career**: Played for Al Ahly (three spells), Al Ain (UAE), Zamalek, and others
- **Total club goals**: Over 250 across his career
- Known for his lethal finishing, aerial ability, and leadership on the pitch

### Key Playing Records
- **Most capped Egyptian player** at the time of his retirement (177 caps)
- **AFCON all-time leading scorer** (at time of retirement)
- **Oldest player to score at an AFCON tournament** — achieving the feat at age 39

## Appointment to Egypt

Hossam Hassan was appointed head coach of the Egypt national team in **February 2024**, replacing Rui Vitoria. His appointment was celebrated across Egypt — bringing home a national hero to lead the Pharaohs. As both a legendary former player and an experienced domestic manager, Hassan commanded immediate respect from the squad.

His mandate was clear: qualify for the 2026 World Cup and make Egypt competitive on the global stage.

## Tactical Approach

Hassan employs a **4-2-3-1** formation with distinctly Egyptian characteristics:

### In Possession
- **Salah-centric attack** — Build play to get the ball to Mohamed Salah in dangerous areas
- **Marmoush as secondary creator** — The Manchester City forward operates in the half-spaces
- **Width from full-backs** — Ahmed Fatouh and Mohamed Hany push high to stretch defenses
- **Directness** — Egyptian football culture favors forward passing and quick transitions

### Out of Possession
- **Disciplined mid-block** — Not overly aggressive but well-organized
- **Double pivot protection** — Hamdy Fathy and Marwan Attia shield the back four
- **Counter-attacking threat** — Pace of Trezeguet and Marmoush on the break

### Philosophy
- **Trust in experience** — Hassan leans heavily on veteran players from Al Ahly and Zamalek
- **Emotional motivation** — As a former player who lived Egypt's greatest moments, he inspires through personal example
- **Domestic league knowledge** — His years managing in the Egyptian Premier League give him deep insight into the available player pool

## Achievements with Egypt

- **2026 World Cup qualification** — Secured Egypt's spot at the expanded tournament
- **Squad harmony** — Resolved tensions that existed under previous management
- **Youth integration** — Called up Hamza Abdelkarim (18) as a statement of trust in the next generation
