## Interesting Facts

### Fact 1: Named After a French Legend
Mahmoud Hassan earned his nickname "Trezeguet" as a child because of his striking resemblance to French striker David Trezeguet in his playing style — particularly his finishing and left-footed technique. The name stuck throughout his career, and he is universally known as "Trezeguet" rather than his given name. Ironically, David Trezeguet himself once commented that he was honored an Egyptian player carried his name with such distinction. The nickname has become so iconic that even official FIFA documents and squad lists use "Trezeguet" as his primary identifier.

### Fact 2: The Aston Villa Promotion Hero
Trezeguet played a crucial role in Aston Villa's promotion to the Premier League in 2019, scoring important goals during the EFL Championship play-off campaign. His time at Villa Park was marked by flashes of brilliance — including a memorable brace against Crystal Palace in the Premier League — but also frustration with inconsistent playing time. After leaving Aston Villa, he rebuilt his career in Turkey with Trabzonspor, where he became a fan favorite, before returning to boyhood club Al Ahly in 2024. His 11 goals in the 2025/26 Egyptian Premier League season at age 31 shows he is still improving.
