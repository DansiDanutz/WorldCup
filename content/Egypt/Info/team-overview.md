# Egypt National Football Team

## Overview

The Egypt national football team (Arabic: منتخب مصر لكرة القدم), known as **The Pharaohs**, represents Egypt in international football and is governed by the Egyptian Football Association (EFA). Egypt is the most successful nation in African football history, with a record seven Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) titles. Despite continental dominance, World Cup success has remained elusive — something the 2026 squad aims to change.

## FIFA World Cup Record

| Year | Result |
|------|--------|
| 1934 | Round of 16 |
| 1990 | Group Stage |
| 2018 | Group Stage |
| 2026 | Qualified (Group G) |

Egypt's World Cup history has been marked by long gaps between appearances — 56 years between 1934 and 1990, and 28 years between 1990 and 2018. The 2026 tournament represents a chance to build momentum with back-to-back World Cup qualifications for the first time.

Seven AFCON titles make Egypt the undisputed kings of African football. The hat-trick of titles in 2006, 2008, and 2010 — achieved under Hassan Shehata — is considered the golden era of Egyptian football.

## Key Achievements

- **Record 7x AFCON Champions** — Three more than any other nation
- **Three consecutive AFCON titles** (2006, 2008, 2010) — An unprecedented achievement
- **FIFA Ranking High**: Reached #9 in July 2010
- **2017 AFCON Runner-Up** — Return to prominence after years of struggle
- **2021 AFCON Runner-Up** — Led by Mohamed Salah's brilliance
- **2018 World Cup qualification** — Ended a 28-year World Cup drought

## Playing Style

Under manager Hossam Hassan, Egypt plays a **4-2-3-1** system that balances defensive solidity with attacking firepower:

- **Mohamed Salah as the focal point** — Everything flows through the Liverpool legend, whether cutting inside from the right or operating centrally
- **Solid defensive base** — Built around Al Ahly's domestic dominance and experienced campaigners
- **Counter-attacking threat** — Omar Marmoush and Trezeguet provide pace on the break
- **Set-piece organization** — Egyptian teams have historically been strong from dead-ball situations
- **Physical presence** — Egyptian players are known for strength and aerial ability

## 2026 World Cup Expectations

Egypt arrives at the 2026 World Cup with one of its most talented squads ever assembled:

- **Mohamed Salah** (Liverpool) — One of the greatest players in Premier League history
- **Omar Marmoush** (Manchester City) — Prolific forward thriving under Pep Guardiola
- **Trezeguet** (Al Ahly) — Experienced winger with 96 caps and 23 international goals
- **Mohamed El Shenawy** (Al Ahly) — Reliable goalkeeper with 76 caps

Drawn in **Group G**, Egypt has a realistic chance of advancing past the group stage for the first time in their World Cup history. The expanded 48-team format and the quality of their attack gives them genuine hope.

## Nickname

**The Pharaohs** (Arabic: الفراعنة) — A reference to ancient Egypt's rulers, symbolizing power and historical greatness.

## Home Stadium

- **Cairo International Stadium** (Capacity: 75,000) — Egypt's fortress and one of Africa's most iconic venues
- **Borg El Arab Stadium** (Alexandria) — Used for select fixtures

## Key Rivalries

- **Algeria** — Intense North African rivalry, heightened after controversial 2010 World Cup qualifiers
- **Cameroon** — Frequent AFCON meetings with high stakes
- **Nigeria** — Regional power rivalry
- **Tunisia** — North African derby matches
