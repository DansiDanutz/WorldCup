## Interesting Facts

### Fact 1: The Barcelona Connection
Hamza Abdelkarim is one of only a handful of Egyptian players to have been part of FC Barcelona's famed La Masia academy system. He joined Barcelona's youth setup as a teenager, impressing coaches with his technical ability and football intelligence. Playing for Barcelona B in the Spanish third division, he is learning his craft in one of football's most demanding environments. His inclusion in Egypt's World Cup squad at just 18 years old — with only 2 senior caps — is a statement of faith from manager Hossam Hassan in Barcelona's development of the player. If he gets on the pitch in 2026, he will be one of the youngest players in the entire tournament.

### Fact 2: Following in Salah's Footsteps
Abdelkarim's rapid rise mirrors Mohamed Salah's early career in some ways — an Egyptian talent spotted young and developed in European football. The parallels have not been lost on Egyptian media, who have dubbed him "The Next Salah" — a label he has publicly tried to distance himself from. He has stated in interviews that he wants to be "the first Hamza Abdelkarim, not the next anyone." His family made significant sacrifices to support his football career, and his selection for the World Cup squad at 18 has been celebrated as a vindication of that decision. Notably, Salah himself has taken a mentoring role with the youngster during national team camps.
