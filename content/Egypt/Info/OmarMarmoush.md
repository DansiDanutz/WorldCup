## Interesting Facts

### Fact 1: The 70 Million Euro Gamble
Manchester City signed Marmoush from Eintracht Frankfurt in January 2025 for approximately 70-75 million euros — a massive fee for a player who had only one exceptional half-season in the Bundesliga. At Frankfurt, he had scored 13 goals with 8 assists in the first half of 2024/25, making him one of Europe's most in-form players. Pep Guardiola personally pushed for the signing, seeing Marmoush as the perfect fit for his positional play system. The move made Marmoush the most expensive Egyptian player in history, surpassing even Mohamed Salah's transfer to Liverpool.

### Fact 2: From Cairo Streets to the Etihad
Marmoush grew up in Cairo and started his career at Wadi Degla, a modest Egyptian club. Unlike Salah, who was spotted by Basel early, Marmoush took a longer route through German football — starting at Stuttgart's reserve team, then St. Pauli in the second division, then Wolfsburg, before his breakthrough at Frankfurt. At one point during his St. Pauli loan, he was playing in Germany's 2. Bundesliga, far from the Champions League spotlight. His journey from Cairo's streets to Manchester City's Etihad Stadium, via Stuttgart, Hamburg, Wolfsburg, and Frankfurt, is one of football's great underdog stories.
