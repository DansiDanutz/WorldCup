# Egypt vs Belgium — Group G, Matchday 1

**Date:** June 12, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## The Pharaoh Meets the Red Devils

Egypt's first World Cup match since 2018 is a collision with Belgium's last golden generation. Mohamed Salah, at 33, leads a nation that has waited 36 years between World Cup appearances (1990 to 2018). Omar Marmoush, now at Manchester City, gives Egypt something they've never had: a genuine second superstar.

## Key Player Matchups

### Mohamed Salah vs Zeno Debast
Salah's lethal combination of pace, trickery, and finishing against the 21-year-old Sporting CP centre-back. Salah has been doing this to elite defenders for a decade. Debast is talented but untested at this level.

### Omar Marmoush vs Thomas Meunier
Marmoush's dynamic movement against the experienced Lille right-back. Marmoush's €70M move to Manchester City proved his quality. Meunier's 60+ caps give Belgium defensive know-how.

### Trezeguet vs Maxim De Cuyper
Trezeguet's 96 caps of experience against Brighton's De Cuyper. The Al Ahly veteran's cunning versus Premier League athleticism.

### Emam Ashour vs Youri Tielemans
The Al Ahly midfielder against Belgium's captain. Ashour's energy and pressing against Tielemans' composure and distribution.

## Storyline: Salah's Final Hour

In 2018, Salah arrived at the World Cup with a injured shoulder and a broken heart. He scored twice in two losses and Egypt went home pointless. The image of Salah walking off the pitch in Volgograd, alone with his thoughts, became the defining image of Egypt's tournament.

Eight years later, Salah is a different player — and a different man. He has won everything at Liverpool, broken every record, and cemented himself as the greatest African footballer of his generation. But the World Cup remains the void in his career. At 33, this is the last chance to fill it.

Hossam Hassan, Egypt's coach and a 1990 World Cup veteran, understands the weight of history. He has built a team that finally doesn't collapse when Salah has an off day. Marmoush, Trezeguet, Zizo — this Egypt squad has depth and resilience that the 2018 version lacked.

## Tactical Preview

**Egypt (4-3-3):** El Shenawy; Mohamed Hany, Abdelmonem, Rabia, Fatouh; Hamdy Fathy, Marwan Attia, Emam Ashour; Marmoush, Salah, Trezeguet

**Belgium (4-2-3-1):** Courtois; Meunier, Theate, Debast, De Cuyper; Onana, Tielemans; Doku, De Bruyne, Trossard; Lukaku

Egypt will absorb pressure and counter through Salah and Marmoush. The key is El Shenawy's distribution — if he can find Salah early in transitions, Belgium's high line becomes a vulnerability. The battle for midfield control between Hamdy Fathy and Tielemans sets the tone.

---

## X-Factor

**Omar Marmoush.** For years, Egypt was a one-man team. Now Marmoush gives them a Plan B, Plan C, and Plan D. His movement drags defenders away from Salah, creating space that hasn't existed for Egypt at previous tournaments. If Belgium double-team Salah, Marmoush will punish them.
