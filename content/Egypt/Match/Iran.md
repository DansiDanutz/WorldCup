# Egypt vs Iran — Group G, Matchday 2

**Date:** June 17, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## Desert Rivals, Distant Strangers

Two Middle Eastern powers meet on American soil in a match that carries the weight of regional pride. Egypt and Iran, separated by politics and geography, have rarely crossed paths on the football field. But both nations live and breathe football — and both arrive at this World Cup believing they can escape the group stage for the first time.

---

## Head-to-Head History

No recorded senior international meetings between Egypt and Iran.

This is a **first-ever meeting** between the two nations — a historic encounter.

## Key Player Matchups

### Mohamed Salah vs Milad Mohammadi
Salah's devastating right foot against Persepolis' experienced left-back. Mohammadi has 55 caps but has never faced a player of Salah's caliber. The Iranian must decide: press high and risk being beaten, or sit deep and surrender territory.

### Omar Marmoush vs Shojae Khalilzadeh
Marmoush's Manchester City sharpness against the Persepolis centre-back. Khalilzadeh is tough and committed but Marmoush's movement in the box could be decisive.

### Mehdi Taremi vs Mohamed Abdelmonem
Two strikers who score for fun at international level. Taremi's 59 goals for Iran against Abdelmonem's Nice defensive nous. The battle of the penalty box predators.

### Saeid Ezatolahi vs Emam Ashour
The midfield engine room. Ezatolahi's 82 caps against Ashour's Al Ahly energy. Whoever controls this zone controls the match.

## Storyline: The Forgotten Rivalry

Egypt and Iran share a football history that never was. Political tensions between the two nations have kept them apart for decades. But on the pitch, there is mutual respect — and mutual desperation. Both teams know that this match, sandwiched between Belgium and New Zealand, is likely the one that decides who advances from Group G.

For Iran's Mehdi Taremi, this is personal. He has scored 59 goals for his country but has never won a World Cup match. The 33-year-old Olympiacos striker knows this is his final chance. Without Azmoun beside him, the burden is heavier than ever.

For Egypt, the match offers a chance to prove they belong. Since their return to the World Cup in 2018 after a 28-year absence, they have yet to win a match. Iran, despite more recent World Cup appearances, have the same problem. One of these teams will finally break their duck.

## Tactical Preview

**Egypt (4-3-3):** El Shenawy; Mohamed Hany, Abdelmonem, Rabia, Fatouh; Hamdy Fathy, Marwan Attia, Emam Ashour; Marmoush, Salah, Trezeguet

**Iran (4-1-4-1):** Beiranvand; Rezaeian, Khalilzadeh, Kanaanizadegan, Mohammadi; Ezatolahi; Jahanbakhsh, Ghoddos, Torabi, Ghaedi; Taremi

Both teams prefer to counter-attack, which creates an interesting tactical paradox: who takes the initiative? Egypt, with Salah and Marmoush, have more individual quality. Iran, with Ezatolahi's midfield control, have more tactical discipline. The team that blinks first — the one that abandons its comfort zone — could be the one that loses.

---

## X-Factor

**Alireza Jahanbakhsh.** The Iran captain has 98 caps and 17 goals. He knows this is his final World Cup. His leadership on the pitch and ability to deliver from set pieces could be the difference in a match between two evenly matched teams. His free kicks are a genuine weapon.
