# Iran vs Egypt — Group G, Matchday 2

**Date:** June 17, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

## The Desert Derby

Two civilizations that have shaped human history meet on the football pitch. Iran and Egypt, both powerhouse nations of the Middle East and North Africa, have somehow never played each other at senior international level. This match — on American soil, far from both homelands — is a historic first.

## Key Player Matchups

Iran's all-time top scorer against Nice's centre-back. Taremi's 59 international goals are remarkable; Abdelmonem's positioning and anticipation are his strength. This is the battle that decides the match.

### Mohamed Salah vs Milad Mohammadi
The defining individual matchup. Salah's world-class movement against Persepolis' left-back. Mohammadi has 55 caps but Salah operates on a different plane.

### Omar Marmoush vs Shojae Khalilzadeh
Marmoush's Manchester City pedigree against the Persepolis enforcer. Khalilzadeh is tough but Marmoush's pace and trickery could be the difference.

### Saeid Ezatolahi vs Hamdy Fathy
The midfield generals. Ezatolahi's 82 caps against Fathy's 63 caps. Both are defensive midfielders who dictate tempo. This battle determines possession and control.

## Storyline: Two Nations, One Dream

Iran and Egypt share a painful World Cup history. Both nations have passionate football cultures, legendary domestic leagues, and millions of devoted fans — yet neither has ever progressed past the group stage of a World Cup. Iran have appeared in six tournaments (1978, 1998, 2006, 2014, 2018, 2022) without escaping the first round. Egypt's record is even more haunting: three appearances (1934, 1990, 2018) with zero group-stage victories.

This match, likely the pivotal Group G encounter, offers one of these nations a chance at redemption. Both coaches — Ghalenoei and Hassan — know that winning here could be the difference between history and heartbreak.

## Tactical Preview

**Iran (4-1-4-1):** Beiranvand; Rezaeian, Khalilzadeh, Kanaanizadegan, Mohammadi; Ezatolahi; Jahanbakhsh, Ghoddos, Torabi, Ghaedi; Taremi

**Egypt (4-3-3):** El Shenawy; Mohamed Hany, Abdelmonem, Rabia, Fatouh; Hamdy Fathy, Marwan Attia, Emam Ashour; Marmoush, Salah, Trezeguet

A fascinating tactical battle between two counter-attacking teams. Neither wants to dominate possession — both prefer to absorb and strike. The team that shows more initiative, that dares to push players forward, gains a crucial advantage. Egypt's individual quality (Salah, Marmoush) gives them the edge, but Iran's tactical discipline could neutralize that advantage.

## X-Factor

**Trezeguet's experience.** With 96 caps for Egypt, Trezeguet has seen everything. His time in the Premier League with Aston Villa, his success at Trabzonspor, and now his return to Al Ahly have forged a player who thrives in high-pressure moments. In a match where both teams are tight and tense, Trezeguet's composure could unlock the door.
