# Iran vs New Zealand — Group G, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## The Must-Win

Iran's final group match is likely a must-win scenario. Regardless of results against Belgium and Egypt, New Zealand represent Iran's best chance for three points. But the All Whites have a habit of making life miserable for superior teams — their 2010 draw with Italy proved that beyond doubt.

---

## Head-to-Head History

No recorded senior international meetings between Iran and New Zealand.

This is a **first-ever meeting** between the two nations.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Mehdi Taremi vs Michael Boxall
Taremi's predatory finishing against the 36-year-old MLS centre-back. Boxall's 50 caps give him experience but Taremi's movement is several levels above anything he faces domestically.

### Alireza Jahanbakhsh vs Liberato Cacace
Iran's captain against Empoli's left-back. Jahanbakhsh's crossing and set-piece delivery against Cacace's Serie A defensive work.

### Chris Wood vs Hossein Kanaanizadegan
Wood's aerial threat against the Persepolis centre-back. Kanaanizadegan has 53 caps of experience but Wood's Premier League physicality is a unique challenge.

### Saman Ghoddos vs Marko Stamenic
Ghoddos's creativity against the Nottingham Forest youngster. Stamenic is talented but Ghoddos's 67 caps of international experience could overwhelm him.

---

## Storyline: The Weight of a Nation

Iranian football fans are among the most passionate in the world. Every World Cup, millions gather around televisions from Tehran to Tabriz, from Isfahan to Shiraz, hoping — praying — that this will finally be the year. Six tournaments and zero group-stage escapes. The heartbreak is generational.

This match against New Zealand is Iran's most realistic path to three points. A win, combined with a result against either Belgium or Egypt, could make history. But the pressure is suffocating. Iran have crumbled under expectation before — the 2-6 loss to England in 2022 was partly attributed to the weight of the moment.

Coach Ghalenoei must manage the psychological burden as much as the tactical one. His players need to approach this match with confidence, not desperation.

## Tactical Preview

**Iran (4-1-4-1):** Beiranvand; Rezaeian, Kanaanizadegan, Khalilzadeh, Mohammadi; Ezatolahi; Jahanbakhsh, Ghoddos, Torabi, Ghaedi; Taremi

**New Zealand (4-4-2):** Sail; Elliot, Bindon, Boxall, Cacace; Howieson, Stamenic, Lewis, Singh; Wood, Barbarouses

Iran will control possession and territory. New Zealand will sit deep, compact, and look for set-piece opportunities through Wood's aerial ability. Iran's width comes from Jahanbakhsh's crossing — if he can deliver quality balls into the box, Taremi will finish them.

---

## X-Factor

**Set pieces on both sides.** Iran's primary attacking threat is dead-ball situations, but New Zealand are equally dangerous through Chris Wood's heading. This match could be decided by which team is more clinical from corners and free kicks — making every foul in dangerous areas a potential match-winning moment.
