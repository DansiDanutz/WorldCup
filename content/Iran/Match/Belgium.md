## The Warrior's Burden

Iran arrive at this match carrying the weight of a controversial squad selection. Coach Ghalenoei's decision to omit Sardar Azmoun — the "Iranian Messi" — has divided a nation. But Mehdi Taremi, with 59 goals in 103 caps, is no consolation prize. He is Iran's heartbeat, their hope, and their ultimate weapon against Belgium's fading golden generation.

## Key Player Matchups

### Mehdi Taremi vs Arthur Theate
Taremi's lethal finishing against the Eintracht Frankfurt centre-back. The Olympiacos striker's movement in the box is world-class. Theate must track him for 90 minutes — one lapse and Iran scores.

### Alireza Jahanbakhsh vs Maxim De Cuyper
The Iran captain's crossing ability against Brighton's De Cuyper. Jahanbakhsh's 98 caps give him supreme positional intelligence. His set-piece delivery is Iran's primary creative weapon.

### Kevin De Bruyne vs Saeid Ezatolahi
De Bruyne's genius against Iran's midfield destroyer. Ezatolahi's 82 caps make him Iran's most experienced midfielder, but stopping De Bruyne requires a tactical masterpiece, not just individual effort.

### Saman Ghoddos vs Amadou Onana
Ghoddos's creativity against Onana's physical dominance. The Al Ittihad Kalba playmaker versus Aston Villa's powerhouse. Technical quality against raw strength.

## Storyline: Ghost of Azmoun

The Tehran newspapers were brutal. "Ghalenoei's Folly," they screamed. Dropping Sardar Azmoun — the forward who had scored in two World Cups — was seen as an act of self-sabotage. But Ghalenoei saw something others didn't: Azmoun's form had declined, his commitment questioned. The coach chose youth over nostalgia.

Now, against Belgium, that decision faces its ultimate test. Taremi is brilliant, but he's 33 and playing in the Greek league. Without Azmoun's pace and power alongside him, Iran's attack lacks its traditional dynamism. The young forwards — Ghaedi, Hosseinzadeh, Taheri — must step up or Ghalenoei's gamble will be remembered as the moment Iran's World Cup died.

## Tactical Preview

**Iran (4-1-4-1):** Beiranvand; Rezaeian, Khalilzadeh, Kanaanizadegan, Mohammadi; Ezatolahi; Jahanbakhsh, Ghoddos, Torabi, Ghaedi; Taremi

**Belgium (4-2-3-1):** Courtois; Meunier, Theate, Debast, De Cuyper; Onana, Tielemans; Doku, De Bruyne, Trossard; Lukaku

Iran's 4-1-4-1 becomes a 4-5-1 without the ball. Ezatolahi sits deep, protecting the back four while the wide midfielders track Belgium's fullbacks. The plan: frustrate Belgium for 60 minutes, then introduce fresh forwards for the final 30. It worked against Wales in 2022. Can it work against Belgium's superior quality?

## X-Factor

**Iran's set-piece threat.** With Jahanbakhsh's delivery and Taremi's finishing, set pieces are Iran's most reliable path to goal against superior opposition. Belgium's zonal marking at corners and free kicks has been vulnerable historically. One dead-ball opportunity could be all Iran needs.
