## Team History

Iran, known as Team Melli, is one of the most successful national teams in Asian football history. They are three-time Asian Cup champions (1968, 1972, 1976) and have been a dominant force in West Asian football for decades. Despite their continental success, the World Cup has remained a frustrating stage -- Iran has never advanced past the group phase in six attempts.

Their World Cup debut came in 1978 in Argentina, where they earned a memorable 1-1 draw against Scotland. The 1998 World Cup in France produced Iran's most iconic moment: a 2-1 victory over the United States in a politically charged group-stage match, with goals from Hamid Estili and Mehdi Mahdavikia.

In recent tournaments, Iran has come agonizingly close to advancing. At the 2018 World Cup in Russia, they defeated Morocco 1-0 and nearly knocked out Portugal in the final group game, losing 1-0 in a match where a late penalty could have changed everything. At Qatar 2022, Iran beat Wales 2-0 but fell to England and the USA, finishing third in the group.

## Playing Style

Under coach Amir Ghalenoei, Iran typically deploys a 4-1-3-2 or 4-2-3-1 formation with a strong defensive foundation. The tactical approach emphasizes:

- **Defensive solidity:** A well-organized back line anchored by Shojae Khalilzadeh and Hossein Kanaanizadegan
- **Physical midfield presence:** Saeid Ezatolahi (1.90m) provides a commanding screen in front of the defense as the primary No. 6
- **Counter-attacking threat:** Quick transitions through wingers like Mehdi Ghaedi and Mohammad Mohebi
- **Set-piece danger:** With tall, physical players and Mehdi Taremi's finishing ability

The team blends European-based experience (Taremi at Olympiacos, Ghoddos in UAE after Brentford) with a strong domestic league core from the Persian Gulf Pro League.

## Expectations for 2026

Iran's 2026 World Cup campaign represents perhaps their best chance yet to reach the knockout rounds. Drawn in Group G with Belgium, Egypt, and New Zealand:

- **Belgium** is the group favorite but is in transition with an aging golden generation
- **Egypt** is dangerous but beatable, especially with Mohamed Salah's international future uncertain
- **New Zealand** is widely considered the weakest team in the group

A win against New Zealand and a result against Egypt could be enough for Iran to make history. The expanded 48-team format (top two in each group plus best third-placed teams advance) also works in their favor.

This is Iran's 7th World Cup appearance and their 4th in a row. The hunger to finally break the group-stage curse is immense, and with Mehdi Taremi leading the line at the peak of his powers, optimism is high among Iranian fans.
