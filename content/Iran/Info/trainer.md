### Playing Career

Ghalenoei began his football journey as a youth player in Tehran and has been involved in Iranian football for nearly half a century. He played as a midfielder in the Iranian domestic league, though his playing career was modest compared to what would follow in management.

### Tactical Approach

Ghalenoei is known for a flexible tactical system built on defensive discipline:

- **4-1-3-2 Primary Setup:** A dedicated defensive midfielder (No. 6) screens the back four, with Saeid Ezatolahi being the undisputed choice when fit
- **Defensive Solidity First:** Iran under Ghalenoei concedes few goals, with a well-drilled back line
- **Controlled Transitions:** Rather than all-out attack, Ghalenoei prefers measured counter-attacks, using the pace of wingers to transition quickly
- **Set-Piece Emphasis:** With physically imposing players, Iran is dangerous from corners and free kicks
- **Domestic Core Reliance:** Ghalenoei trusts players from the Persian Gulf Pro League who understand his system

His philosophy has been described as pragmatic and results-oriented, prioritizing not losing over winning pretty -- an approach that has drawn both praise for its effectiveness and criticism from fans who want more attacking football.

### Achievements

- **Most successful coach in Iran Pro League history** by wins and titles
- **Multiple Iran Pro League championships** with different clubs (Esteghlal, Sepahan, Tractor)
- **Qualified Iran for 2026 World Cup** -- their 4th consecutive appearance
- **Led Iran to their 7th overall World Cup** -- the nation's best-ever qualifying run
- **Iran Pro League record:** Most wins by any manager in league history

---

### Reputation and Controversies

Ghalenoei's career has not been without controversy. In Iranian football media, he has been called both "The General" (for his tactical discipline and command) and "Boss of the Mafia" (by critics who question his influence over player selection and refereeing decisions). His omission of Sardar Azmoun from the 2026 World Cup squad was a particularly divisive decision.

Despite the critics, his record speaks for itself. He is the most decorated domestic coach in Iranian football history and has earned the trust of the Iran Football Federation to lead Team Melli on the world stage.

### Coaching Philosophy Quote

> "In football, you must first make sure you do not lose, and then find a way to win." -- A principle that defines Ghalenoei's approach to managing Iran.
