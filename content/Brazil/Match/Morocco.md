# Brazil vs Morocco — Group C, Matchday 1

**June 12, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

| Matches | Brazil Wins | Draws | Morocco Wins |
|---------|-------------|-------|--------------|
| 3 | 2 | 0 | 1 |

Brazil leads historically, but Morocco won their most recent meeting — a **2-1 friendly victory in 2023** that shocked the football world. The only World Cup meeting was in 1998 group stage, where Brazil won 3-0.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Vinicius Junior vs Achraf Hakimi
Real Madrid's Ballon d'Or contender against PSG's right-back, arguably the best in the world. This is the marquee individual battle of the entire group stage. Vini's trickery and acceleration against Hakimi's pace and intelligence. These two train against each other at Madrid-PSG level — but the World Cup is different.

### Neymar vs Sofyan Amrabat
The boy from Santos, back home after a storied European career, against the midfield engine who ran himself into the ground during Morocco's 2022 semifinal run. At 34, this is Neymar's final World Cup. Amrabat will try to make it a nightmare.

### Raphinha vs Nayef Aguerd
Barcelona's winger against Real Sociedad's centre-back. Raphinha's dribbling and crossing against Aguerd's aerial dominance. The battle on Brazil's right flank will be fierce.

---

## Storyline: Atlas Lions vs the Seleção

Morocco's 2022 World Cup semifinal run changed African football forever. Now Walid Regragui's men believe they can beat anyone — including five-time champions Brazil. Carlo Ancelotti, the most decorated club manager in history, makes his World Cup debut at 66 with theSeleção. SoFi Stadium, 70,000 fans, Hollywood's backyard — this is the glamour fixture of the group stage. Brazil's attacking brilliance against Morocco's defensive fortress. Something extraordinary will happen.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Brazil (4-3-3)** — Ancelotti's version: controlled chaos. Vinicius left, Raphinha right, Neymar floating. Bruno Guimaraes anchors midfield with Premier League intensity. Alisson provides safety from the back.

**Morocco (4-2-3-1)** — Regragui's tried-and-true system. Amrabat screens the defense, Ziyech creates, Hakimi attacks from right-back, En-Nesyri finishes. Disciplined, athletic, and devastating on the counter.

---

## X-Factor: Hakim Ziyech's Left Foot

"The Wizard" has scored goals from 30+ yards that defy physics. Against a Brazil side that will push players forward, Ziyech will have space. One swing of that magical left foot could change everything.
