# Brazil vs Scotland — Group C, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Brazil and Scotland have a rich World Cup history. They've met in **three World Cups**: 1974 (0-0 draw), 1982 (Brazil 4-1), and 1998 (Brazil 2-1). Brazil is unbeaten in all three meetings (2 wins, 1 draw). The 1982 match featured Zico, Socrates, and Falcao at their peak — widely considered the greatest Brazil team never to win the World Cup.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Vinicius Junior vs Andy Robertson
Real Madrid's electrifying winger against Liverpool's Scottish captain. These two know each other intimately from El Clásico vs Premier League crossover. Robertson's tenacity against Vini's trickery — it's a heavyweight bout in miniature.

### Neymar vs Scott McTominay
Santos' magician against Napoli's midfield powerhouse. McTominay has 15 international goals from midfield — he's not just a destroyer but a genuine threat. Neymar will float and probe; McTominay will hunt and press.

### Raphinha vs Kieran Tierney
Barcelona's right winger against Celtic's versatile defender. Tierney's committed defending against Raphinha's unpredictable dribbling. The left flank will be a battleground.

---

## Storyline: The Ghost of 1982

Scotland has faced Brazil at three World Cups and never won. But the 0-0 draw in 1974 and the narrow 2-1 loss in 1998 show the Tartan Army can compete. Steve Clarke has built Scotland's most competitive team in a generation: Robertson (Liverpool), McTominay (Napoli), McGinn (Aston Villa), Tierney (Celtic). These are Champions League-level players. Brazil is Brazil — five titles, infinite talent, and Ancelotti on the bench. But Scotland has dreamed of this moment for 28 years. SoFi Stadium will be divided: samba gold on one side, tartan on the other.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Brazil (4-3-3)** — Depending on group position, Ancelotti may rotate. But even a second-string Brazil features superstars. The attacking talent is overwhelming.

**Scotland (5-4-1)** — Clarke will likely go with a back five to match Brazil's width. Robertson and Tierney as wing-backs, McTominay and McGinn central, Shankland up front. Disciplined, physical, brave.

---

## X-Factor: John McGinn's Big-Game Mentality

The Aston Villa midfielder has 20 international goals — an extraordinary return for a central midfielder. McGinn thrives when the stakes are highest. If Scotland can win set pieces, McGinn's knack for arriving late in the box could produce a moment of magic against the run of play.
