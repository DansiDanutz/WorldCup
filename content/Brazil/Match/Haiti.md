# Brazil vs Haiti — Group C, Matchday 2

**June 17, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

These nations have met in competitive CONCACAF/CONMEBOL crossover matches. Most notably, Brazil won **7-1** against Haiti in the 2016 Copa América Centenario — a match that remains Haiti's heaviest competitive defeat.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Vinicius Junior vs Jean-Kevin Duverne
Real Madrid's global superstar against FC Nantes' solid centre-back. Duverne has Ligue 1 experience but has never faced anything like Vinicius' dribbling repertoire. It's a mismatch that Haiti must find a way to mitigate through team defense.

### Neymar vs Ricardo Ade
Santos' returning king against FC Cincinnati's defender. Neymar at 34 still has the magic — the flicks, the tricks, the vision. Ade must stay disciplined and not dive into challenges Neymar will easily avoid.

### Bruno Guimaraes vs Bryan Alceus
Newcastle's midfield general against Le Mans' hard-working midfielder. The quality gap is enormous, but Alceus' work rate and physicality can at least make it uncomfortable.

---

## Storyline: The Beautiful Game Meets Caribbean Pride

Haiti is appearing in only their second World Cup — 52 years after their first. Les Grenadiers carry the hopes of 11 million Haitians and a global diaspora. Brazil represents football royalty — five World Cups, Pelé, Ronaldo, Ronaldinho, and now Vinicius Junior. The gulf in class is immense, but that's precisely what makes World Cup magic possible. Haiti plays with nothing to lose and everything to gain. Gabriel Calderón, the Argentine coach, will have his team organized and fighting. Brazil must not underestimate Caribbean heart.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Brazil (4-3-3)** — Ancelotti will rotate if they beat Morocco. But even a rotated Brazil side features world-class talent. Expect possession dominance and attacking waves.

**Haiti (4-4-2)** — Deep block, physical, counter-attacking. Nazon's pace on the break, Foster's hold-up play, and Placide's experience in goal. Survival mode for 90 minutes.

---

## X-Factor: Duckens Nazon's Pace

The KV Kortrijk forward has genuine speed and physicality. If Haiti can win the ball in midfield and release Nazon quickly, he could exploit the space behind Brazil's high line. One chance is all it takes.
