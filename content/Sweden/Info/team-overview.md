### Best Result

**Runners-up in 1958** on home soil, losing 5-2 to Brazil in the final at Rasunda Stadium in Stockholm. The team featuring Nils Liedholm, Gunnar Gren, and Kurt Hamrin captured the imagination of a nation.

### Third Place (1994)

Sweden finished third at USA 1994 with one of their greatest-ever squads. Thomas Brolin, Martin Dahlin, and Kennet Andersson formed a devastating attacking trio, while Tomas Ravelli's penalty shootout heroics against Romania in the quarter-finals remain iconic.

## Road to 2026 Qualification

Sweden's path to the 2026 World Cup was far from straightforward. They failed to win a single game in their qualifying group, which Switzerland ultimately topped. The campaign appeared to be slipping away before Graham Potter was appointed head coach in October 2025. Under his guidance, Sweden navigated the UEFA Nations League play-off route, with Viktor Gyokeres emerging as the hero by scoring four goals across two play-off matches, including a spectacular hat-trick against Ukraine. The dream of returning to the world stage was eventually secured through sheer determination and Potter's revitalizing influence.

---

## Playing Style

Under Graham Potter, Sweden employs a **flexible 3-4-2-1 / 4-2-3-1** system that emphasizes:

- **Possession with purpose:** Patient build-up play through the midfield, with Yasin Ayari and Lucas Bergvall providing technical quality in the center
- **Physical strikers:** Gyokeres and Isak offer two of Europe's most lethal forwards, combining pace, power, and clinical finishing
- **Defensive discipline:** Victor Lindelof marshals the back line with experience, supported by Isak Hien's aggression
- **Wide attacking play:** Anthony Elanga and Benjamin Nygren provide width and direct dribbling from the flanks
- **Set-piece threat:** Sweden has historically been strong at set pieces, and the current squad is no different with tall, athletic players throughout the lineup

---

## Expectations for 2026

Sweden enters the tournament with cautious optimism. Drawn in Group F alongside the Netherlands, Japan, and Tunisia, the group is challenging but navigable. The presence of two world-class strikers in Gyokeres and Isak gives Sweden a genuine goal threat in every match.

**Realistic target:** Reaching the Round of 16 would represent a solid tournament, though a deep run into the quarter-finals is not out of the question if Gyokeres continues his extraordinary scoring form. The team's fate may depend on whether Potter can get the best out of his talented but relatively inexperienced supporting cast.

**Key question:** Can Sweden's defense hold up against elite attacking teams? The Netherlands match on June 20 in Houston will be the defining test.
