# Sweden vs Japan — Group F, Matchday 2

**Date:** June 17, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Sweden and Japan met at the 2012 Olympics (Japan won 1-0). No World Cup meetings previously.

---

## The Storyline: Physical vs Technical

A classic collision of philosophies. Sweden's physical, direct approach against Japan's technical precision and rapid ball circulation. Sweden's height advantage at set pieces could be decisive — Japan traditionally struggles against physically imposing teams.

Both teams view this as winnable. The result could determine who joins the Netherlands in the knockout rounds.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Viktor Gyokeres vs Japan's Center-Backs
Gyokeres' physicality against Japan's agile defenders. Set pieces and crosses heavily favor Sweden.

### Anthony Elanga vs Yuto Nagatomo
Elanga's pace against Nagatomo's experience. Wide areas are where Sweden stretches Japan.

### Lucas Bergvall vs Takefusa Kubo
Bergvall's midfield intelligence against Kubo's dribbling. Young talents on the world stage.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Sweden (4-3-3):** Direct attacking, Gyokeres as focal point. Set pieces a major weapon with height advantage. Physical defending disrupts Japan's passing.

**Japan (4-2-3-1):** Quick passing and movement to bypass pressing. Kubo and Mitoma provide width. Make it about technique, not physicality.

---

## X-Factor

**Sweden's set-piece dominance.** With tall, physical players, Sweden can score from dead balls against a defense that struggles aerially.
