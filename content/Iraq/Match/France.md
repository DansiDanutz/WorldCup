# Iraq vs France — Group I, Matchday 1

**Date:** June 12, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Iraq and France have never met in a World Cup match. Their football histories are worlds apart.

---

## The Storyline: Lions of Mesopotamia vs Les Bleus

Iraq's return to the World Cup after 40 years is one of the tournament's most emotional stories. The Lions of Mesopotamia, who united a war-torn nation by winning the 2007 Asian Cup, now face France — a nation that has appeared in two of the last three World Cup finals.

The gulf in quality is enormous. France fields Mbappe, Tchouameni, Saliba — players who operate at the highest level every week. Iraq's squad features talented individuals but lacks that collective elite experience.

Yet football has always been Iraq's escape. In 2007, nobody gave them a chance either. The Lions of Mesopotamia have made a career of defying odds. Against France, they play with nothing to lose — and that makes them dangerous.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Zidane Iqbal vs Aurelien Tchouameni
Iqbal's technical quality against Tchouameni's midfield dominance. The former Manchester United youngster faces one of the world's best.

### Aymen Hussein vs William Saliba
Hussein's finishing against Saliba's defensive class. One chance might be all Iraq gets.

### Ali Al-Hamadi vs Kylian Mbappe (different flanks)
Al-Hamadi's direct running against France's wide defense. Iraq's winger must track back and help defend.

### Amir Al-Ammari vs Ousmane Dembele
Al-Ammari's work rate against Dembele's brilliance. A stark talent gap that Amir must bridge with effort.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Iraq (5-4-1):** Maximum defensive discipline. Deep block, narrow lines, deny France space in behind. Counter-attacks through Iqbal and Hussein. Set pieces are the best hope.

**France (4-3-3):** Dominant possession, Mbappe's pace, and Dembele's creativity. France will look to stretch Iraq's defensive block and create chances through width.

---

## X-Factor

**Iraq's emotional power.** A nation that has endured decades of conflict finds unity through football. That emotional weight creates an intensity that transcends talent gaps.
