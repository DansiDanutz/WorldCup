### Tactical Approach

Arnold is known for a structured, disciplined approach that emphasizes physical fitness and defensive organization:

- **4-2-3-1 Primary Setup:** Double pivot in midfield provides defensive cover while allowing the No. 10 freedom to create
- **High Pressing:** Arnold's teams press aggressively, particularly in wide areas, forcing turnovers high up the pitch
- **Set-Piece Organization:** Meticulous planning on both attacking and defending set pieces
- **Counter-Attacking Threat:** Quick transitions from defense to attack, using pacey wingers
- **Physical Conditioning:** Arnold places enormous emphasis on fitness; his teams are among the fittest in their competitions
- **Man-Management:** Known for building strong relationships with players and creating a tight-knit squad culture

---

### Dedication to Iraq

Arnold's commitment to the Iraq job has been remarkable. Despite his contract not requiring him to live in Iraq, he chose to move there six months before officially starting the role. He told reporters:

> "My contract stated I didn't have to live in Iraq, but after the first camp I felt I needed to be here. I needed to understand the culture, the players, and what this team means to the people."

This dedication earned him the trust of Iraqi fans and players alike. When Iraq qualified for the World Cup, Arnold was mobbed by Iraqi Australians in emotional scenes that went viral.
