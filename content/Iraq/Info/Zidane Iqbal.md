## Funny and Interesting Facts

### 1. Named After Zinedine Zidane

Iqbal was named after French legend **Zinedine Zidane**, one of the greatest footballers of all time. His father, a passionate football fan, chose the name in honor of the French-Algerian star. The irony is that Iqbal plays in a similar position to his namesake -- as a creative midfielder -- and shares some of Zidane's elegance on the ball. When asked about the pressure of his famous name, Iqbal laughed: "My dad set the bar pretty high. I am just trying to be the best Zidane Iqbal I can be."

### 2. The Manchester United Debut That Was Too Short

Iqbal made his Manchester United first-team debut in a Champions League dead rubber, coming on as a late substitute. He touched the ball only a few times, but those few touches went viral in Iraq -- fans watched the moment on repeat for days. However, his time at United was frustrating. He struggled to break into the first team and eventually moved to FC Utrecht for just 850,000 pounds. The transfer fee was so low that some United fans called it "the steal of the century." Iqbal has since said leaving United was the best decision of his career, as regular playing time at Utrecht prepared him for the World Cup.
