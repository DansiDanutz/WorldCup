## Funny and Interesting Facts

### 1. The First Iraqi in the Premier League

Ali Al-Hamadi made history by becoming the **first Iraqi player ever to appear in the English Premier League** when he featured for Ipswich Town. Born in Iraq, he moved to England as a child and came through the English youth system. When he made his Premier League debut, Iraqi fans -- particularly the large diaspora community in London -- packed pubs and living rooms to watch. He received thousands of messages from Iraqi fans, including some who told him they had never watched a Premier League match before but now supported Ipswich because of him.

### 2. Scored the Opener in the Qualifying Final

In the playoff final against Bolivia that sealed Iraq's World Cup qualification, it was the 23-year-old Al-Hamadi who opened the scoring with a composed finish. After the match, he revealed that he had been carrying a hamstring injury but refused to tell the medical staff because he did not want to miss the most important match of his life. He played through pain for 75 minutes before being substituted, and Aymen Hussein went on to score the winner. Al-Hamadi celebrated both goals as if he had scored them himself.
