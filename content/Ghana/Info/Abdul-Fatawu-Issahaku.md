## Skills and Strengths

- **Dribbling:** Issahaku is a mesmeric dribbler with the ball at his feet. His low center of gravity, quick feet, and close ball control allow him to glide past defenders in tight spaces.
- **Left Foot:** Possesses a magical left foot capable of devastating curling shots, precise through balls, and dangerous crosses. His left-footed strikes from distance are a trademark.
- **Vision and Creativity:** Sees passes that few others can, with the technical ability to execute them. He is Ghana's primary creative force in the final third.
- **Fearlessness:** Despite his young age, Issahaku plays with no fear. He takes on defenders, attempts ambitious passes, and shoots from distance — backing up his confidence with genuine talent.
- **Set Pieces:** A superb dead-ball specialist who can bend free kicks around walls and deliver pinpoint corners.

## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. Teenage AFCON Sensation
Issahaku burst onto the scene at the 2021 U-20 Africa Cup of Nations, where he was named the tournament's Best Player as a 17-year-old. He led Ghana's young Black Satellites to the title, dazzling scouts from across Europe with his performances. His performances were so impressive that Portuguese giants Sporting CP signed him shortly after — a remarkable move for a teenager from the Ghanaian lower divisions. He reportedly turned down offers from Bayern Munich and Liverpool to join Sporting, citing their track record of developing young wingers.

### 2. The Tamale Trickster
Growing up in Tamale, northern Ghana's largest city, Issahaku learned his football on dusty streets and red-earth pitches. He has recounted how he used to play barefoot until his early teens, and how his childhood friends would gather to watch him perform tricks and skills. His signature move — a no-look drag-back followed by an acceleration past the defender — earned him the nickname "The Tamale Trickster" in local football circles. He still performs the move in professional matches. Despite his rise to fame, he returns to Tamale every off-season to run football clinics for local children.
