## Playing Career

Queiroz had a modest playing career as a goalkeeper in Portuguese football, but his impact on the game would come from the dugout rather than the pitch. He retired early from playing to pursue coaching.

## Major Achievements

- **2x FIFA World Youth Championship:** 1989, 1991 (with Portugal's "Golden Generation" including Luis Figo, Rui Costa, and Joao Pinto)
- **5x World Cup qualifications:** South Africa (2002), Portugal (2010), Iran (2014, 2018, 2022)
- **Premier League titles (as assistant):** 3 with Manchester United (2006–07, 2007–08, 2008–09)
- **Champions League (as assistant):** 1 with Manchester United (2007–08)
- **AFCON Runners-up:** 2021 with Egypt
- **5th consecutive World Cup as coach:** 2026 with Ghana (2002, 2010, 2014, 2018, 2022 — this will be his 5th or 6th tournament)

## Tactical Approach

Queiroz is one of football's most defensive-minded and tactically disciplined coaches. His approach can be characterized as:

### Defensive Organization First
- Teams are built from the back, with a compact, well-drilled defensive block.
- Emphasizes positional discipline and minimizing space between the lines.
- Opponents are forced into low-percentage shots from distance.

### Pragmatic Over Romantic
- "Less improvisation, more compression; less romance, more results" sums up his Ghana philosophy.
- Willing to sacrifice attacking flair for defensive solidity.
- Focuses on set pieces and counter-attacks for goals rather than sustained possession.

### Man-Management
- Known for strict discipline and a no-nonsense approach.
- Has sometimes clashed with star players who prefer creative freedom.
- Demands total tactical commitment from every player.

### Tournament Specialist
- Queiroz has built a reputation as a tournament coach — his teams often overperform at World Cups despite having less individual talent than opponents.
- Iran's brave performances against Argentina and Portugal in 2014 and 2018 are testament to his ability to set up a team for a single match.

## Why Ghana Hired Queiroz

Ghana appointed Queiroz specifically for the 2026 World Cup, replacing Otto Addo. The GFA's rationale:

1. **World Cup experience:** No active coach has managed at more World Cups.
2. **Defensive improvement:** Ghana conceded too many goals in recent tournaments; Queiroz specializes in defensive organization.
3. **African connection:** Born in Mozambique, Queiroz has an understanding of African football culture.
4. **Short-term impact:** Hired on a tournament-only contract, Queiroz's task is to get Ghana out of a difficult group.

## What to Expect at World Cup 2026

- Ghana will be extremely difficult to break down.
- Matches against England and Croatia will likely see Ghana sit deep and counter.
- The Panama match will be Queiroz's best chance to secure a win and potentially advance.
- His experience navigating tournament pressure could be invaluable in a tight group.
