## Playing Style

Under Carlos Queiroz, Ghana has adopted a pragmatic and structured approach. The philosophy can be summarized as "less improvisation, more compression; less romance, more results." Queiroz's system prioritizes:

- **Defensive discipline:** A compact, organized back line with a focus on minimizing space and denying opponents clear chances.
- **Midfield control:** Thomas Partey anchors the midfield as the primary ball-winner and distributor, supported by energetic runners like Kwasi Sibo and youngster Caleb Yirenkyi.
- **Quick transitions:** The attacking threat comes from rapid counters, utilizing the pace of players like Antoine Semenyo, Ernest Nuamah, and Abdul Fatawu Issahaku on the flanks.
- **Set-piece threat:** With physical players like Derrick Luckassen and Jerome Opoku, Ghana poses a significant danger from corners and free kicks.

The likely formation is a 4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1, with Jordan Ayew as the focal point in attack.

---

## The 2010 Quarter-Final Heartbreak

Ghana's most famous — and most painful — World Cup moment came in the 2010 quarter-final against Uruguay in Johannesburg. With the score at 1-1 in the final seconds of extra time, Luis Suarez handled the ball on the goal line to prevent a certain winner. Asamoah Gyan missed the resulting penalty, and Uruguay won the ensuing shootout. The image of Suarez celebrating on the sideline while Ghana's players collapsed in despair remains one of the most iconic — and heartbreaking — moments in World Cup history.

## 2026 Expectations

Ghana faces a challenging group. England and Croatia are established World Cup contenders, while Panama are the underdogs. Expectations are measured but hopeful:

- **Minimum target:** A strong showing against Panama and compete against Croatia for second place in the group.
- **Optimistic scenario:** A place in the Round of 16 or beyond, which would be only the second time the Black Stars advance from the group stage.
- **Key question:** Can Queiroz's defensive organization compensate for the gap in individual quality between Ghana and the top European teams?

The squad blends experienced campaigners (Jordan Ayew, Thomas Partey, Baba Rahman) with exciting young talent (Antoine Semenyo, Ernest Nuamah, Caleb Yirenkyi), giving Ghana a balance of know-how and energy.
