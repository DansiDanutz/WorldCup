## Skills and Strengths

- **Incredible Stamina and Durability:** Williams holds the La Liga record for 251 consecutive appearances — a feat that speaks to his extraordinary fitness, professionalism, and consistency.
- **Pace:** One of the fastest players in La Liga, even in his early 30s. His acceleration over the first 10 meters is devastating for defenders.
- **Pressing:** A tireless presser who sets the intensity for Athletic Club's high-energy style. His work without the ball is as valuable as what he does with it.
- **Loyalty and Leadership:** A one-club man who has made over 500 appearances for Athletic Club. He captains the team and embodies the club's Basque identity philosophy.
- **Versatility:** Can play as a central striker, right winger, or second striker, giving Queiroz multiple tactical options.

## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. The Record That May Never Be Broken
Inaki Williams played 251 consecutive La Liga matches — a streak that lasted from April 2016 to January 2023 (nearly seven years without missing a single league game). The streak finally ended when he tested positive for COVID-19. During that incredible run, he played through minor injuries, fatigue, and personal challenges. When asked about it, he simply said: "If I can walk, I can play." The record is considered one of the most unbreakable in Spanish football, surpassing the previous record by over 70 matches.

### 2. Two Brothers, Two Countries
Perhaps the most unique story in world football: Inaki and his younger brother Nico Williams both play for Athletic Club in Bilbao, but represent different national teams. Inaki chose Ghana (his parents' homeland) while Nico chose Spain (his birth country). Both played at the 2022 World Cup for different nations. Their parents, Maria and Felix, traveled between Qatar's stadiums to watch both sons in different matches. Inaki has said: "I chose Ghana because of my parents. They sacrificed everything to come to Spain. Playing for Ghana is my way of honoring them."
