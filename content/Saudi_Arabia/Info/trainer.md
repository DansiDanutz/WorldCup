### Tactical Approach

Donis brings a European tactical framework adapted to Saudi football's strengths:

- **4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1:** Flexible system that can shift between defensive solidity and attacking width
- **High Pressing:** Encourages aggressive pressing in the opponent's half, using the energy and fitness of a young Saudi squad
- **Width and Crossing:** Utilizes Al-Dawsari on the left and overlapping full-backs to deliver crosses into the box
- **Ball Possession:** Prefers controlled build-up play from the back, a departure from previous more direct approaches
- **Defensive Discipline:** Has worked extensively on defensive shape, particularly against top teams
- **Knowledge of Saudi Players:** Having managed in the Saudi Pro League for nearly a decade, Donis knows the strengths and weaknesses of virtually every player in the squad

---

### Appointment Context

Donis was appointed in **April 2026**, just **59 days before the World Cup**, replacing **Herve Renard** who was sacked despite having qualified the team. The appointment raised eyebrows given the short timeframe, but Donis's deep familiarity with Saudi football -- he had managed four different Saudi clubs -- made him the logical choice.

> "I have been working in Saudi football for over a decade. I know these players, I know this culture, and I know what Saudi football can achieve on the world stage." -- Georgios Donis
