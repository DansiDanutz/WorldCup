## Team History

Saudi Arabia is one of Asia's traditional football powerhouses. The Green Falcons have won the AFC Asian Cup three times (1984, 1988, 1996) and have been a consistent presence at the World Cup since their stunning debut in 1994.

Their 1994 World Cup campaign in the United States remains the golden moment of Saudi football. After beating Morocco and Belgium (with a legendary individual goal by Saeed Al-Owairan) and losing to the Netherlands, Saudi Arabia advanced to the Round of 16 -- their best-ever World Cup result. Al-Owairan's solo goal, where he dribbled from his own half past five Belgian defenders, is considered one of the greatest World Cup goals ever scored.

The following World Cups (1998, 2002, 2006) were disappointing, including an 8-0 loss to Germany in 2002. After missing the 2010 and 2014 tournaments, Saudi Arabia returned in 2018 and 2022.

The 2022 World Cup in Qatar produced one of the greatest upsets in World Cup history: **Saudi Arabia beat Argentina 2-1** in the group stage, with Salem Al-Dawsari scoring a stunning winning goal. Despite losing to Poland and Mexico and failing to advance, the victory over Argentina -- who went on to win the tournament -- remains a source of immense national pride.

## Playing Style

Under coach Georgios Donis, who brings deep knowledge of Saudi football from years managing clubs in the Kingdom, Saudi Arabia plays with a distinct identity:

- **Domestic-Based Squad:** 25 of 26 players play in the Saudi Pro League, creating exceptional team chemistry
- **Quick Transitions:** The team excels at rapid counter-attacks, using the pace of wingers like Salem Al-Dawsari and Ayman Yahya
- **Technical Skill:** Saudi players are known for their close control and dribbling ability, a hallmark of Middle Eastern football
- **Physical Improvement:** Under Donis, the team has worked on matching European teams physically
- **Defensive Organization:** Hassan Tambakti and Abdulelah Al Amri form a reliable center-back partnership
- **Goalkeeping Strength:** Nawaf Al Aqidi's stoppage-time saves in qualifying secured their World Cup spot

## Expectations for 2026

Group H presents a massive challenge for Saudi Arabia:

- **Spain** is one of the tournament favorites, loaded with world-class talent
- **Uruguay** brings South American grit and quality, with Darwin Nunez leading the line
- **Cape Verde** is the weakest team in the group, representing Saudi Arabia's best chance for points

The expanded 48-team format gives Saudi Arabia a slim chance of advancing as one of the best third-placed teams. A win against Cape Verde and a competitive showing against Uruguay could be enough.

Additionally, Saudi Arabia is the designated **host of the 2034 FIFA World Cup**, adding extra motivation to perform well in 2026 as a building block for the home tournament.
