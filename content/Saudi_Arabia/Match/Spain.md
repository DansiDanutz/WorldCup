## The 2022 Echo

Saudi Arabia stunned Argentina 2-1 in their 2022 World Cup opener — a result that shook the planet. Now they face another Iberian giant in Spain. Can lightning strike twice? The Green Falcons believe it can. Salem Al-Dawsari, the man who scored that famous goal against Argentina, is still here. The dream lives on.

## Key Player Matchups

### Salem Al-Dawsari vs Dani Carvajal
The defining contest. Al-Dawsari's magical left foot against Carvajal's experience. If Al-Dawsari can replicate his Argentina goal — cutting inside and unleashing a thunderbolt — anything is possible.

### Firas Al Brikan vs Robin Le Normand
Al-Ahli's striker against Atletico Madrid's centre-back. Al Brikan has 56 caps and 14 goals for Saudi Arabia. Le Normand's La Liga quality gives him the edge.

### Saud Abdulhamid vs Nico Williams
The Lens right-back — Saudi Arabia's only European-based player — against Williams' devastating pace. Abdulhamid's French league experience is crucial here.

### Mohamed Kanno vs Rodri
Al-Hilal's defensive midfielder against the world's best in that position. Kanno's 52 caps against Rodri's complete mastery. The midfield battle is one-sided on paper.

## Storyline: The New Coach's Gamble

Georgios Donis walked into a firestorm. Herve Renard, the beloved French coach who led Saudi Arabia to the Argentina miracle, was sacked in April 2026 after a string of poor results. Donis had barely two months to prepare this team for the World Cup.

The squad is almost entirely domestic-based — 25 of 26 players from the Saudi Pro League. That's either their greatest strength (unmatched chemistry) or their fatal weakness (limited exposure to elite opposition). Against Spain's pass-masters, every gap in quality will be exposed.

But Saudi Arabia have something no other team can replicate: the memory of 2022. Every player in that dressing room watched Al-Dawsari's goal against Argentina. Every player knows that impossible things happen at the World Cup.
