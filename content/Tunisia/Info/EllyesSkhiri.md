## Interesting Facts

### 1. The Bundesliga "Running Man"

During his four seasons at FC Koln (2019-2023), Skhiri became known as the **"Laufmaschine" (running machine)** of the Bundesliga. He consistently ranked among the top midfielders in the league for distance covered per match, often exceeding 12 kilometers per game. In one memorable statistic, he was recorded covering 13.8 kilometers in a single match -- more than any other player on the pitch. This extraordinary stamina, combined with his tactical intelligence, made him the heartbeat of every team he played for. His move to Eintracht Frankfurt in 2023 was seen as a significant coup for the club, acquiring a proven Bundesliga performer on a free transfer.

### 2. French-Born, Tunisian at Heart

Like many players in the Tunisia squad, Skhiri was born and raised in France (Lunel, near Montpellier) and came through the French youth system. He played for Montpellier's academy and first team before moving to Germany. He qualifies for Tunisia through his parents' heritage and has spoken about the pride he feels representing the country. Despite having lived in Germany for years and France before that, he has maintained strong connections to Tunisia, visiting regularly and involving himself in charitable work in the country. His decision to represent Tunisia over France -- made when he was already established in Ligue 1 -- was a deliberate choice rooted in family and identity rather than footballing convenience.
