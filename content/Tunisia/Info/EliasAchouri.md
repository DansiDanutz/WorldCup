## Interesting Facts

### 1. The Scandinavian Journeyman

Achouri's career path has been anything but conventional. After starting at Esperance de Tunis, one of Africa's biggest clubs, he embarked on a nomadic journey through Algeria, France, Finland, and Denmark. His stint in the Finnish second division with Horna in 2020-21 could have been a career dead-end, but instead it was a turning point -- he scored regularly and earned a move to Viborg FF in Denmark. From there, his performances earned a transfer to FC Copenhagen, Denmark's biggest club, where he has become an important squad player. The journey from Tunis to Copenhagen via Algeria, France, and Finland is a testament to his perseverance and determination to build a European career on his own terms.

### 2. Tunisia's Primary Goal Threat

In a Tunisia squad that has historically struggled to score goals at World Cups (just 15 goals in 18 World Cup matches across all tournaments), Elias Achouri carries a significant burden as one of the team's primary attacking threats. His goal-scoring record at Copenhagen -- double figures across all competitions -- gives Lamouchi a rare commodity in the squad: a forward who scores regularly at club level. With Tunisia drawn in a difficult group against the Netherlands, Japan, and Sweden, Achouri's ability to take the limited chances that come Tunisia's way could be the difference between another group-stage exit and a historic first knockout-round appearance.
