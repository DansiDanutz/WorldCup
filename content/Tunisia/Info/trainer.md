### Key Achievements as Manager

- **Ivory Coast (2012-2014):** Took charge of a golden generation including Didier Drogba, Yaya Toure, and Gervinho. Qualified for the 2014 World Cup but had a painful group-stage exit despite a talented squad
- **Nottingham Forest (2019-2020):** Took Forest to the brink of the Championship play-offs, a notable achievement for a club that had been struggling in the second tier
- **Stade Rennais (2022-2023):** Managed in Ligue 1, gaining valuable experience in one of Europe's top five leagues

## Tactical Approach

Lamouchi is a **pragmatic, defensively-minded coach** who prioritizes organization and discipline:

- **Formation:** Strongly prefers a **4-2-3-1** with a double pivot providing defensive security
- **Defensive solidity:** Builds teams from the back, emphasizing clean sheets and compact defensive shape
- **Double pivot:** Uses two defensive midfielders (typically Skhiri and Khedira for Tunisia) to protect the back four
- **Counter-attacking:** When possession is won, looks for quick transitions through the creative players (Hannibal, Ben Slimane)
- **Set pieces:** Practices set-piece routines extensively for both attacking and defending
- **Player trust:** Has shown willingness to give young players opportunities, as evidenced by the exclusion of veterans and inclusion of 19-year-old Khalil Ayari

### Philosophy

Lamouchi has spoken about his **painful 2014 World Cup experience** with the Ivory Coast, where despite a talented squad, they were eliminated in the group stage. That failure taught him the importance of tactical discipline over individual talent. With Tunisia, he has deliberately built a structured, organized team rather than relying on individual brilliance.

## The Tunisia Connection

Lamouchi was born in Lyon, France, to Tunisian parents, giving him a natural connection to the country he now manages. He has spoken about the emotional significance of leading Tunisia:

> "My parents' homeland. I grew up with Tunisian culture, Tunisian food, Tunisian values. To lead the Eagles of Carthage is more than a job -- it is a personal mission."

This connection has helped him quickly earn the trust of both players and fans, despite being appointed just months before the World Cup.
