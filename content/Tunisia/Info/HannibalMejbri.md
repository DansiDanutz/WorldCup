## Interesting Facts

### 1. The Manchester United Academy Product Who Chose Tunisia

Hannibal Mejbri was born in Ivry-sur-Seine, a suburb of Paris, and grew up in the French football system. He joined the prestigious ASEC Mimosas youth setup in Paris before being scouted by Manchester United at age 16. At United, he was widely regarded as one of the most talented players in the academy, winning the club's Young Player of the Year award. Despite being eligible for France and having represented France at youth levels, he chose to play for **Tunisia**, the country of his parents' origin. The decision was deeply personal -- he has spoken about watching Tunisia play in the World Cup as a child and dreaming of wearing the red shirt. His commitment to Tunisia has made him a hero in the country, viewed as proof that the diaspora can strengthen the national team.

### 2. The Premier League Debut Against Liverpool

Hannibal made his Premier League debut for Manchester United in a high-stakes match against Liverpool at Anfield -- one of the most intimidating venues in world football. Coming on as a substitute, the teenager showed no fear, immediately getting involved in the play and even engaging in a heated exchange with Liverpool players. That fearless introduction became a viral moment and endeared him to Manchester United fans, who appreciated his combative spirit. While his Manchester United career ultimately did not yield the regular first-team football he craved (just 7 appearances), that Anfield debut demonstrated the mentality that makes him so valuable to Tunisia -- he is a player who rises to the occasion, not one who shrinks from it.
