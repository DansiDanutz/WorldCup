# Norway vs Iraq — Group I, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Norway and Iraq have never met in an official FIFA match.

---

## The Storyline: The Lions of Mesopotamia's Last Stand

Iraq's World Cup story is one of resilience against extraordinary odds. The 2007 Asian Cup champions, who united a war-torn nation through football, face Norway in what could be their final group match.

Norway enters as heavy favorites. Haaland and Odegaard represent a level of individual quality that Iraq's squad, drawn primarily from Middle Eastern and smaller European leagues, cannot match. But Iraq has built their identity on defying expectations.

For Norway, this is about professional execution. A comfortable win builds momentum for the knockout rounds. For Iraq, it's about pride — showing the world they belong on this stage, regardless of the result.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Erling Haaland vs Iraq's Center-Backs
Haaland's movement against Iraq's deep defensive block. The goal threat is constant.

### Martin Odegaard vs Rebin Sulaka
Odegaard's creativity against Sulaka's defensive work. Iraq's midfielder must disrupt the supply line.

### Zidane Iqbal vs Sander Berge
Iqbal's technical quality against Berge's power. The young Iraqi midfielder has something to prove.

### Aymen Hussein vs Norway's Defense
Hussein's finishing against Norway's organization. One chance might be all Iraq gets.

## Tactical Preview

**Norway (4-2-3-1):** Dominant possession, direct to Haaland. Odegaard creates, the wide players stretch Iraq's defense. Comfortable in attack.

**Iraq (5-4-1):** Maximum defensive discipline. Deep block, physical defending. Counter through Iqbal's creativity and Hussein's finishing.

## X-Factor

**Iraq's fighting spirit.** The 2007 Asian Cup proved that Iraq can produce extraordinary performances when motivated by national pride. That spirit cannot be underestimated.
