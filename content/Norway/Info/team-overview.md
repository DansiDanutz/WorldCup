# Norway - FIFA World Cup 2026

## Group I: France, Senegal, Iraq, Norway

---

## Team History

### The Land of the Midnight Sun

Norway has a rich footballing tradition but has struggled to qualify for major tournaments in recent decades. Despite producing world-class individual talents, the team has not reached a World Cup since 1998.

### World Cup Record

| Year | Host | Result | Key Moment |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| 1938 | France | Round of 16 | First World Cup appearance |
| 1994 | USA | Group Stage | Earned respect with competitive performances |
| 1998 | France | Round of 16 | Reached knockout stage; last major tournament appearance |

### The Haaland & Odegaard Era

Norway's current generation features two genuine superstars: Erling Haaland and Martin Odegaard. Their presence has transformed Norway from also-rans into a team that genuinely believes it can compete with Europe's best.

---

## Playing Style

Norway plays a **direct, counter-attacking 4-4-2 or 4-2-3-1** emphasizing:
- **Haaland as focal point**: Every attack flows through getting the ball to Erling Haaland
- **Odegaard's creativity**: The Arsenal captain dictates play from midfield
- **Physicality**: Tall, strong team that dominates aerial duels
- **Directness**: Vertical passing that bypasses midfield when possible
- **Set pieces**: Haaland and towering defenders are a constant threat

---

## World Cup 2026 Prospects

Norway qualified for their first World Cup in 28 years, largely thanks to Haaland's 18 goals in qualifying. Drawn in a tough Group I alongside France, Senegal, and Iraq, progression will be difficult but not impossible.

**Key Question**: Can Haaland and Odegaard carry Norway to the knockout stage?
