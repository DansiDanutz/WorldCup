## Key Player Matchups

### Son Heung-min vs Mothobi Mvala
Son's world-class movement against Mvala's physical defending. The Sundowns enforcer must stay disciplined — one mistimed challenge on the edge of the box and Son will punish from the free kick.

### Kim Min-jae vs Lyle Foster
Bayern Munich's "Monster" vs Burnley's Premier League battler. Foster has the physicality to compete, but Kim has faced Haaland and Mbappe. This is a mismatch on paper, but World Cups create surprises.

### Lee Kang-in vs Teboho Mokoena
Two midfield conductors with different styles. Lee is the artist; Mokoena is the organizer. If Lee finds space between the lines, South Africa is in trouble.

---

## Storyline: Asia vs Africa

This is a fixture that represents the global growth of football. South Korea, with players at Real Madrid, Bayern Munich, PSG, and the Premier League, represents Asia's finest. South Africa, built around Mamelodi Sundowns' CAF Champions League core, represents African resilience. Son Heung-min alone earns more than South Africa's entire squad — but money doesn't score goals. In a match both teams likely must win to advance, the pressure is immense.

---

## Tactical Preview

**South Korea (4-2-3-1)** — Hong Myung-bo will attack from the start. Son drifts left, Lee Kang-in orchestrates, and Hwang Hee-chan provides the work rate. They'll dominate possession.

**South Africa (4-4-2)** — Deep block, compact midfield, and hit on the counter through Foster's hold-up play and Makgopa's pace. Tau provides the magic dust.

---

## X-Factor: Percy Tau's Magic

The "Lion of Judah" is capable of producing something from nothing. His time at Al-Ahly has sharpened his big-game mentality. If South Africa can keep it close for 70 minutes, Tau has the quality to snatch a result.
