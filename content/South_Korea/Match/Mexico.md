# South Korea vs Mexico — Group A, Matchday 2

**June 16, 2026** | Estadio Akron, Guadalajara | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

| Matches | South Korea Wins | Draws | Mexico Wins |
|---------|------------------|-------|-------------|
| 6 | 1 | 1 | 4 |

South Korea has **never beaten Mexico at a World Cup** (0-2 in World Cup meetings). The 2018 loss still stings — Mexico won 2-1 despite Son Heung-min's late goal. The overall record heavily favors Mexico.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Son Heung-min vs Mexico's Right Side
"Sonaldo" has 52+ international goals and will drift left to cut inside. Mexico's right-back must decide: show him onto his weaker right foot or risk the trademark curling shot. There is no good answer.

### Kim Min-jae vs Santiago Gimenez
"The Monster" (Bayern Munich) vs "Bebelo" (AC Milan). Two European giants' players going head-to-head. Kim's raw strength against Gimenez's intelligent movement. This is a showcase matchup between two of Asia's and CONCACAF's finest.

### Lee Kang-in vs Edson Alvarez
PSG's playmaker against the midfield sentinel. Lee's close control and vision can unpick any lock, but Alvarez's 90+ caps of experience give him the know-how to suffocate creative players.

---

## Storyline: Son's Redemption Arc

In 2018, Son Heung-min scored a late consolation against Mexico that meant nothing — South Korea was already eliminated from contention. Eight years later, at 33 and likely in his final World Cup, Son has a chance to rewrite history. He's South Korea's all-time top scorer, a Premier League Golden Boot winner, and a global icon. But he's never beaten Mexico. Hong Myung-bo, the 2002 semifinal hero, coaches from the sidelines now. Can he orchestrate the upset his star player desperately needs?

---

## Tactical Preview

**South Korea (4-2-3-1)** — Protection for Kim Min-jae, width from Hwang Hee-chan and Son, creativity from Lee Kang-in. The Taegeuk Warriors will concede possession but strike with devastating counters.

**Mexico (4-3-3)** — Aguirre pushes Lozano wide and feeds Gimenez through the middle. Obed Vargas, the Atletico Madrid wonderkid, adds energy from midfield.

---

## X-Factor: Hwang Hee-chan's "Bull" Mentality

The Wolves forward earned his nickname for a reason — he never stops running, never stops fighting. If Son is South Korea's elegance, Hwang is their heartbeat. His pressing could force Mexico errors that South Korea's quality attackers can punish.
