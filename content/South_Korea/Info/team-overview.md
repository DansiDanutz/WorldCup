# South Korea - FIFA World Cup 2026

## Group E: France, Japan, New Zealand, South Korea

---

## Team History

### The Taegeuk Warriors

South Korea is Asia's most consistent World Cup performer, with nine consecutive appearances and a historic semi-final run in 2002 as co-hosts.

### World Cup Record

| Year | Host | Result | Key Moment |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| 2002 | South Korea/Japan | 4th Place | Beat Italy and Spain on a historic run to the semi-finals |
| 2010 | South Africa | Round of 16 | Lost to Uruguay |
| 2022 | Qatar | Round of 16 | Beat Portugal in a dramatic final group game |

### The Son Heung-min Era

South Korea's current golden generation is led by Son Heung-min, one of the greatest Asian footballers in history. His presence elevates a squad that increasingly features players at top European clubs.

---

## Playing Style

South Korea plays a **counter-attacking 4-2-3-1** emphasizing:
- **Pace on the break**: Lightning-fast transitions from defense to attack
- **Son Heung-min**: Everything flows through their talisman
- **Discipline**: Well-organized defensive structure
- **Set pieces**: Effective from corners and indirect free-kicks
- **Mental resilience**: Never gives up; famous for late goals

---

## World Cup 2026 Prospects

Drawn in a difficult group with France and Japan, South Korea faces an uphill battle to reach the knockout stage. But with Son in top form, anything is possible.

**Key Question**: Can Son Heung-min carry South Korea past the group stage?
