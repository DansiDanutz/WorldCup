## Skills and Strengths

### Physical Dominance
Rios is a powerhouse in midfield. His combination of size (1.83m), strength, and athleticism makes him almost impossible to bypass in central areas. He wins the vast majority of physical duels and provides an impenetrable shield in front of the defense.

### Ball Recovery
His primary role is winning the ball back, and he excels at it. Rios has an innate sense of where the ball is going and positions himself to intercept or tackle. His tackling technique is clean and effective, rarely conceding fouls in dangerous areas.

### Ball Progression
Unlike many defensive midfielders who simply win the ball and give it to more creative teammates, Rios can carry the ball forward effectively. His dribbling through midfield -- using his strength to ride challenges -- is an underrated aspect of his game.

### Positional Discipline
Rios understands his role perfectly. He does not wander out of position, does not try to play passes beyond his range, and maintains his position even when teammates push forward. This discipline is the foundation of Colombia's midfield balance.

### Aerial Ability
His height and timing make him effective in aerial duels, both defensively and on attacking set pieces. Winning headers in midfield is crucial for maintaining possession and preventing counter-attacks.

## Defining Moments

### Palmeiras Breakthrough (2023)
Rios emerged as a key player for Palmeiras during the 2023 season, helping them win the Brazilian Serie A. His performances in midfield caught the attention of European scouts and established him as one of the best defensive midfielders in South America.

### Benfica Move (2024)
Benfica secured Rios's signature in 2024, beating several Premier League clubs to his services. He adapted quickly to European football and became a regular starter in the Primeira Liga.

### Colombia's Midfield Anchor
Under Nestor Lorenzo, Rios has become the lindestra -- the fulcrum -- of Colombia's midfield. His partnership with Jefferson Lerma provides the foundation upon which the creative talents of James Rodriguez and Luis Diaz can flourish.

## Interesting Facts

### 1. The Futsal Origins
Rios began his football career not on grass but on the futsal court. He played competitive futsal until age 15, which developed his exceptional close control and quick reflexes in tight spaces. He credits futsal with developing his ability to receive the ball under pressure and turn away from opponents in midfield. He has said that if he had not transitioned to outdoor football, he would have pursued a career in futsal and represented Colombia internationally in the indoor version of the sport.

### 2. The Late Developer
Unlike many Colombian internationals who are identified as teenagers, Rios was not called up to any national youth teams and was considered an ordinary prospect until his mid-20s. He was released by Flamengo after limited opportunities and spent time in Mexican and lower-division Brazilian football before Palmeiras took a chance on him. He has spoken about how being overlooked motivated him to prove his doubters wrong. His rise from journeyman to Benfica starter and Colombia regular in the span of two years is one of the most remarkable late-bloomer stories in recent South American football.
