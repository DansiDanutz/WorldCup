| Field | Details |

---

## Playing Career

- Played as a defender for **Boca Juniors** and **San Lorenzo** in Argentina
- Brief stint at **Swindon Town** in England
- Career cut short by injury, transitioned to coaching early

---

## Coaching Career

### Key Roles
- **Cerro Porteno** (Paraguay) — First major head coaching role
- **Independiente del Valle** (Ecuador) — Built reputation for developing young talent
- **Boca Juniors** — Short stint at his former club

### Colombia National Team (2024-present)
- Appointed in 2024 after Colombia missed the 2022 World Cup
- Led Colombia to the **2024 Copa America final** (lost 1-0 to Argentina in extra time)
- Secured **automatic qualification** for the 2026 World Cup with a strong campaign
- Achieved a **12-game unbeaten streak** in 2024

---

## Tactical Approach

**Formation:** 4-3-3

- **Defensive organization**: Compact mid-block, quick transitions from defense to attack
- **Wing play**: Utilizes pacy wingers (Diaz, Sinisterra) for devastating counter-attacks
- **Set-piece threat**: Colombia is dangerous from corners and free kicks
- **Flexible pressing**: Medium pressing rather than high-intensity, conserving energy for attacking bursts
- **Squad rotation**: Trusts a deep bench, giving minutes to young players like Jhon Duran

### Philosophy
"We want to be protagonists. Colombia has a tradition of beautiful football, but we also need to be smart defensively."

---

## Personality

Lorenzo is known for his calm demeanor on the touchline. He prefers to let his tactics speak and gives players freedom to express themselves in the final third. He has earned the respect of the Colombian dressing room through his tactical acumen and man-management skills.
