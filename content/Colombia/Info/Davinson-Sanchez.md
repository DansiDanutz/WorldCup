## Skills and Strengths

### Aerial Dominance
At 1.87m with excellent timing, Sanchez is dominant in the air. He wins the majority of defensive headers and is a threat on attacking set pieces. His 4 goals for Colombia include several from headers.

### Defensive Reading
Sanchez reads the game well, anticipating danger and positioning himself to intercept passes and block shots. His experience across multiple leagues has sharpened his defensive intelligence.

### Recovery Pace
Despite not being the fastest over long distances, Sanchez has good acceleration over short distances, allowing him to recover when caught out of position. His recovery tackles are a trademark.

### Physical Strength
Sanchez is a powerful defender who relishes physical battles with strikers. He uses his strength to shield the ball, win shoulder-to-shoulder challenges, and clear his lines under pressure.

### Big-Game Experience
With appearances in a Champions League final, Copa Libertadores, Premier League, and Copa America, Sanchez has extensive experience in football's biggest matches. This composure under pressure is invaluable for Colombia.

## Defining Moments

### Copa Libertadores Title (2016)
Sanchez was part of the Atletico Nacional side that won the 2016 Copa Libertadores, Colombia's greatest club football achievement in decades. The tournament showcased his potential to a global audience.

### Ajax Breakthrough (2016-17)
His single season at Ajax was spectacular. Six goals from center-back in the Eredivisie demonstrated his attacking threat, and his defensive performances earned him a move to Tottenham for a reported 42 million euros.

### Champions League Final (2019)
Sanchez started the 2019 Champions League final against Liverpool. Despite Tottenham's defeat, the experience of competing on football's biggest club stage was invaluable.

### Galatasaray Renaissance
After falling out of favor at Tottenham, Sanchez revitalized his career at Galatasaray, becoming a defensive leader and helping the club win consecutive Turkish league titles. This resurgence earned him a central role in Colombia's World Cup campaign.

## Interesting Facts

### 1. The Record Transfer
When Tottenham signed Sanchez from Ajax in 2017 for 42 million euros, he became the most expensive Colombian defender in history and one of the most expensive defenders in Premier League history at the time. The fee was remarkable for a player who had only one season of European football. Sanchez later revealed that he almost did not join Ajax -- he was on the verge of signing for Barcelona B when Ajax made a late offer that changed the trajectory of his career.

### 2. The Chocolate Business
Sanchez runs a successful chocolate brand in Colombia called "Davinson Chocolate," producing premium chocolate bars from cacao grown in his home region of Caloto, Cauca. The business was inspired by his grandfather, who was a cacao farmer. Sanchez has said that when his football career ends, he plans to dedicate himself fully to the business and to supporting cacao farmers in rural Colombia. The brand's packaging features his signature and the Colombian flag, and a portion of profits goes to youth football programs in Cauca.
