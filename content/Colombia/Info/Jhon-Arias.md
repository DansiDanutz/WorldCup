## Skills and Strengths

### Dribbling and Creativity
Arias is a natural dribbler with quick feet and excellent close control. He operates primarily on the right flank, cutting inside onto his stronger right foot to create shooting and passing angles. His ability to beat defenders one-on-one makes him Colombia's primary wide threat on the right.

### Crossing and Final Ball
Despite being right-footed and playing on the right wing, Arias has developed the ability to deliver dangerous crosses with both feet. His crossing accuracy and decision-making in the final third have improved significantly under Abel Ferreira at Palmeiras.

### Versatility
Arias can operate as a traditional winger, an inverted winger, or as a central attacking midfielder. This flexibility gives Nestor Lorenzo multiple tactical options.

### Work Rate
Like many modern wingers, Arias contributes defensively. He tracks back to help his fullback and presses from the front when Colombia loses possession. His work rate complements the creative freedom given to James Rodriguez.

### Big-Game Experience
Having played for several of Brazil's biggest clubs -- Flamengo, Atletico Mineiro, Fluminense, and Palmeiras -- Arias is accustomed to high-pressure environments. His experience in the Copa Libertadores is invaluable.

## Defining Moments

### Palmeiras Success (2023-Present)
Arias has been a key player in Palmeiras's recent success under Abel Ferreira. His consistency and productivity from the right flank have made him one of the most effective wingers in Brazilian football.

### Colombia's Right Flank Solution
Under Lorenzo, Arias has solved Colombia's long-standing problem at right wing. His combination of creativity, work rate, and goalscoring threat has made him an automatic selection.

### Copa Libertadores Experience
Arias's experience in South America's premier club competition -- including deep runs with both Fluminense and Palmeiras -- has prepared him for the intensity of World Cup football.

## Interesting Facts

### 1. The Brazilian Journeyman
Arias has played for more Brazilian clubs than Colombian clubs, making him one of the rare Colombian internationals who built his entire career in Brazil. He speaks fluent Portuguese and has adapted so well to Brazilian life that Palmeiras fans consider him one of their own. He has stated in interviews that he feels "half Colombian, half Brazilian" and that playing in Brazil's intense football culture prepared him for international football in ways that staying in Colombia never could have.

### 2. The Gaming Connection
Arias is an avid gamer who streams FIFA and other video games on social media during his free time. He has participated in charity gaming tournaments alongside other professional footballers and once revealed that he learned English partly by playing online games with English-speaking teammates. His Palmeiras teammates have joked that he is better at FIFA than he is at real football, a claim Arias cheerfully disputes. He has said that gaming helps him relax before big matches and maintain the calm mindset that characterizes his playing style.
