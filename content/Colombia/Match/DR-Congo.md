# Colombia vs DR Congo — Group K, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

First-ever meeting between Colombia and DR Congo in official competition.

---

## The Storyline: South American Flair Meets African Power

Colombia's attacking brilliance faces DR Congo's physical resilience in a cross-continental clash that could determine who advances from Group K. On paper, Colombia are clear favorites — but the World Cup doesn't care about paper.

DR Congo's European-based squad has quality that Colombia must respect. Aaron Wan-Bissaka can neutralize any winger. Yoane Wissa has the pace to punish any defensive lapse. Cedric Bakambu has been scoring goals in Europe for years.

The contrast in styles makes this compelling. Colombia will want to play — quick passes, movement, creativity. DR Congo will want to fight — physical challenges, set pieces, counter-attacks. Whose identity prevails?

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Luis Diaz vs Aaron Wan-Bissaka
Diaz's electrifying dribbling against Wan-Bissaka's world-class one-on-one defending. This is a Premier League matchup transplanted to the World Cup stage.

### James Rodriguez vs Chancel Mbemba
James' vision against Mbemba's defensive organization. If Mbemba can keep James quiet, Colombia loses its creative hub.

### Jhon Duran vs DR Congo's Center-Backs
Duran's physicality against DR Congo's defensive power. This will be a battle of strength and will.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Colombia (4-3-3):** Express their attacking talent. James pulls the strings, Diaz and Arias stretch the defense, and Duran provides the finishing touch. Colombia will dominate possession and create chances.

**DR Congo (4-3-3):** Physical, organized, and dangerous on the counter. Wissa and Silas have the pace to exploit any space behind Colombia's high line. Set pieces are a major weapon.

---

## X-Factor

**Aaron Wan-Bissaka's tackling.** The Manchester United defender is arguably the best one-on-one tackler in world football. If he can neutralize Luis Diaz, Colombia's entire attacking structure is compromised.
