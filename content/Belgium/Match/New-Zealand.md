# Belgium vs New Zealand — Group G, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## David and the Departing Goliath

The final group match could be a dead rubber or a do-or-die encounter. Belgium, with their tournament progression potentially secured, face a New Zealand side fighting for survival — or glory. The All Whites have never won a World Cup match in normal time (their only positive result was a 1-1 draw with Italy in 2010). Chris Wood, 34 years old and still scoring in the Premier League, leads one final charge.

---

## Head-to-Head History

No recorded competitive or friendly meetings between Belgium and New Zealand at senior international level.

This is a **first-ever meeting** between the two nations.

## Key Player Matchups

### Romelu Lukaku vs Michael Boxall
Lukaku's overwhelming physicality against the 36-year-old Minnesota United centre-back. Boxall has 50 caps but has never faced a striker of Lukaku's caliber. The 40-goal gap in international experience tells the story.

### Kevin De Bruyne vs Marko Stamenic
The 22-year-old Nottingham Forest midfielder against De Bruyne's genius. Stamenic is New Zealand's brightest young talent, but this is a mismatch of generational proportions. Can the young Kiwi learn on the job?

### Chris Wood vs Arthur Theate
The Nottingham Forest striker against the Eintracht Frankfurt centre-back — a Premier League reunion. Wood's aerial ability and hold-up play are well known to Theate from their domestic encounters.

### Jeremy Doku vs Liberato Cacace
Doku's dazzling dribbling against the Empoli left-back. Cacace has Serie A experience but Doku's pace could overwhelm him.

---

## Storyline: The Last Stand of Chris Wood

Chris Wood has scored 80 goals for New Zealand. He has played in the Premier League for over a decade. He has carried the All Whites through Oceania qualifying more times than he can count. At 34, this is almost certainly his final World Cup.

New Zealand's World Cup history is a book of heartbreak. In 1982, they lost all three matches. In 2010, they drew all three — including a famous 1-1 with Italy — but still went out. They didn't qualify in 2014 or 2018. In 2022, they lost to Costa Rica in the intercontinental playoff. Every campaign ends in tears.

Against Belgium, Wood faces the ultimate test: can one man's determination overcome a squad worth a hundred times more? Wood's Premier League experience means he won't be intimidated. But can the same be said for his teammates?

New Zealand will sit deep in a compact 4-4-2, using Wood's aerial presence for set pieces and Barbarouses' experience on the counter. Belgium's rotated squad could lack cohesion — exactly what Bazeley's side needs.

## X-Factor

**Rotation vs Desperation.** If Belgium have already qualified, Garcia's squad rotation could give New Zealand an opening they'd never get against a full-strength Belgian side. The All Whites' desperation — fighting for their first-ever World Cup win — against Belgium's complacency could produce the tournament's biggest upset.
