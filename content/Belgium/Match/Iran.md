## The Giant and the Ghost

Belgium, the fallen giants of 2018, face Iran — a team that has haunted major tournaments with moments of brilliance but never escaped the group stage. Iran's 2-6 loss to England in 2022 was a humiliation, but their win over Wales showed what they're capable of. Against Belgium's aging golden generation, Iran smells an opportunity.

## Key Player Matchups

### Kevin De Bruyne vs Saeid Ezatolahi
The Shabab Al-Ahli defensive midfielder has 82 caps and will be tasked with shadowing De Bruyne. Ezatolahi is Iran's midfield anchor — physically imposing and tactically disciplined, but has never faced a player of De Bruyne's caliber in competitive international football.

### Romelu Lukaku vs Hossein Kanaanizadegan
Lukaku's 89+ international goals against Iran's experienced centre-back (53 caps). The Persepolis defender is rugged and battle-tested in Asian qualifying, but Lukaku at Napoli is a different beast entirely.

### Mehdi Taremi vs Arthur Theate
Taremi has 59 goals in 103 caps for Iran — a phenomenal strike rate. The Olympiacos forward is Iran's talisman. His movement and finishing against Theate, the Eintracht Frankfurt centre-back, could expose Belgium's defensive vulnerability.

### Jeremy Doku vs Ramin Rezaeian
Doku's blistering pace against the 34-year-old Sepahan full-back. Rezaeian has 61 caps of experience but Doku's acceleration could be the difference-maker.

## Storyline: The Azmoun Ghost

Iran's squad announcement sent shockwaves through Tehran — Sardar Azmoun, the "Iranian Messi" with 82+ caps, was left out. Coach Amir Ghalenoei made the most controversial decision in Iranian football history. Azmoun's absence means the attacking burden falls entirely on Mehdi Taremi, now 33 and playing in Greece.

The question haunting every Iranian fan: without Azmoun, do they have enough firepower against Belgium? Or does Taremi, the ever-reliable warrior with 59 international goals, carry the weight alone? Ghalenoei gambled on youth — Ghaedi, Hosseinzadeh, Taheri — over experience. This match reveals whether that gamble was genius or madness.

## Tactical Preview

**Belgium (4-2-3-1):** Courtois; Meunier, Theate, Debast, De Cuyper; Onana, Tielemans; Doku, De Bruyne, Trossard; Lukaku

**Iran (4-1-4-1):** Beiranvand; Rezaeian, Khalilzadeh, Kanaanizadegan, Mohammadi; Ezatolahi; Jahanbakhsh, Ghoddos, Torabi, Ghaedi; Taremi

Iran will defend deep and compact, looking to frustrate Belgium's creative forces. The plan is simple: survive the first hour, then unleash Taremi and Ghaedi on tired legs. Belgium must break down a low block without becoming vulnerable to the counter — exactly the scenario that cost them against Morocco in 2022.

## X-Factor

**Mehdi Ghaedi.** The Al-Nasr winger is Iran's secret weapon. With Azmoun gone, Ghaedi's direct dribbling and unpredictable movement become Iran's most dangerous attacking outlet. If Belgium focus entirely on Taremi, Ghaedi will punish them from the shadows.
