## The Shadow Duel: De Bruyne vs Salah

Two of the Premier League's greatest ever imports meet on the world stage — not as club rivals, but as captains of nations. Kevin De Bruyne, the assist king now orchestrating Napoli's attack, faces Mohamed Salah, Liverpool's pharaoh who has rewritten every scoring record at Anfield. Both men are 34 and 33 respectively. This could be their final World Cup. The stakes are eternal.

## Key Player Matchups

### Kevin De Bruyne vs Mohamed Salah
The marquee duel. De Bruyne's 117 caps and 36 goals for Belgium make him the most creative force in international football. Salah's 67 goals in 116 caps for Egypt make him his nation's greatest ever player. Both are coming off club seasons where they proved age is just a number. De Bruyne at Napoli has found new rhythm; Salah at Liverpool remains relentless.

### Romelu Lukaku vs Mohamed Abdelmonem
Lukaku's 89+ international goals make him Belgium's all-time top scorer. The Napoli striker's physicality against Nice centre-back Abdelmonem will be a battle of raw power versus tactical positioning.

### Jeremy Doku vs Ahmed Fatouh
Doku's electrifying dribbling against Zamalek's Fatouh down Belgium's left flank could stretch Egypt's defensive shape. Doku's Manchester City pace versus Egyptian league nous.

### Omar Marmoush vs Zeno Debast
Marmoush's move to Manchester City has sharpened his already lethal finishing. The young Belgian centre-back Debast, now at Sporting CP, faces his toughest test yet.

## Storyline: The Pharaoh's Last Dance

Mohamed Salah has carried Egypt on his shoulders for a decade. He dragged them to the 2018 World Cup with a penalty against Congo — then injured his shoulder in the Champions League final weeks before and was never the same in Russia. Egypt didn't qualify in 2022. Now 33, this is surely his final chance at World Cup glory.

But standing in his way on Matchday 1 is Kevin De Bruyne — a man whose own World Cup story is filled with heartbreak. The third-place finish in 2018 was a peak; the group-stage exit in 2022 was a catastrophe. Both men know this tournament defines their legacy.

Egypt coach Hossam Hassan, himself a World Cup veteran from 1990, has built a team around Salah that finally doesn't rely entirely on him. Omar Marmoush's emergence as a genuine second threat gives Egypt a dimension they've never had at a World Cup.

## Tactical Preview

**Belgium (4-2-3-1):** Courtois; Meunier, Theate, Debast, De Cuyper; Onana, Tielemans; Doku, De Bruyne, Trossard; Lukaku

**Egypt (4-3-3):** El Shenawy; Mohamed Hany, Abdelmonem, Rabia, Fatouh; Hamdy Fathy, Marwan Attia, Emam Ashour; Marmoush, Salah, Trezeguet

Belgium will dominate possession through De Bruyne and Tielemans, looking to feed Lukaku in the box and Doku on the counter. Egypt will sit deep, compact, and unleash Salah and Marmoush on rapid transitions. The battle of midfield control — Onana's physicality versus Hamdy Fathy's experience — will determine the tempo.

## X-Factor

**Hamza Abdelkarim.** The 18-year-old Barcelona B forward is Egypt's wildcard. If Belgium's defense settles into a comfortable rhythm, Abdelkarim's energy and fearlessness off the bench could shatter it. He has nothing to lose and everything to prove. One moment of teenage audacity could change Group G.
