### Best Result: 3rd Place (2018)

Belgium's greatest World Cup achievement came in Russia in 2018, where Roberto Martinez led a golden generation to a 3rd-place finish. Key moments included:

- **Quarter-final comeback vs Japan**: Trailed 2-0 before winning 3-2 with a last-gasp winner
- **Quarter-final vs Brazil**: Won 2-1 to eliminate the tournament favorites
- **Semi-final vs France**: Lost 1-0 to the eventual champions in a tight, tactical match
- **3rd-place playoff**: Beat England 2-0 with goals from Thomas Meunier and Eden Hazard

### 1986: 4th Place

Belgium's previous best was a 4th-place finish in Mexico 1986, where a team led by Enzo Scifo and Jan Ceulemans reached the semi-finals before losing to Argentina (featuring Diego Maradona).

## Playing Style

Under Rudi Garcia, Belgium aim to combine their traditional attacking flair with improved defensive organization:

- **Possession-based attacking football**: Garcia's hallmark is fluid, attacking play with an emphasis on offensive movement
- **4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1 formation**: Flexible attacking shapes that maximize the talent of De Bruyne, Doku, and Trossard
- **Wing play**: Jeremy Doku's dribbling and Leandro Trossard's intelligence provide width and penetration
- **Lukaku as target man**: Romelu Lukaku's physical presence and finishing remain central to the attack
- **Midfield control**: Youri Tielemans and Amadou Onana provide the engine room balance
- **Courtois's leadership**: Thibaut Courtois anchors the defense with world-class goalkeeping

## Expectations for 2026

Belgium enter the 2026 World Cup in a period of transition:

- **The "Golden Generation" is aging**: De Bruyne (34), Courtois (34), and Lukaku (33) are likely in their final World Cup
- **New coach effect**: Rudi Garcia was appointed in January 2025, replacing Domenico Tedesco after a disappointing Euro 2024
- **Youri Tielemans as captain**: The Aston Villa midfielder has been given the armband, signaling a new era of leadership
- **Young talent emerging**: Players like Dodi Lukebakio, Charles De Ketelaere, and Matias Fernandez-Pardo represent the next wave
- **Topped qualifying group unbeaten**: Strong qualifying campaign suggests the team is finding its identity under Garcia
- **Key question**: Can Belgium's remaining golden-generation stars combine with emerging talent to make a deep tournament run?
