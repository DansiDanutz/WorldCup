- **Dribbling**: One of the best dribblers in world football -- has the highest successful dribble rate in the Premier League
- **Acceleration**: Explosive burst of speed that leaves defenders standing, especially in one-on-one situations
- **Flair and creativity**: Not afraid to try the unexpected -- stepovers, flicks, and audacious skill moves
- **Two-footed**: Comfortable cutting inside from either wing onto either foot
- **Low center of gravity**: At 1.73 m, he uses his size to his advantage, changing direction faster than taller defenders
- **Progressive carrying**: Among the best in the Premier League at carrying the ball forward and breaking lines
- **Young and improving**: At 24, he is still developing and has his peak years ahead
- **Fearlessness**: Takes on any defender without hesitation, regardless of reputation

## Interesting Facts

1. **He chose Belgium over Ghana despite strong Ghanaian ties**: Doku was born in Antwerp to Ghanaian parents and was eligible to represent both Belgium and Ghana internationally. The Ghanaian Football Association actively courted him, with multiple attempts to convince him to play for the Black Stars. However, Doku chose Belgium, having come through the Belgian youth system from a young age. His father has spoken about the difficulty of the decision, but Jeremy felt a deep connection to Belgian football after growing up in the Anderlecht academy.

2. **He was being compared to Eden Hazard before he even turned 18**: Belgian media began comparing Doku to Eden Hazard when he was just 16 and breaking into the Anderlecht first team. The comparisons were based not just on their similar playing style (both small, explosive dribblers) but also their shared Belgian-Ghanaian heritage and their path from Anderlecht to bigger clubs. Doku has acknowledged the comparison but says he wants to be "Jeremy Doku, not the next Eden Hazard."
