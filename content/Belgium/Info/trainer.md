## Managerial Career

### Saint-Etienne (Reserves) & Dijon (2001-2007)
- Began coaching in the French lower divisions
- Led Dijon from the Championnat National (third tier) to Ligue 2

### Le Mans (2007-2008)
- Brief but successful spell in Ligue 1, establishing his reputation for attractive football

### Lille (2008-2013)
- **Ligue 1 champion (2010-11)** -- Lille's first league title in 57 years
- **Coupe de France winner (2011)** -- completed a historic double
- Developed players including Eden Hazard, Yohan Cabaye, and Gervinho
- His Lille team played some of the most exciting football in Europe
- Named Ligue 1 Manager of the Year (2011)

### Roma (2013-2016)
- Led Roma to a **club-record 10 consecutive wins** to start the 2013-14 Serie A season
- Achieached Champions League qualification
- Reached the Coppa Italia semi-finals
- Known for attacking, entertaining football at the Stadio Olimpico
- Sacked in January 2016 after a decline in results

### Marseille (2016-2019)
- Reached the **Europa League final (2018)**, losing to Atletico Madrid
- Led Marseille back into European competition
- Implemented an attacking philosophy despite budget constraints
- Resigned in October 2019

### Lyon (2019-2021)
- Reached the **Champions League semi-finals (2020)** -- Lyon's best European run in a decade
- Defeated Manchester City and Juventus in the knockout rounds
- Left the club in 2021

### Napoli (2021-2022)
- Brief spell in Serie A, continuing his Italian football experience
- Helped develop Napoli's squad structure

### Al Nassr (2023-2024)
- Managed in the Saudi Pro League
- Worked with Cristiano Ronaldo at the club
- Gained experience of managing high-profile players in a different football culture

### Belgium National Team (2025-Present)
- Appointed January 24, 2025, succeeding Domenico Tedesco
- Contract through the 2026 World Cup
- Tasked with getting the best out of Belgium's remaining golden-generation stars while integrating younger players
- Topped the qualifying group unbeaten
- Named Youri Tielemans as captain, signaling a new leadership structure

## Tactical Approach

Garcia is known for his attacking, possession-based philosophy:

- **Fluid offensive structures**: Players interchange positions freely in the attacking third
- **4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1**: Flexible formations that prioritize attacking width
- **High defensive line**: Pushing the team up the pitch to compress space
- **Technical midfield play**: Emphasizes ball retention and progressive passing
- **Wing play**: Encourages wingers to take on defenders one-on-one
- **Pressing**: Organized pressing when possession is lost
- **Set-piece preparation**: Known for detailed set-piece routines

### Strengths
- Excellent at building attacking patterns and creating scoring opportunities
- Has managed at the highest club level in multiple countries (France, Italy, Saudi Arabia)
- Experience developing young talent (Hazard at Lille, various players at Lyon)
- Adaptable to different football cultures and playing styles

### Criticisms
- Has not won a major trophy since the 2011 Ligue 1/Coupe de France double with Lille
- Champions League semi-final with Lyon was a bubble-tournament achievement (COVID era)
- Some critics argue his attacking philosophy can leave defenses exposed

## Personal

- Born in Nemours, France
- Of Spanish descent through his parents
- Known for his passionate touchline presence and emotional reactions
- Fluent in French, Italian, and English
- His father, Jose Garcia, was a Spanish immigrant who worked as a miner in northern France
- A former teammate described him as "a coach who lives and breathes attacking football"
- His experience managing Cristiano Ronaldo at Al Nassr gives him understanding of how to work with global superstars -- relevant for managing Belgium's big personalities
