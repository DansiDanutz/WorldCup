# New Zealand vs Belgium — Group G, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## The All Whites' Impossible Dream

New Zealand's final group match pits them against Belgium's galaxy of stars. Chris Wood, the Nottingham Forest striker who has carried New Zealand for over a decade, faces the ultimate test against Thibaut Courtois and Belgium's defense. The All Whites have never beaten a European team at a World Cup. History says this is impossible. Football says otherwise.

---

## Head-to-Head History

No recorded competitive or friendly meetings between New Zealand and Belgium at senior international level.

This is a **first-ever meeting** between the two nations.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Chris Wood vs Thibaut Courtois
Wood's aerial dominance against Real Madrid's goalkeeper. Two Premier League stalwarts meeting on the World Cup stage. Wood's heading is his superpower; Courtois's shot-stopping is among the best in history.

### Marko Stamenic vs Kevin De Bruyne
The 22-year-old Nottingham Forest midfielder against the greatest playmaker of his generation. Stamenic is New Zealand's brightest talent, but this is like facing a hurricane.

### Liberato Cacace vs Jeremy Doku
The Empoli left-back against Manchester City's electric winger. Cacace's Serie A experience is valuable but Doku's pace could be unplayable.

### Kosta Barbarouses vs Zeno Debast
The 35-year-old Wellington Phoenix veteran against the 21-year-old Sporting CP centre-back. Experience against youth — Barbarouses must use every ounce of cunning.

## Storyline: 2010 Ghosts

The ghost of 2010 haunts New Zealand. In South Africa, the All Whites drew all three group matches — including a famous 1-1 with eventual champions Italy — but still went home. They became the only team to go unbeaten at a World Cup and still be eliminated. The injustice of it still burns.

Coach Danny Bazeley, a former New Zealand international himself, has spoken about that tournament with reverence and pain. His players know the history. Against Belgium, they have nothing to lose — and that makes them dangerous.

Belgium, conversely, could be complacent. If their progression is already secured, Rudi Garcia may rotate heavily. A second-string Belgium side against a desperate New Zealand team is the recipe for a World Cup shock.

## Tactical Preview

**New Zealand (4-4-2):** Sail; Elliot, Bindon, Boxall, Cacace; Howieson, Stamenic, Lewis, Singh; Wood, Barbarouses

**Belgium (4-2-3-1, rotated):** Lammens; Castagne, Mechele, De Winter, Ngoy; Witsel, Raskin; Lukebakio, De Ketelaere, Moreira; Charles De Ketelaere

New Zealand's 4-4-2 is designed for resilience. Two banks of four protect the center, with Wood and Barbarouses offering counter-attacking threat. The plan: defend for 70 minutes, then throw everything forward in the final 20.

---

## X-Factor

**Belgium's complacency.** If Belgium have already qualified and field a rotated side, the gap in quality narrows dramatically. Witsel (37) and the substitutes lack the cohesion of the first team. New Zealand's unity and desperation could exploit the cracks.
