# New Zealand vs Iran — Group G, Matchday 3

**Date:** June 22, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## The Battle of the First-Timers

Both New Zealand and Iran are searching for their first-ever World Cup knockout stage appearance. Iran have been to six tournaments without escaping the group. New Zealand have been to two. This match could be the one that finally breaks one nation's curse — or extends both.

---

## Head-to-Head History

No recorded senior international meetings between New Zealand and Iran.

This is a **first-ever meeting** between the two nations.

## Key Player Matchups

### Chris Wood vs Hossein Kanaanizadegan
Wood's physical Premier League presence against the Persepolis centre-back. Kanaanizadegan has 53 caps and is experienced in Asian competition, but Wood's power and aerial threat are unique challenges.

### Mehdi Taremi vs Tyler Bindon
Taremi's 59 international goals against the 21-year-old Reading defender. Bindon has 15 caps and is raw but talented. Taremi's movement could expose the youngster's inexperience.

### Alireza Jahanbakhsh vs Sam Sutton
Iran's captain and cross specialist against the 23-year-old Wellington Phoenix defender. Jahanbakhsh's 98 caps against Sutton's 8 — a monumental experience gap.

### Marko Stamenic vs Saeid Ezatolahi
The Nottingham Forest youngster against Iran's midfield anchor. Stamenic's energy against Ezatolahi's 82 caps of positional discipline.

## Storyline: Curse Breakers

Every four years, Iran arrive at the World Cup with hope. And every four years, they leave with heartbreak. Six tournaments, zero knockout stage appearances. For a nation that breathes football, the pain is generational. Fathers pass the heartbreak to sons.

New Zealand's story is different but equally painful. They've only been to two World Cups, but in 2010 they achieved something remarkable — going unbeaten through three matches — only to be eliminated on goal difference. The injustice of that exit still fuels the current squad.

This match, the final group game for both teams, offers a chance to rewrite history. For one nation, it could be the day the curse finally breaks. For the other, the agony continues for another four years.

## X-Factor

**Mehdi Ghaedi's dribbling.** If Iran struggle to break down New Zealand's low block through conventional passing, Ghaedi's direct dribbling from the Al-Nasr winger's position could be the spark that ignites Iran's attack. His unpredictability is exactly what's needed against an organized defense.
