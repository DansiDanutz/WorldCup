## Playing Career
Bazeley had a modest playing career in New Zealand's domestic leagues before transitioning to coaching. His playing experience gave him a deep understanding of the challenges faced by players from smaller footballing nations.

## Tactical Philosophy

Bazeley favors a flexible tactical system that adapts to opposition strength:

- **Against stronger teams:** Organized 4-5-1 low block with rapid counter-attacks through the wings
- **Against equal/weaker teams:** Proactive 4-3-3 with higher pressing and territorial dominance
- **Set pieces:** Both offensive and defensive set pieces are heavily drilled — a core strength
- **Player development:** Integrates young European-based players with experienced A-League campaigners

### Key Principles
1. **Defensive Solidity:** The foundation is always a well-organized defensive structure
2. **Physical Intensity:** Pressing triggers and aggressive challenges in midfield
3. **Direct Transition:** Quick vertical passing when possession is won
4. **Aerial Dominance:** Exploiting height advantages at both ends of the pitch
