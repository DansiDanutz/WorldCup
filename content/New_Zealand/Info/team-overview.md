# New Zealand National Football Team — All Whites

## Overview

The New Zealand national football team, known as the **All Whites**, represents New Zealand in international football and is governed by New Zealand Football (NZF). Named in contrast to the famous All Blacks rugby team, the All Whites have been a competitive force in the Oceania Football Confederation (OFC) and have earned respect on the world stage for their resilience and organization.

## World Cup Record

| Year | Result | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1982 | Group Stage | First ever World Cup qualification |
| 2010 | Group Stage | Unbeaten (3 draws), narrowly missed knockout stage |
| 2026 | Qualified | Third World Cup appearance |

- **Appearances:** 3 (1982, 2010, 2026)
- **Best Result:** Group Stage (2010 — unbeaten in group with 3 draws)
- **OFC Nations Cup Titles:** 6 (1973, 1998, 2002, 2008, 2016, 2024)

## Playing Style

Under head coach Danny Bazeley, New Zealand plays a pragmatic 4-3-3 or 3-4-3 system built on defensive discipline, physical presence, and efficient set-piece play. The All Whites are difficult to break down and pose a constant aerial threat from corners and free kicks.

### Key Tactical Elements
- **Low Block:** Comfortable absorbing pressure and hitting on the counter
- **Set Pieces:** Major offensive weapon with tall, athletic players
- **Direct Play:** Quick transitions from defense to attack using pace on the wings
- **Physicality:** Strong in challenges and aerial duels across the pitch
- **Team Chemistry:** A tight-knit squad with many players sharing European club experience

## Notable Achievements
- **2010 FIFA World Cup:** Finished group stage unbeaten (drew with Italy, Paraguay, Slovakia) — the only team eliminated without losing a match
- **Oceania dominance:** Won 6 OFC Nations Cup titles
- **Qualification record:** Dominant in OFC qualifying, regularly reaching intercontinental playoffs

## Famous Players (Historical)
- **Wynton Rufer** — All-time leading scorer, 1982 World Cup veteran
- **Ryan Nelsen** — Captain and defensive rock at 2010 World Cup
- **Simon Elliott** — Midfield maestro, record cap holder for many years
- **Winston Reid** — Scored the dramatic last-minute equalizer against Slovakia in 2010
- **Shane Smeltz** — Prolific striker, scored against Italy in 2010

## Current Generation
The 2026 squad features a blend of European-based professionals and A-League experience, led by Chris Wood's goalscoring prowess and a solid defensive unit. The team's strength lies in its organization, set-piece threat, and the improving quality of players in top European leagues.
