## Team History

### Rising Football Nation

The United States has historically been a footballing underdog, but the sport has grown exponentially since the 1990s. Hosting the 1994 World Cup sparked a generation of interest, and the USMNT has become a consistent World Cup qualifier.

### World Cup Record

| Year | Host | Result | Key Moment |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| 1930 | Uruguay | 3rd Place | Best ever finish; Bert Patenaude scored the first World Cup hat-trick |
| 2002 | South Korea/Japan | Quarter-finals | Lost 1-0 to Germany in a tight quarter-final |
| 2014 | Brazil | Round of 16 | Tim Howard's 16 saves against Belgium became legendary |
| 2022 | Qatar | Round of 16 | Lost to Netherlands; Pulisic generation emerged |

### The Golden Generation

The current USMNT features the most talented squad in American history, with players at elite European clubs like AC Milan, Juventus, and Monaco. Christian Pulisic leads a group that believes they can compete with anyone.

---

## Playing Style

The USA typically plays an **energetic 4-3-3** emphasizing:
- **Athleticism and pace**: Among the fittest teams at the tournament
- **High pressing**: Aggressive off-the-ball work rate
- **Direct running**: Players who attack space with the ball
- **Set-piece threat**: Physically imposing players who dominate aerial situations

---

## World Cup 2026 Prospects

As a **host nation**, the USA has automatic qualification and the support of home crowds. Expectations are higher than ever -- many believe this team can reach the quarter-finals or beyond.

**Key Question**: Can the USMNT handle the pressure of playing at home?
