### The Most Underrated Great Manager

Lionel Scaloni has achieved something no other Argentina manager has accomplished: he took a team in absolute crisis and transformed it into the most successful national team on the planet. With **three consecutive major trophies** (Copa America 2021, World Cup 2022, Copa America 2024), he has built a dynasty.

If Argentina wins the 2026 World Cup, Scaloni would become **the first manager in football history to win five consecutive major international trophies**.

---

### Playing Career

Scaloni was a versatile, hardworking player:

- **Newell's Old Boys** (youth) -- Began his football education in Argentina
- **Estudiantes** (1996-1997) -- Early professional appearances
- **Deportivo La Coruna** (1997-2006) -- Spent 9 years in Spain; won La Liga (2000) and Copa del Rey (2002)
- **West Ham United** (loan, 2006) -- Brief spell in England
- **Racing Santander** (2006-2007)
- **Lazio** (2007-2008) -- Finished his career in Serie A
- **Mallorca** (2008-2009)
- **Atalanta** (loan, 2014-2015) -- Final playing stint
- **Argentina National Team** -- Earned 7 caps

As a player, Scaloni was known for his work rate, versatility (he played right-back, midfield, and even winger), and tactical awareness. He was never a star but was respected as a dependable professional.

### Tactical Approach

#### Philosophy: Flexibility and Intelligence

Scaloni's approach is often compared to Zidane's and Ancelotti's: he **studies opponents meticulously and adjusts his tactics and team selection accordingly**. There is no rigid Scaloni system -- there is a Scaloni method.

Key tactical principles:

1. **Opponent-specific preparation**: During Russia 2018, Scaloni's role on the coaching staff was to observe future opponents. He was particularly struck by France, which influenced his tactical evolution.

2. **Formation fluidity**: Scaloni switches between 4-3-3, 4-4-2, and 4-3-1-2 seamlessly. In the 2022 World Cup final alone, he made multiple formation changes during the match to counter France's adjustments.

3. **Pressing with purpose**: Argentina doesn't press for the sake of pressing -- they press in coordinated triggers, forcing opponents into predictable patterns.

4. **Maximizing Messi**: The entire system is designed to give Messi the ball in dangerous areas while surrounding him with runners and workers who create space.

5. **Emotional intelligence**: Scaloni built a "family" culture within the squad. The team's celebration of Messi and their willingness to sacrifice for each other is a direct result of Scaloni's man-management.

#### The Scaloni Method

- Watch every opponent match multiple times
- Design a specific game plan for each opponent
- Select players based on form and tactical fit, not reputation
- Foster unity through shared experiences (the squad famously spent weeks together before the 2022 World Cup)
- Trust the process and the players

### The 2026 Challenge

Scaloni faces a unique challenge: motivating a team that has already won everything. The hunger to prove themselves is gone -- replaced by the desire to **cement a legacy as the greatest dynasty in international football history**. If he can guide Argentina to another World Cup, he will have achieved something no manager in the history of the sport has ever done.
