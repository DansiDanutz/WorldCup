### Skills and Strengths

Lionel Messi is widely considered the greatest footballer of all time. Even at 38, his vision, passing, and set-piece delivery remain without peer.

**Key strengths:**
- **Vision**: Sees the pitch like nobody else; his passing range is unparalleled in football history
- **Dribbling**: Low center of gravity, ball glued to his foot; can beat multiple defenders in a phone booth
- **Free kicks**: The greatest free-kick taker in history; goalkeepers barely move
- **Playmaking**: Has evolved from a goalscorer to the ultimate creator
- **Football IQ**: Reads the game three moves ahead; positional sense is genius-level
- **Calmness under pressure**: Never rushed, never panicked, always finds the right pass
- **Leadership**: Since 2021, has grown into a captain who lifts the entire team

### Funny and Interesting Facts

**1. The 2022 World Cup "WHAT ARE YOU DOING?" Photos**
During the 2022 World Cup final, a photo went viral of Messi appearing to ask teammate Lisandro Martinez "QUE HACES?" (What are you doing?) after a defensive mix-up. The image became the most shared sports meme of 2022, spawning thousands of variations. Messi reportedly found it hilarious and even used the phrase as a joke in the dressing room afterward.

**2. The Double Chocolate Milanesa Ritual**
Messi is famously obsessed with milanesa (breaded cutlet) and chocolate. Before every big match, his pre-game meal is reportedly a double chocolate milanesa -- a dish his mother Celia makes. During the 2022 World Cup, the Argentine chef at the team hotel had to learn Celia's exact recipe because Messi refused to eat any other version. When Argentina won the World Cup, his first request was reportedly: "Now I can finally have my mam's milanesa in peace."
