# Argentina vs Algeria — Group J, Matchday 1

**Date:** June 12, 2026 | **Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

Argentina and Algeria have **never met** in an official FIFA match. This is their first-ever encounter at any level, making it a complete unknown — and a fascinating storyline for the opening group match.

---

## The Storyline: Ghosts of Gijon

Algeria's World Cup identity was forged in 1982, when they shocked West Germany 2-1 only to be eliminated by the "Disgrace of Gijon" — a brazenly fixed 1-0 result between West Germany and Austria that sent both European teams through. For 44 years, Algerian football has carried the wound of that injustice.

Now, in 2026, the Desert Foxes face a different giant — Lionel Messi's Argentina, the defending world champions. The irony is thick: Algeria must now prove themselves against the very caliber of team that once conspired against them. Riyad Mahrez, in his farewell World Cup, has spoken about wanting to "right the historical wrongs" of Algerian football.

Meanwhile, Argentina opens their title defense against a team with nothing to lose. Scaloni will warn his players: Algeria nearly knocked out eventual champions Germany in 2014 (losing 2-1 AET). Underestimate them at your peril.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Lionel Messi vs Ismail Bennacer
Messi will drift into the spaces Bennacer is supposed to patrol. Bennacer's intelligence and positioning will be tested to the absolute limit. If Bennacer can disrupt Messi's rhythm, Algeria has a chance.

### Julian Alvarez vs Aissa Mandi
Alvarez's relentless pressing against Mandi's composure on the ball. If Alvarez forces errors in Algeria's defensive third, the floodgates could open.

### Riyad Mahrez vs Marcos Acuna
Mahrez's trickery and experience against Acuna's aggressive defending. This is where Algeria's best hope of creating chances lies — Mahrez cutting inside from the right.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Argentina (4-3-3):** Scaloni's side will dominate possession, probing for openings through Messi's vision and Mac Allister's runs from deep. Emiliano Martinez provides the psychological edge. The midfield trio of Enzo Fernandez, Mac Allister, and De Paul will look to suffocate Algeria's transitions.

**Algeria (4-3-3):** Petkovic will set up to absorb pressure and strike on the counter. Mahrez, Gouiri, and Boudaoui have the pace to punish any Argentine complacency. Algeria will look to make the game ugly — physical, disruptive, and tense.

---

## X-Factor

**The Opening Match Nerves.** Defending champions often struggle in their opening game — France drew with Uruguay in 2002, Spain lost to Switzerland in 2010. If Algeria scores first, the ghost of Gijon transforms from a wound into a battle cry.
