- **Date of Birth:** April 10, 1992
- **Age:** 34
- **Position:** Striker / Left Winger / Forward
- **Current Club:** Al Nassr FC (Saudi Arabia)
- **Nationality:** Senegalese
- **Height:** 175 cm (5'9")
- **Preferred Foot:** Right

## Skills and Strengths

- **Explosive Pace:** Even at 34, Mane retains devastating speed that terrifies defenders
- **Dribbling Mastery:** Can beat defenders with close control, body feints, and sudden changes of direction
- **Clinical Finishing:** Has evolved from a wide forward into a lethal goalscorer, capable of finishing with either foot
- **Work Rate:** Relentless pressing from the front; track record of chasing lost causes
- **Big-Game Mentality:** Scores in finals and crucial moments -- the 2022 AFCON final penalty being the prime example
- **Leadership:** The spiritual leader of the Senegal team, even when not wearing the armband
- **Versatility:** Can play as a left winger, second striker, or central forward
- **Humility:** Despite his achievements, remains grounded and beloved by teammates and fans

## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. The Village Hero Who Built a Hospital

Mane was born in Bambali, a tiny village in the Casamance region of southern Senegal. His family was poor, and his father forbade him from playing football, wanting him to focus on education. When his father died when Mane was seven, he was raised by his uncle. Despite the hardship, Mane pursued football relentlessly, eventually leaving his village at 15 to try out for teams in Dakar. After achieving footballing success and immense wealth, Mane has poured millions of his own money back into Bambali. He built a hospital, a school, a mosque, and a gas station for the village. He provides monthly stipends to families in need and has funded clean water projects. In 2022, he was awarded the inaugural Socrates Award at the Ballon d'Or ceremony for his humanitarian work. When asked why he spends so much on charity, Mane famously said: "I do not need to have luxury cars or big castles. I prefer that my people have a little bit of what life has given me."

### 2. The Phone in the Ball and the Stranded Genius

As a teenager, Mane traveled 400 kilometers from his village to Dakar to attend a football trial, carrying nothing but a pair of boots and his dreams. He arrived at the ASC Generation Foot academy, a well-known production line for Senegalese talent, and immediately stood out. Legend has it that during his first training session, the coaches were so impressed by his raw talent that they thought he was a professional player who had come for a visit. When they learned he was a trialist, they signed him immediately. Before his career took off, Mane used to call his family from a shared phone, and he was once so poor that he could not afford a proper football and would practice with rolled-up socks or bundled cloth. One of his coaches at the academy later recalled that the only thing Mane wanted to do was play football -- he had no interest in anything else.
