- **Pace and Movement:** Quick across the ground with intelligent off-the-ball movement that creates chances

- **Physicality:** Strong enough to hold off defenders and compete in the Premier League
- **Pressing:** High-energy pressing from the front fits both club and international systems
- **Link-Up Play:** Good at combining with wingers and midfielders in the final third
- **Versatility:** Can play as a lone striker or in a front two

## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. The Gambian-Senegalese Identity

Jackson was born in Banjul, the capital of Gambia, but moved to Senegal as a child and considers himself Senegalese. He was eligible to play for both Gambia and Senegal, and there was reportedly interest from the Gambian Football Federation to call him up. However, Jackson chose Senegal, the country where he grew up and developed as a footballer. His story reflects the complex national identities that exist in West Africa, where borders are often porous and families have roots on both sides. He has spoken about how proud he is to represent Senegal and how the team has welcomed him as one of their own.

### 2. The Rapid Rise from Portugal to Chelsea

Jackson's path to the Premier League was unconventional. He started his professional career in the Portuguese lower leagues with Casa Pia, a small club in Lisbon. From there, he moved to Villarreal in Spain, where he initially struggled and was loaned out to Almeria in the Spanish second division without much success. But his breakthrough came when he returned to Villarreal and hit a rich vein of form in the second half of the 2022-23 season, scoring goals regularly in La Liga. Chelsea signed him in the summer of 2023 for a reported 35 million euros, and despite some inconsistency, he has established himself as a Premier League striker. His journey from the Portuguese third tier to Chelsea in just four years is a testament to his determination and talent.
