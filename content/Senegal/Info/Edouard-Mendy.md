# Edouard Mendy - Player Profile

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Edouard Osoque Mendy
- **Nickname(s):** "The Safe Hands," "Edouard the Great"
- **Date of Birth:** March 1, 1992
- **Age:** 33
- **Position:** Goalkeeper
- **Current Club:** Al Ahli FC (Saudi Arabia)
- **Nationality:** Senegalese (Guinean-Bissau heritage)
- **Height:** 197 cm (6'6")
- **Preferred Foot:** Right
- **Role:** Starting goalkeeper for Senegal

---

## Skills and Strengths

- **Shot Stopping:** Exceptional reflexes and ability to make point-blank saves; his shot-stopping won Chelsea the Champions League
- **Aerial Command:** At 6'6", he dominates his penalty area, claiming crosses with authority
- **Distribution:** Improved significantly with the ball at his feet; comfortable playing out from the back
- **One-on-One Ability:** Remarkable at closing angles and making saves in breakaway situations
- **Composure:** Rarely flustered, even in high-pressure moments; his calm demeanor spreads through the defense
- **Big-Game Experience:** Champions League winner, AFCON winner, Club World Cup winner
- **Penalty Saving:** Has a strong record in penalty shootouts, including the 2022 AFCON final

## Interesting Facts and Stories

### 1. From Unemployment to Champions League Winner in Four Years

Mendy's career trajectory is one of the most remarkable in modern football. In 2014, after being released by Marseille's reserve team, he was unemployed and seriously considered quitting football altogether. He was living on unemployment benefits in France, training alone in local parks, and wondering if his dream was over. His agent managed to arrange a trial at Stade de Reims in Ligue 2, where he eventually earned a contract. From there, his rise was meteoric: Reims to Rennes (where he helped them qualify for the Champions League), then to Chelsea in 2020 for a reported 22 million pounds. Within a year, he had won the Champions League. In 2021, he was named the Best FIFA Goalkeeper. The story of a man who was collecting unemployment benefits in 2014 holding the Champions League trophy in 2021 is one of sport's greatest comeback stories.

### 2. The AFCON Final Penalty Heroics

Mendy's most iconic moment for Senegal came in the 2022 AFCON final against Egypt. After 120 minutes of tense, goalless football, the match went to penalties. Mendy saved Mohanad Lasheen's penalty in the shootout, diving to his left to push the ball away. When Sadio Mane stepped up to take the decisive penalty (after the fourth round), he initially missed but was given a retake due to encroachment. Mane scored the retake, and Senegal won their first-ever AFCON title. Mendy's save was the turning point. He later revealed that he had studied every Egyptian penalty taker extensively on video before the match, and his preparation paid off. The image of Mendy celebrating with his teammates after the final whistle, tears streaming down his face, became one of the most iconic moments in African football history.
