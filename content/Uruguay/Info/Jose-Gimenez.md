## Skills and Strengths

### Aggressive Defending
Gimenez embodies the Atletico Madrid defensive philosophy instilled by Diego Simeone. He is fearless in the tackle, relishes physical confrontations, and never shirks a challenge. His aggression sets the tone for Uruguay's defense.

### Aerial Prowess
With 8 goals in 99 international appearances -- an excellent return for a center-back -- Gimenez is a genuine threat on set pieces. His timing in the air and willingness to attack the ball make him dangerous in both penalty areas.

### Tactical Intelligence
Having spent over a decade at Atletico Madrid under one of football's most tactically disciplined coaches, Gimenez reads the game at an elite level. He organizes the backline, anticipates danger, and positions himself to intercept.

### Leadership
As captain of Uruguay, Gimenez is the emotional heartbeat of the team. His passionate celebrations, fierce commitment, and willingness to play through pain set the standard for everyone around him.

### Passing Range
While primarily known for his defending, Gimenez has developed a reliable passing game. He can play long diagonal balls and threaded passes through midfield, adding an additional dimension to Uruguay's build-up play.

## Defining Moments

### Atletico Madrid Debut (2013)
Gimenez signed for Atletico Madrid as an 18-year-old unknown from Danubio. He made his debut in September 2013 and within months was part of the squad that won La Liga, breaking the Barcelona-Real Madrid duopoly.

### The Godin Succession
Gimenez was long viewed as the successor to Diego Godin, Uruguay's legendary center-back. When Godin left Atletico Madrid in 2019, Gimenez seamlessly assumed the mantle, becoming the defensive leader at both club and international level.

### 2022 World Cup Group Stage
Despite Uruguay's disappointing group stage exit at the 2022 World Cup, Gimenez's individual performances were commendable. He scored a dramatic late goal against Ghana, though it was not enough to advance.

### Reaching 99 Caps
Heading into the 2026 World Cup with 99 caps, Gimenez is on the verge of joining the exclusive century club for Uruguay. His longevity at the highest level -- over a decade at Atletico Madrid -- is a testament to his professionalism and resilience.

## Interesting Facts

### 1. The One-Club Man (Almost)
Gimenez has spent his entire European career at Atletico Madrid, joining as a teenager in 2013 and remaining for over 12 years. In modern football, where players change clubs every few years, this loyalty is remarkable. He has reportedly turned down offers from Manchester United, Inter Milan, and Tottenham to stay in Madrid. He and his family are so embedded in the city that his children were born in Madrid and consider themselves both Uruguayan and Spanish.

### 2. The Emotional Warrior
Gimenez is famous for his tearful pre-match interviews and emotional reactions during national anthem ceremonies. He openly wept during the national anthem before Uruguay's 2018 World Cup quarterfinal against France, a moment that went viral and endeared him to fans worldwide. He once revealed in an interview that he cries before every major international match because "wearing this shirt means everything to me and my family." His grandmother, who raised him in Toledo, Uruguay, reportedly watches every match wrapped in the Uruguayan flag.
