# Uruguay vs Saudi Arabia — Group H, Matchday 1

**Date:** June 12, 2026
**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026, Group Stage
**Venue:** United States

---

## The Press and the Miracle Workers

Uruguay open their World Cup against the team that produced the greatest upset of 2022 — Saudi Arabia's 2-1 victory over Argentina. But Bielsa's Uruguay are not Argentina. They don't coast. They don't relax. They press for 90 minutes with terrifying intensity. Saudi Arabia's miracle-workers face a completely different challenge.

No recorded senior international meetings between Uruguay and Saudi Arabia.

## Storyline: The Argentina Lesson

Saudi Arabia's 2022 win over Argentina came with a crucial lesson that everyone remembers: they pressed high, scored twice, and then held on for dear life. It was brave, brilliant, and ultimately meaningless — they lost their next two matches and went home.

Against Uruguay, the same approach would be suicidal. Bielsa's team presses harder and recovers faster than Argentina. They don't have Messi walking around the pitch. They have Valverde covering every blade of grass and Ugarte winning every tackle. If Saudi Arabia press Uruguay high, they'll be shredded on the counter.

Coach Donis must find a different approach. Sit deeper, absorb pressure, and rely on Al-Dawsari's individual brilliance for a moment of magic. It's pragmatic but it's the only realistic path to a result.

## X-Factor

**Darwin Nunez's unpredictability.** The striker is chaos personified. He can miss sitters and score worldies in the same five-minute stretch. Saudi Arabia can defend perfectly for 89 minutes, but Nunez only needs one chance — one moment of chaos — to decide the match.
