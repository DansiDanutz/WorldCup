# Czech Republic vs South Korea — Group A, Matchday 3

**June 21, 2026** | Estadio BBVA, Monterrey | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**Very limited history** — no competitive meetings and only a handful of friendlies. This first competitive clash is uncharted territory for both nations.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Patrik Schick vs Kim Min-jae
The Bundesliga sharpshooter against Bayern Munich's "Monster." They've faced each other in German football — Schick knows Kim's strengths and weaknesses intimately. But knowing Kim's game and beating him are different challenges entirely.

### Tomas Soucek vs Hwang In-beom
West Ham's tireless engine against Feyenoord's midfield conductor. Soucek covers more ground than anyone; Hwang sees passes nobody else sees. Physical dominance against technical excellence.

### Adam Hlozek vs Son Heung-min
The rising star against the established legend. Hlozek (23) is the future of Czech football. Son (33) is South Korea's greatest-ever player. Both will want to leave their mark on this match.

---

## Storyline: Legacy on the Line

For South Korea, a win could mean a fourth consecutive Round of 16 appearance. For the Czechs, it could mean their first knockout-stage win as an independent nation. Son Heung-min has hinted this is his final World Cup — every touch could be his last on football's grandest stage. Patrik Schick, meanwhile, is in his prime at 29, carrying the hopes of a football-mad nation that hasn't tasted World Cup glory since Czechoslovakia's 1962 final. Two teams, two different kinds of desperation. The match has the ingredients of a classic.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Czech Republic (4-2-3-1)** — Structured, patient, lethal from set pieces. Koubek's side won't be rushed. Schick will wait for his moment.

**South Korea (4-2-3-1)** — More dynamic, with Son's movement and Lee Kang-in's creativity. But they can be vulnerable at set pieces — and the Czechs are set-piece specialists.

---

## X-Factor: Son Heung-min's Farewell

If this is indeed Son's final World Cup match, emotion will drive him to extraordinary heights. The man who scored the goal that knocked out Germany in 2018 and beat Portugal in 2022 has a habit of producing when it matters most. The Czechs must contain not just Son's talent but his will.
