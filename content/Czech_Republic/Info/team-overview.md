## Team History

### The Czech Football Tradition

The Czech Republic carries the rich football heritage of Czechoslovakia, which finished World Cup runners-up in 1934 and 1962. Since the peaceful split with Slovakia in 1993, the Czech Republic has established its own identity as a technically gifted European side.

### World Cup Record

- **Best result**: Runners-up as Czechoslovakia (1934, 1962); Round of 16 as Czech Republic (2006, 2026)
- **Appearances** (as Czech Republic): 3 (2006, 2026, plus Czechoslovakia's 8)
- **Titles**: 0
- **European Championship**: Runners-up (1996), Semifinalists (2004)

## Playing Style

Under veteran coach **Miroslav Koubek** (age 74), the Czechs play disciplined European football:

- **Technical quality**: Strong ball retention and intelligent passing
- **Set-piece threat**: Excellent from corners and free kicks (Schick, Soucek)
- **Defensive organization**: Compact 4-2-3-1, hard to break down
- **Counter-attacking**: Quick transitions using Hlozek and Sulc
- **Experience**: Core of Premier League and Bundesliga players

## 2026 Outlook

The Czechs qualified through the UEFA playoffs and arrive as dangerous underdogs. With no traditional powerhouse in Group A (Mexico, South Korea, South Africa), advancing from the group is realistic. A Round of 32 berth is the target, with dreams of a first knockout-stage win as an independent nation.
