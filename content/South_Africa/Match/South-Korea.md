## Key Player Matchups

### Son Heung-min vs Mothobi Mvala
The Premier League Golden Boot winner against South Africa's defensive anchor. Son's ability to cut inside and unleash his right foot is legendary — Mvala must ensure Son never gets that half-yard of space.

### Evidence Makgopa vs Kim Min-jae
The Orlando Pirates striker against Bayern Munich's "Monster." Makgopa's raw pace against Kim's physical dominance. It's a classic speed-vs-strength battle.

### Percy Tau vs Hwang In-beom
Two creative playmakers who will try to unlock each other's defenses. Tau (Al-Ahly) has continental experience across Africa. Hwang (Feyenoord) has European pedigree. Whichever #10 has the better day wins.

---

## Storyline: Battle of the Underdogs

Both South Africa and South Korea enter this match knowing it's likely their best chance at three points in Group A. The loser is probably going home. The winner keeps hope alive for the Round of 32. Son Heung-min carries an entire nation on his shoulders — 50 million South Koreans will stop everything to watch. But Bafana Bafana carry an entire continent's hopes. Africa vs Asia, two underdogs scrapping for survival. This is what the World Cup is all about.

---

## Tactical Preview

**South Africa (4-4-2)** — Broos will set up deep and compact, forcing South Korea to break them down. Foster and Makgopa offer genuine counter-attacking threat.

**South Korea (4-2-3-1)** — Hong Myung-bo's side will dominate possession. Lee Kang-in pulls strings, Hwang Hee-chan provides the bulldog energy, and Son is the finisher.

---

## X-Factor: Lyle Foster's Physicality

The Burnley striker has been battle-tested in the Premier League. If he can unsettle Kim Min-jae physically and create chaos in the box, South Africa has a real chance. Foster's hold-up play could be the key to springing counters.
