## Team History

### Bafana Bafana

South Africa, affectionately known as "Bafana Bafana" (The Boys), carries the pride of an entire continent. As 2010 World Cup hosts, South Africa became the first African nation to stage football's greatest tournament.

### World Cup Record

- **Best result**: Group Stage (1998, 2002, 2010, 2026)
- **Appearances**: 4 (including 2026)
- **Titles**: 0
- **Africa Cup of Nations**: 1 (1996)

## Playing Style

Under Belgian coach **Hugo Broos**, South Africa plays organized, counter-attacking football:

- **Defensive discipline**: Compact 4-5-1, difficult to break down
- **Goalkeeping excellence**: Ronwen Williams is one of Africa's best
- **Pace on the break**: Quick transitions using Makgopa and Mokoena
- **Physical presence**: Strong and athletic across the pitch
- **Set-piece threat**: Dangerous from free kicks and corners

---

## 2026 Outlook

South Africa returns to the World Cup after a 16-year absence. Group A is challenging but not impossible — a point against Mexico in the opener could set up a crucial match against South Korea or Czech Republic. Advancing from the group would be a historic achievement for Bafana Bafana.
