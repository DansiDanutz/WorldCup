## Career History

### Queen's Park (2013-2015)
- Began his senior career at Queen's Park in the Scottish lower divisions
- Scored regularly at the amateur and semi-professional level
- Made 30 appearances, scoring 13 goals

### Aberdeen (2015-2017)
- Earned a move to Aberdeen in the Scottish Premiership
- Struggled to establish himself in the first team
- Made only a handful of appearances and was sent out on loan

### Loan Spells and Lower Leagues
- Loaned to Dunfermline Athletic and St Mirren, where he found his scoring touch
- At St Mirren, he scored crucial goals that helped the club win the Scottish Championship
- Despite the goals, Aberdeen released him in 2017

### Ayr United (2017-2019)
- Dropped to Scottish League One to join Ayr United
- This is where Shankland's career truly ignited
- Scored an extraordinary **34 goals in 41 appearances** in the 2018-19 season
- Won the **PFA Scotland League One Player of the Year**
- His prolific scoring attracted attention from clubs at every level in Scotland

### Dundee United (2019-2022)
- Signed for Dundee United, who had just been promoted to the Scottish Premiership
- Continued his prolific form at the top level, scoring 28 goals in 52 appearances
- His goals helped establish Dundee United in the Premiership
- Earned his first Scotland call-up based on his club form

### Beerschot (2022-2023)
- Moved to Belgian club Beerschot for a new challenge
- Struggled to adapt to Belgian football and the move did not work out
- Returned to Scotland after one season

### Heart of Midlothian (2023 -- Present)
- Signed for Hearts, the club he supported as a boy
- The move revitalized his career
- Scored regularly in the Scottish Premiership, re-establishing himself as one of the most natural finishers in Scotland
- His form earned him a recall to the Scotland squad
- Selected for the 2026 World Cup squad as one of Scotland's primary striking options

---

## Playing Style and Strengths

### Natural Finishing
- Shankland's primary attribute is his finishing -- he is a pure goalscorer
- Clinical in front of goal with both feet and his head
- Has a striker's instinct for being in the right place at the right time
- His goal-to-minute ratio at every club has been impressive

### Movement and Positioning
- Intelligent movement in and around the penalty area
- Anticipates where the ball will arrive better than most
- Makes well-timed runs that lose markers
- His spatial awareness allows him to find pockets of space in congested defenses

### Work Rate
- Despite being primarily a finisher, Shankland works hard for the team
- Presses from the front and contributes to the defensive effort
- Holds the ball up effectively to bring others into play
- Willing to drop deep to link play when required

### Mental Resilience
- Overcame rejection at Aberdeen to rebuild his career in the lower leagues
- His journey from Scottish League One to the World Cup is a testament to perseverance
- Has never lost confidence in his ability to score goals at any level
- Steve Clarke has praised his "unshakeable belief" in himself

## Interesting Facts

1. **From League One to the World Cup** -- Shankland's journey from Scottish League One to the World Cup is one of the most remarkable in Scottish football history. In 2018, he was playing in front of crowds of 2,000 at Ayr United. Eight years later, he is preparing to face Brazil at the World Cup. His 34-goal season at Ayr remains one of the most prolific in modern Scottish lower-league history.

2. **The Hearts-Loving Goalscorer** -- Shankland is a lifelong Hearts fan who grew up watching the club from the stands at Tynecastle. When he signed for Hearts in 2023, he described it as his "dream move." In his first Edinburgh derby against Hibernian, he scored twice and celebrated with such passion that he was booked for removing his shirt -- revealing a t-shirt that read "FTM" (an affectionate Hearts phrase). The booking was worth every penny, according to Shankland, who called it "the best booking of my life."
