## Career History

### Queen's Park (2012-2013)
- Began his career in the Scottish fourth tier with amateur club Queen's Park
- Made 40 appearances and caught the eye of professional scouts
- Was not offered a contract by any senior club as a youth player, playing amateur football while at university

### Dundee United (2013-2014)
- Signed professionally for the first time at age 19
- Won the **SPFL Young Player of the Month** for September 2013
- Made 38 appearances, scoring 2 goals
- His rapid rise earned him a move to England

### Hull City (2014-2017)
- Signed for newly-promoted Hull City in the Premier League
- Experienced relegation in his first season but stayed loyal
- Helped Hull earn promotion back to the Premier League via the playoffs in 2016
- Made 116 appearances across three seasons
- Attracted attention from Liverpool with his consistent performances

### Liverpool (2017 -- Present)
- Signed for an initial fee of around GBP 8 million in July 2017
- Initially a backup to Alberto Moreno, he seized his opportunity and never looked back
- Under Jurgen Klopp, Robertson became one of the best left-backs in world football

#### Trophies at Liverpool
- **Champions League** (2019) -- Started in the final against Tottenham
- **Premier League** (2020) -- Ended Liverpool's 30-year title drought
- **FIFA Club World Cup** (2019)
- **UEFA Super Cup** (2019)
- **FA Cup** (2022)
- **League Cup** (2022, 2024)
- **Community Shield** (2022)

## Playing Style and Strengths

### Attacking Output
- One of the most creative full-backs in Premier League history
- Holds the record for most Premier League assists by a defender
- Exceptional crossing accuracy from wide and deep positions
- Makes relentless overlapping runs that stretch opposition defenses
- Combines brilliantly with left-sided attackers

### Defensive Qualities
- Tenacious in one-on-one defending
- Excellent recovery pace to cover behind the defensive line
- Strong positional awareness, rarely caught out of position
- Willing to put his body on the line -- has played through injuries consistently

### Leadership
- Named Scotland captain in 2018, becoming one of the youngest captains in recent history
- Leads by example with his work rate and commitment
- Vocal organizer on the pitch, constantly communicating with teammates
- Has worn the armband through Scotland's return to major tournaments

### Stamina and Durability
- Famously never seems to tire, covering enormous distances every match
- Klopp once said Robertson could "play every minute of every game"
- His fitness levels are among the best in the Premier League
- Rarely injured despite a demanding schedule across multiple competitions

## Interesting Facts

1. **Amateur to Anfield in Five Years** -- Robertson was playing amateur football for Queen's Park while studying at the University of Glasgow. Five years later, he was starting in a Champions League final. He is often cited as one of football's greatest late-blooming success stories. He was released by Celtic's youth academy at age 15, told he was too small to make it.

2. **Record-Breaking Assists** -- Robertson became the defender with the most Premier League assists in history, surpassing the previous record held by Leighton Baines. His partnership with Trent Alexander-Arnold at Liverpool created arguably the most productive full-back pairing the Premier League has ever seen, with both players regularly notching double-digit assist seasons.
