## Career History

### Manchester United Youth Academy (2002-2017)
- Joined Manchester United's academy at age 5
- Progressed through every age group at the club
- Was on the brink of being released multiple times during his teens due to growth-related injuries
- José Mourinho handed him his first-team debut in May 2017 against Arsenal
- Mourinho later described him as "my perfect player" -- a young professional who gave everything

### Manchester United (2017-2024)
- Made 250+ appearances for the club over seven seasons
- Won the **Europa League** (2017), though he did not feature in the final
- Became a reliable squad player under Mourinho, Solskjaer, Rangnick, and Ten Hag
- Developed a reputation for big-game performances -- scoring important goals against Leeds, Manchester City, and Liverpool
- Often played out of position (center-back, defensive midfield) due to his versatility
- Won the **FA Cup** (2024) in his final season at the club
- Departed in summer 2024 after the club did not offer him the contract extension he sought

### Napoli (2024 -- Present)
- Signed for Napoli in August 2024 for approximately EUR 30 million
- Under Antonio Conte, McTominay transformed into one of the best midfielders in Serie A
- Won the **2024-25 Serie A title** with Napoli, playing a pivotal role
- Scored 12 goals in Serie A, extraordinary for a central midfielder
- Named **Serie A Player of the Year** for the 2024-25 season -- the first Scottish player to win a major league Player of the Year award in decades
- His bicycle kick goal against Denmark in World Cup qualifying became one of the iconic moments of Scotland's campaign

## Playing Style and Strengths

### Physical Dominance
- Standing 6'4" (193 cm), McTominay is an imposing physical presence in midfield
- Wins an exceptional percentage of aerial duels
- Uses his body to shield the ball effectively under pressure
- His long strides allow him to cover ground quickly

### Goal Threat from Midfield
- Has developed into a genuine goal threat from central midfield
- His 15 international goals from 70 caps is a remarkable return for a midfielder
- Deadly from late runs into the box -- times his arrivals perfectly
- Capable of spectacular goals (bicycle kick vs Denmark, long-range strikes)
- At Napoli, Conte built tactical setups specifically to exploit McTominay's goal-scoring runs

### Versatility
- Has played defensive midfield, box-to-box, centre-back, and even emergency striker
- His willingness to play wherever needed has sometimes masked his best position
- At Napoli, settled into his ideal role as a roaming box-to-box midfielder

### Mental Resilience
- Overcame multiple career setbacks at Manchester United (squad rotation, criticism, injuries)
- Responded to every challenge with improved performances
- His move to Italy and subsequent success demonstrated remarkable adaptability
- Thrives under pressure -- some of his best performances come in the biggest matches

## Interesting Facts

1. **Serie A Player of the Year from Scotland** -- McTominay became the first Scottish player to win a top-five European league Player of the Year award since Kenny Dalglish's era. His transformation from a squad player at Manchester United to the best midfielder in Italian football within a single season is one of the most remarkable career resurrections in recent memory. Italian fans gave him the nickname "McTominator."

2. **The Bicycle Kick That Sent Scotland to the World Cup** -- His stunning bicycle kick against Denmark in World Cup qualifying became an instant iconic moment in Scottish football history. The goal, which sealed a vital victory, was compared to Wayne Rooney's famous bicycle kick against Manchester City and was watched millions of times on social media. McTominay later admitted he had never attempted a bicycle kick in a match before.
