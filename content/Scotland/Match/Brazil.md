# Scotland vs Brazil — Group C, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

| Matches | Scotland Wins | Draws | Brazil Wins |
|---------|---------------|-------|-------------|
| 3 | 0 | 1 | 2 |

Three World Cup meetings, no Scotland victories. But the history is rich: a **0-0 draw in 1974**, a memorable **4-1 Brazil win in 1982** (Zico, Socrates, Falcao at their peak), and a **2-1 Brazil win in 1998**. Scotland has scored against Brazil at every World Cup meeting.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Andy Robertson vs Vinicius Junior
Liverpool's captain against Real Madrid's superstar. Robertson's tenacity and leadership against Vini's electrifying dribbling. They've never met at international level — this is a fresh chapter in a storied Scotland-Brazil rivalry.

### Scott McTominay vs Bruno Guimaraes
Napoli's midfield powerhouse against Newcastle's general. Two Premier League rivals meeting on the world stage. McTominay's surprising goal threat (15 international goals) adds danger to Scotland's midfield.

### John McGinn vs Neymar
Aston Villa's talisman against Santos' king. McGinn's 20 international goals from midfield prove he can produce moments of magic. Neymar at 34 is still Neymar — one flick, one shot, one moment of genius can change everything.

---

## Storyline: The Tartan Army's Impossible Dream

Scotland has played Brazil three times at World Cups and never won. But they've never been embarrassed either — that 0-0 in 1974 was a defensive masterclass, and the 1998 match was competitive until the end. Steve Clarke's Scotland is the most talented generation in decades: Robertson (Liverpool), McTominay (Napoli), McGinn (Aston Villa), Tierney (Celtic). These are Champions League players. Brazil is Brazil — but even Brazil can have off days. The Tartan Army will fill SoFi Stadium with noise and belief. After eight World Cups of group-stage heartbreak, could this finally be Scotland's moment?

---

## Tactical Preview

**Scotland (5-4-1)** — Clarke matches Brazil's width with a back five. Robertson and Tierney as wing-backs, McTominay and McGinn providing goals from midfield, Shankland leading the line. Disciplined, physical, brave.

**Brazil (4-3-3)** — Ancelotti's side will dominate possession. Even with rotation, the quality is overwhelming. Scotland must be perfect defensively and clinical with any chances.

---

## X-Factor: Lawrence Shankland's Striker's Instinct

The Hearts forward has 7 goals in 20 Scotland appearances — not prolific, but he scores important goals. In a match where Scotland might get only one or two chances, Shankland's ability to sniff out an opportunity could be the difference between history and heartbreak.
