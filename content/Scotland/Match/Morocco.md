# Scotland vs Morocco — Group C, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**Very limited history.** These nations have barely met in football. This first competitive encounter is a blank slate — and that makes it dangerous for both sides.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Andy Robertson vs Achraf Hakimi
Liverpool's captain against PSG's right-back. Two of the best in their positions worldwide. Robertson's leadership and crossing against Hakimi's pace and attacking quality. This flank battle could decide the match.

### Scott McTominay vs Sofyan Amrabat
Napoli's midfield powerhouse against Fenerbahce's tireless engine. McTominay's surprising goal threat against Amrabat's relentless pressing. Both players define their teams' midfield identity.

### Kieran Tierney vs Youssef En-Nesyri
Celtic's versatile defender against Fenerbahce's aerial specialist. Tierney's commitment against En-Nesyri's heading ability. Set pieces could be where this match is won or lost.

---

## Storyline: The Battle for Survival

By Matchday 3, this match could determine which team advances. Scotland has never escaped the group stage in eight attempts. Morocco reached the semifinals in 2022 and carries the weight of expectation. But World Cup pressure is a great equalizer. Steve Clarke's Scotland plays with Scottish fire — passion, physicality, and a refusal to accept defeat. Walid Regragui's Morocco plays with African pride — discipline, athleticism, and belief. When the Tartan Army's bagpipes meet the Atlas Lions' roar, something extraordinary will happen.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Scotland (5-4-1 or 4-4-2)** — Clarke's organized system adapts to the situation. Robertson and Tierney provide width, McTominay and McGinn add goals, Shankland finishes.

**Morocco (4-2-3-1)** — Proven, disciplined, dangerous. Regragui's system has been tested against Spain, Portugal, and France. Hakimi attacks, Ziyech creates, En-Nesyri finishes.

---

## X-Factor: John McGinn's 20 Goals

The Aston Villa midfielder's goalscoring record from midfield is extraordinary. With 20 international goals, he's Scotland's most dangerous weapon. Against Morocco's defense, his late arrivals in the box could produce the goal that makes Scottish football history.
