# Scotland vs Haiti — Group C, Matchday 3

**June 22, 2026** | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | FIFA World Cup 2026

---

## Head-to-Head History

**No previous meetings.** First-ever encounter between Scotland and Haiti. Two proud footballing nations with no shared history — until now.

---

## Key Player Matchups

### Lawrence Shankland vs Jean-Kevin Duverne
Hearts' striker against Nantes' centre-back. Shankland's finishing against Duverne's Ligue 1 experience. This is a battle between two players at similar levels — neither has faced the other's style before.

### John McGinn vs Bryan Alceus
Aston Villa's midfield talisman against Le Mans' hard-working midfielder. McGinn's 20 international goals against Alceus' physicality. The quality gap is real, but Alceus won't back down.

### Andy Robertson vs Carlens Arcus
Liverpool's captain against Auxerre's right-back. Robertson's world-class crossing against Arcus's Ligue 1 experience. Robertson should dominate this flank, but Arcus has nothing to lose.

---

## Storyline: Eight World Cups of Heartbreak

Scotland has played in eight World Cups and never advanced past the group stage. Not once. The pain is generational — fathers and grandfathers who made the same lonely journey home. Haiti's story is different but equally emotional: 52 years between World Cups, a nation united by football through hardship. Both teams know this match could be their best chance at a result in Group C. The Tartan Army will sing "Flower of Scotland" with tears in their eyes. Haiti's fans will beat drums and wave flags. Two nations that love football with their whole hearts, both desperate for a moment of joy. This match will be played on emotion, not tactics.

---

## Tactical Preview

**Scotland (5-4-1 or 4-4-2)** — Clarke's disciplined system. Robertson and Tierney provide width, McTominay and McGinn control midfield, Shankland leads the line. They'll be favorites and should dominate possession.

**Haiti (4-4-2)** — Physical, counter-attacking. Nazon and Foster offer pace on the break. Calderón's Argentine influence brings defensive structure. They'll absorb pressure and strike when opportunity arises.

---

## X-Factor: Duckens Nazon's Never-Say-Die Attitude

The KV Kortrijk forward has scored crucial goals for Haiti throughout qualifying. His pace and determination make him dangerous against any defense. In a match where Scotland will push players forward, Nazon's counter-attacking threat is Scotland's nightmare scenario.
