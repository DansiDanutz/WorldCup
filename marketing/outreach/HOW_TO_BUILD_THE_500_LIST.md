# How to build the 500-influencer list (fast + legit)

I won't fabricate 500 names/emails — fake contacts would waste your 5 days and
wreck your domain reputation. Here's the exact way to pull a real, verified list
quickly, plus how to pick the *best* 500.

---

## 1) Pick the right creators (size matters — smaller usually converts better)
- **Micro (10k–100k):** highest engagement + reply rates, cheapest/free to partner. **Prioritize these.**
- **Mid (100k–500k):** good reach, still reachable.
- **Macro (500k+):** low reply odds in 5 days; only if warm intro.

## 2) Niches to target (in priority order)
1. **Fantasy football / bracket** creators (closest intent)
2. **Football tipsters / predictions** (audience already "picks")
3. **Club/national-team fan accounts** (US, Canada, Mexico = host nations; UK, Brazil, Nigeria, India big football audiences)
4. **Football highlights / culture / memes** (huge reach, cheap)
5. **Telegram football channels** (insanely high conversion for this kind of game)

## 3) Where + how to find them (free-first)
- **TikTok / Instagram / YouTube:** search `#fantasyfootball`, `#worldcup2026`, `#footballpredictions`, `#soccertok`; mine hashtag + "Reels/Shorts" results.
- **Telegram (high ROI):** `tgstat.com` and `telemetr.io` — filter by football category + region + size. Channels list contact/admin.
- **X/Twitter:** advanced search for "World Cup prediction" / "fantasy"; open public **Lists** like "football creators".
- **YouTube:** search niche, sort by recent + view count; channel "About" tab often has a business email.
- **Discord:** disboard.org — search "football"/"soccer" servers.

## 4) Tools that pull lists + emails (free tiers exist)
- **Discovery:** Modash (free trial), Heepsy, HypeAuditor, Upfluence — filter by niche, geo, size, engagement; export CSV.
- **Emails:** business email in bio/About first; else Hunter.io, Apollo.io, Snov.io.
- **Verify before sending:** NeverBounce or ZeroBounce (skip anything not "valid").

## 5) "Best 500" scoring rubric (score each 0–2, keep the top 500)
| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Niche fit | off-topic | adjacent | football/fantasy/tipster |
| Engagement rate | <1% | 1–3% | >3% |
| Audience geo | mismatch | mixed | US/CA/MX/UK/BR/NG/IN |
| Size band | 500k+ | 100k–500k | 10k–100k |
| Recent activity | dormant | monthly | posts weekly |
| Reachable | no contact | DM only | public business email |

Sort by total score → that's your priority order. Work top-down.

## 6) Deliverability survival kit (or 500 emails = 500 spam folders)
- **Separate sending domain** (e.g., `worldcup26.team`), never your main domain.
- Configure **SPF, DKIM, DMARC**.
- **Warm up** inboxes (Instantly/Smartlead auto-warmup) ~3–5 days *(tight on your timeline — see reality check)*.
- Send **20–40/day per inbox**, 2–3 inboxes max.
- Verify every address first.

## ⚠️ Reality check for a 5-day window
Cold-emailing 500 from a fresh domain **cannot** be done well in 5 days — warmup alone takes longer, and most will hit spam. Higher-ROI play for *this week*:
- **50 hand-picked DMs** to the best-fit micro creators (DMs need no warmup, get read).
- **20 tailored B2B emails** to sportsbooks/affiliates (these you CAN do by hand, well).
- **5–10 Telegram football channels** (fastest conversion of all).
- Keep building the 500 list in parallel for a **post-launch / next-tournament** email campaign done properly.

Track everything in `outreach_tracker.csv`.
